This is my candidate for the game contest: --------- I M P L O --------- by Luis BolaÃ±os Mures Implo is an original territory game for two players: Black and White. It is played on the intersections of a square board, which is initially empty. The suggested size is 19x19, as in Go, but any others are also possible. There are two kinds of stones of each color: pawns and kings. Draws and cycles are not possible in this game. For a comfortable play on a physical board, you can use stones with one white and one black side. Half the stones show some symbol on the black side, and half the stones show it on the white side. These represent black and white kings, respectively. DEFINITIONS In this rules, "adjacent" always means "orthogonally adjacent". A group is a set of like-colored, adjacent stones. A group can contain pawns, kings or both. A pawn is dead when it belongs to a group with no empty intersections adjacent to it. A dead group is a group with one or more dead pawns. Alpha is the color of the last played stone, which need not be the player's color. Beta is the color which is not alpha. PLAY Starting with Black, players take turns placing one stone of ANY color on an empty intersection. After a placement, if there are no dead pawns, the turn ends. If there are any dead pawns: a) All the beta dead pawns become alpha kings. This is called "flipping". b) All the alpha pawns in the groups adjacent to the beta dead groups are removed from the board. c) If there are any alpha dead groups at this moment, a) and b) are repeated with colors inverted, i.e., all the pawns in the alpha dead groups become beta kings, and all the beta pawns in the groups adjacent to the alpha dead groups are removed. A player can pass his turn at any moment. When both players pass in succession, the game ends. OBJECTIVE At the end of the game, the player with the most stones (pawns and kings) of his color on the board wins. In case of a tie, the player who passed first during the game wins. SAMPLE POSITIONS x -> black stone # -> black king o -> white stone @ -> white king - Position #1: o x . . x o . x . . x . . x . x x . x . . . . . . Black places a black pawn on b4, flipping the two white pawns (which become black kings) and removing five black pawns plus the played pawn: # . . . x # . . . . . . . x . . . . x . . . . . . - Position #2: . . # # . x # o x x . x @ . . . . x . . x # x o x White places a black pawn on d3, flipping the white pawn (which becomes a black king) and removing seven black pawns plus the played pawn: . . # # . . # # . . . . @ . . . . . . . . # . o x Please note that the black pawns on a1 and a4 are also removed. Pawns and kings of the same color, if adjacent, are all part of the same group. The black pawn on e1 is not removed because it does not belong to any group adjacent to the flipped pawn's group. - Position #3: . . o o . o @ # # o . . # # # . o o o # . . . . o White places a white pawn on b3. None of the black stones are flipped, because they are all kings. Thus, as there are no dead black groups, the pawns in the surrounding white groups are not removed: . . o o . o @ # # o . o # # # . o o o # . . . . o COMMENTS Implo is a game similar to Go in that players try to surround enemy groups in order to score points. In Implo, however, the groups which succeed in the battle get corrupted by vanity and die, whereas the defeated (surrounded) enemy groups are not only kept alive by their captors, but converted into invincible soldiers to their service. Also, as players can use stones of both colors, they can take advantage of their own losses by joining the opponent's attacking groups into one. This way, they are forced to finish their charge together and die en masse.

Some people seems to find it a bit difficult to understand the rules of Implo as they are worded now. They are very simple, though, so I must be blamed for not having been able to explain them properly. To make them clearer, it may be useful to summarize the three basic concepts used in this game: a) Captures proceed as in Go, except that surrounded groups are not removed, but flipped to show the opposing color. (Also, still as in Go, dead stones of the opposite color to the played stone are flipped first. Then, if any stones of the played color remain dead, they are flipped the same way.) b) When such flippings are made, all the groups surrounding the flipped group(s) are removed. c) To prevent cycles, any stone on the board can only be flipped once. Once it is flipped, it can't be removed from the board, too. Flipped stones are called kings. I've added the above text to the rules file. The game rules are still exactly the same, so I hope this doesn't break the contest rules.
