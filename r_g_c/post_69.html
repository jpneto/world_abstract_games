Presumably some of the strategies for other connection games apply to Slither (e.g. place your pieces efficiently, don't commit too soon, control the center). The slither move adds a new aspect, though. It seems to me to serve two functions: 1) it gives the opportunity to "correct" slight tactical errors 2) it allows you to build a dynamic rather than static attack. The logical implications of function #2 is that you have more attacking options to work with, as well as an increased ability to "sacrifice" pieces (i.e. force your opponent to close off certain potential connections) in building an attack. Steve

As a tactical matter, I've noticed that it is usually a bad thing to put two of your own stones in alignment with one space between them. This invites the opponent to wedge in between, and it is theen usually diffi cult to impossible (depending on what's nearby) to get you stones connected again! So in extending from a stone into new(ish) territory, it pays to stretch to 2 spaces in between, if you want to stay in the same line. Any other useful tips? - Boardly Bill P.S.
