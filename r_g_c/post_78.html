On Dec 16, 6:40 pm, Nick Bentley wrote: > blah blah blah: > > http://nickbentley.posterous.com/new-game-breach Nick said: "The lack of strategy is the most difficult issue to fix so I start with that. Morro creates strategy through negative feedback. Negative feedback here refers to a penalty for taking the lead or getting closer to the win condition. In Morro, when you take the lead, your opponent gets a stone advantage. It's the opposite of "the rich get richer" dynamic. With negative feedback "the rich get poorer": sometimes taking the "lead" is a bad idea. As a result the players have to take the long view and play toward the endgame, rather than focus just on taking the lead. Voila. Strategy." Your thoughts about the relationship between negative feedback and possible increased strategy are interesting. The "grail" I mentioned to Corey is a game with one single drawn state; i.e. a single draw node on the game tree, which would be reached with perfect play. Well, that immediate remark was in response to a comment about Morro, which is a good example. Because of the sub-goal negative feedback, it is often a good diea NOT to make a new longest line, so that's what I'd call coldness in subgoal, as one does eventually want to make the longest line! But OC there's never coldness in moves in Morro - it's always good to have another move, one never wants to pass. And that, BTW, makes an excellent definition of coldness in moves, without any fancy talk about values and so forth: if one NEVER wants to pass, it is a hot game, if one would sometimes want to pass, it has a cold element. -- Torrid Taylor ** If you can't stand the heat, play Nim games.
