I was struck by Nick's plea about moku-games: > All n-in-a-row games (that I know of) suffer from > some mixture of 4 problems: > > 1. not enough strategy (tactics dominate) > 2. draws > 3. imbalance > 4. the pylon problem Joao and I have been playing a game of our own creation for some time. This seems to be a good time to announce it. It addresses three of the four problems above. The one it fails to address is (1) - as tactics still dominate it, even more so! There is no strategy at all, though conceivably some artificial alterations to the rules could change that. However at the moment we love it because of its quick-fire rapid-change features. (2) draws - we have never had one, and I cannot see how one could ever occur without co-operation. (3) imbalance is corrected by the 12* move modifier, as so often. (4) the pylon problem, (the presence of "dead clusters" having no further real effect on the game), *simply no longer exists* ! We call it "SUPER-PENTE", as that is what it most closely resembles, but another more descriptive name would be good. RULES ============================================ The idea is simple. It is played on a Go board; (an infinite board in theory, but that never goes down well, with the general public!) The goal is to make 4 (or more) in a row, (orthogonal or diagonal). The winner is whoever first achieves this. The move order is, one placement by the first player, which must be in the centre of the board; subsequently two placements per turn, *subject to the restriction* that the two stones must not be in the same ortho-or-diagonal line, whether adjacent or not. (The key idea:) If a line of 2 or more adjacent opponent stones become trapped between an existing stone and a newly-played stone, they are removed from the board. Stones are otherwise not moved. ======================================= END RULES I could give some hints or example games, but it will be more fun for you to find them out for yourselves. The key features of the game are as mentioned above - surprising twists of advantage and fate as the game proceeds, very rapid execution of plans, and the like. Games are usually quite short. -- Breathless Bill
