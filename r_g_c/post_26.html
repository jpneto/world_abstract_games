Welcome to Recre.Games.Combinatorial's first abstract game design contest. Game Design Constraints: 1. Win Condition: winner is player with majority stones on board when board is full. 2. Capture by Conversion: stones convert to the opposite color during the gameplay by some mechanism. 3. Playable in the iggc sandbox: this means the board for your game must use a square or hexagonal tiling. 4. Game should be for 2 players only. Contest Rules 1. Each entrant may submit no more 1 game to the contest. 2. Final entries are due on or before Dec 23rd 3. If you submit your entry before then, you may modify it up until the due date. 4. Any member of this forum may enter a design. 5. Entrants: submit your game by sending a link to the final rules to nickobento [at] gmail.com. Do so even if you've posted the rules on this forum. I'll publish a list of links on this forum the day after the entries are due. 6. Following the deadline, there will be a two-week evaluation period (Dec 23 to Jan 6), during which voters will have a chance to playtest the submitted games. 7. To be an eligible voter, you must a) be a member of this forum; and b) play each of the contest entries in the iggc sandbox during the evaluation period at least once. 8. Votes are due on or before Jan 6, 2011 9. To vote, each voter will provide a list of the games ordered by preference, from most preferred to least preferred. The winner will be determined by an instant runoff (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instant-runoff_voting) 10. To voters: please send your votes to nickobento [at] gmail.com 11. I'll announce the winner on January 7, 2011 Note: designers may vote for their own game (the election method will not be mucked up by this)

I hope someone has fun with it. the game will take only 30 turns for each player. Draws are not possible. Here's the text only version: Travicus tra â€“ pull vicus â€“ change Board and Pieces Travicus is played on an 8x8 board with white and black stones. The stones are placed on the board cells as in checkers or chess, and not on the intersections as in Go. The initial position has alternating colored stones in each of the 4 corners. White stones at a1 and h8, black stones at a8 and h1. Objective The goal is to have more stones of your color on the board than your opponent has. The game ends when the board is full. Play The first player begins the game as white. When the second player takes his first turn, he may choose to change colors with white rather than moving. Each turn consists of 4 moves, some of which are optional: * The player may move one of his stones one space horizontally or vertically. Diagonal moves are not permitted. This first move is optional. * The player may again move one of his stones one space horizontally or vertically. This stone may be the same stone as the stone moved in step 1, but the player may choose to move a different stone. This second move is optional. * The player may move one of his stones one more time. Again, the player may move one of the stones that has already been moved, or a different stone. On this third move, the player may also pull, and thus capture, one or more of his opponent's stones. This third move is optional. * Finally, the player must place a new stone of his color on the board in any empty space. This fourth move is not optional. To capture an opponent's stone, a pull mechanism is used. All pulled pieces must be immediately adjacent, and in a straight line. If black is pulling, only the immediately adjacent white pieces are pulled. Once an empty cell, or another black piece is reached, the pulling effect ceases. To pull, one stone of the player's color is moved to an empty adjacent cell, and all of the opponent's pieces that were behind that stone are pulled one step forward. Once pulled, the opponent's stones are removed from the board and replaced with the pulling player's stones. If Othello like pieces are used, the pieces may be simply flipped to change their color. When pulling, there is not net gain or loss with respect to the total number of stones on the board, only the color of the pulled stones changes. The pulling stone stays the same color. When the board is full, if each side has an equal number of stones, white wins. Â© 2010 Thomas Foy

Here's mine, Alea Evangelion: http://xifeng.posterous.com/new-as-yet--game The title is a terrible, three-way, three-language pun. I picked it because the capture mechanic is kind of like Evangelism: one stone converts all the stones around it. The driving idea behind it was ko-fights in Go, though I can't know for sure that such phenomena will be as prolific here as I hope. Because of that inherently unstable phenomenon, there's a possibility you could find yourself repeating the same sequence of moves over and over again. In that case I've adopted a somewhat unorthodox provisional rule: you must play a different move that breaks the cycle, or continue spinning your wheels in stubborn defiance of the rules of fun. Enjoy, Tristan
