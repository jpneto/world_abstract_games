Damn! I generally don't like the repeats-allowed versions, for two reasons. 1) I was introduced via "Bulls and Cows", where the no-repeat rule was standard, and developed cool pencil-and-paper techniques for playing well. So I selfishly dislike to change. 2) The repeats-allowed version is much harder to keep track of during a pencil-&-paper game. And harder in a bad way - it just adds complications without adding much depth. And this is not merely subjective:- I found that the rules used by servers seem to be rather illogical - they are not purely numerical, but involve a certain "human-oriented" aspect. This is borne out by the fact that they are annoyingly difficult to program - it can be done, but a simpler purely-numerical rule would make it so much easier even to get a computer to *play* the game! So, I haven't done much on servers, though I consider myself one of the best (at no-repeats).
