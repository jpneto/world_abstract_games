Maybe; it's interesting to note that kingwise-adjacency allows both players to have one huge group on the board, and indeed on smallish boards filled in at random this would be almost certain to happen! However, the odd-group rule forbids this, so it would be interesting to see what would happen. It may be that the game would become one of very local tactics as players tried to make or prevent tiny isolated groups, around the edges of a giant 2-group ocean. In such a case parity would be king, and the game might have a similar feel to Dots-&-Boxes where that very feature is dominant for most of the game. > Maybe even a trivial win for one of the players, Possibly, especially if it is too easy to kill isolated groups, by joining them to the main ocean. It would be nice to find out. > Also, I think it isn't good to have too few groups at the end of > the game, because the better player will have a harder time > showing his superiority. Well, yes, but only if it is really easy to kill isolated bits. > The game becomes more, say, "volatile". I would call it volatile only if it turned out EASY to make isolated groups, as then no-one would have a real idea of the likely parity till near the end. That sounds a bit like Reversi, to most of us! And come to think of it, that would likely be the case with your own Xodd.
