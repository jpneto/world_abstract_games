I've played about half the games, but it was hard getting matches. I wanted to play them all, but it was not feasible. Kitaran is obviously the best out of the bunch. It scales nicely, is balanced, has great clarity, balances strategy and tactics equally, and the infinite problem turned out to be an illusion. Deadcell was my favorite of the rest that I played. A fun little brawl with what felt like a lot of potential. Pilf was quite fun. Lots of securing territory then invading. It felt very similar to Hexxagon, though. Arrello was colder than a minesota winter. Not something I'm fond of, but cold games seem popular around these parts. The strategy of trying to secure lines is a fun one, trying to secure parity isn't so fun. Bonus points for being really clear, though. Verto kind of made me angry, I felt like I had very little choice in what I could do, and even with the placement restriction it felt too easy to clump up in the center. Implo took too long, we did not finish on the 9x9 board. I can't believe the instructions suggest 19x19. I'm sorry I couldn't play the rest of the games.
