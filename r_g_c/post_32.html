Can't say much without having played it, but the underlying idea (as I see it), that better spaces on the board should be more difficult to get, is a good one. In that respect, it reminds me a bit of conhex and aronda. Also like aronda, play would likely proceed from the outside in. The issue I see with swaparound seems to be that while there is the potential to change colors five times, I'm guessing it would probably only happen once or twice, in which case it just amounts to a player getting to go twice in a row. And what if someone plays the first move in the center? Then after a swap, you're just playing Y on a different board. Still, it's an interesting idea and I'd like to see how it actually plays.
