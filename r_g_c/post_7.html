On Nov 12, 11:09 am, Jackspritz wrote: > Christian Freeling made the error of posting game-related material to > rec.bile.insults, a big no no. I'm going to mirror these posts > describing his fascinating design process for Sygo here. Well, what did I tell you? Nobody cares. And if I had posted it myself, it would for some members just have been a confirmation of their idea that I'm a conceited old brat. I discovered the symple mechanism a month ago or so and made four games on it, six if you count translations, and four of these since Nick announced the contest. Are they all great games? No, not according to Mark Thompson's definition, to which I subscribe. Symple lacks drama, Charybdis has too much of it, Lhexus is inconsequential. But Sygo is another matter. I mowed the grass, put the ball on the penalty spot, removed the keeper and and said "hey guys, put 2 and 2 together and you may have a great game. But no-one was paying attention, fortunately, so I could invent it myself before somebody else even saw the ball and the empty goal. I'm an annoyingly talented reclusive and I rub people the wrong way by doing what I do without much effort and without consulting, trying, testing, talking and the like. Well, let me shade that: on my return I did cooperate with Arty and David Bush, and I wasn't in the best of shapes back then. Next Query, which is actually the closest thing to 'square hex, went almost unnoticed. Yet when I hear members say that they need at least six weeks, or that they've been working on an idea 'for months', I can't help thinking "if you can't find it in a day, you won't find it in a month either". That's why I don't think much about games, usually. If I can't see my prey, I leave it, and go walking with the dogs. If I see it, I hunt it down. Bummer for the tedious diggers, but not my problem. And that's why I only post in reply, should that not already be clear from the lack of response your mirrored post about Sygo gets.
