My submission is now named Kitaran. The most recent rules will always be at http://xifeng.posterous.com/new-as-yet--game This is only my second game to make it this far in the development cycle (I've played it a few times and had a lot of fun). As such, I'm new to a lot of the emotions that surround making a game. It's almost like something alive: I may have invented the rules, but I had very little idea what kind of game those rules would create. So I have a kind of irrational love for it. On a turn, you may place a stone of your color on an empty space, or you may flip all the enemy stones adjacent to one of your stones already on the board. You may not flip a stone that was flipped on your opponent's most recent turn, or one that was placed on that turn. If you cannot flip all of the stones surrounding a piece, you may not use that piece to flip any stones. But any stone may be the center of a conversion. When the board is full, the game ends immediately. The player with the most stones wins. You may not pass. If one player is determined to have an advantage, players may agree on a komi before they begin. I've taken a slightly different route from most designers I've followed. I was not interested in making a mathematically elegant game; I am not concerned that it is not theoretically finite. I have tried to instill my own values in this game, but like a child, it has its own nature that may turn out quite different from what I expected. So far it is going in the directions I hoped: the yin-yang cycle is reflected in both old-young and firm-yielding forms at different levels of abstraction, it feels very clear, and there's a distinct feel of motion. Even should it become degenerate in the future, learning it and playing it will be a rewarding experience until then. Best luck to everyone, Tristan
