As people may know, I usually like to thoroughly play-test a game before releasing it into the wild. However, as Nick Bentley has inaugurated a policy of throwing out half-considered ideas, in order to jeep up discussion and cross-fertilization on them, I thought I'd try the same. I am also inspired by Nick's excellent theme of approximating negative feedback by having a game where one wins by finishing up with the largest connected group, while at the same time having rule-sets that otherwise encourage keeping groups as small as possible. Such games typically are fun to play, as Joao and I have found. So here is my idea: 1: Hex-hex board (IMHO best for largest=group-wins) 2: One stone played per turn in an empty cell. (Standard variations:- 12* modifier (for speed emails), Maybe use "Sabotage" modifier - i.e. may play opponent stones) 3. (The key new rule) Instead of a placement, one may do: If two of one's own stones have an uninterrupted queen-move view of an opponent stone, they may kill it (by joint co-ordination, military style), AND all the group it is part of !! That very last point is what keeps the groups small for as long as poss. It is OC directly borrowed from Nick's last game idea. The idea of killing by co-ordination is part of Abbot's "Ultima" game, though not elsewhere AFAIK. Perhaps the game could be called "Triangulation". If anyone has any comments, or suggestions to make that might improve the game, please write in. Any of the above 3 points (or any others) are fair game! -- Bill of writes Said Hamlet to Ophelia I'll draw a sketch of thee. What kind of pencil shall I use? 2B or not 2B?

Hi, disregarding the group winning condition and the placement only style, I used "triangulated" pieces already in Attangle. http://attangle.com/rules/official Here the two attacking pieces are moved, i.e. one of them is placed on top of the attacked piece and the other one (the "supporter piece") is taken back to the stock. This way the attacker, gaining a stronger position through the capture, is also weakened at the same time because he loses a piece already placed on the board. It's another game but regarding the capture mechanism we met here. Maybe it's helpful bringing your idea forward. Dieter
