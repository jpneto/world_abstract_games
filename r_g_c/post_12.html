As the game is completely impartial, (especially with PIE), it doesn't really matter WHAT physical shape the board is, so long as it is combinatorially the same, i.e. marked into three regions along the sides, and trivalent internally. So we could have, "SQUARE Y"... a a a a a a a a b . . . . . . . . . c b . . . . . . . . c b . . . . . . . . . c Winner is a connected group b . . . . . . . . c touching all three letters. b . . . . . . . . . c b . . . . . . . . c b . . . . . . . . . c b . . . . . . . . c [Assuaging the bookcase storing problem!] b . . . . . . . . . c b b b b c c c c ...or various length-ratios of RECTANGULAR Y. Note that the a, b, c regions need not be symmetric or even have the same length of cells.
