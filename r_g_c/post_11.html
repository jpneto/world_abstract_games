First about corners in Connection Games. It strikes me again, as others have observed long ago, that the standard basic games like Hex and Y do not even have *slightly* special corners, if the game is so re-formatted. So Hex, for example, 4x4, is just x x x . . . . o o . . . . o o . . . . o o . . . . x x x with these pre-set stones; and the task being to join them up, in Atoll-like fashion. Similarly in Y, the board starts with three rims of neutral colors, = . + = . . + the task being to connect your group to all = . . . + three neutral colors. = . . . . - - - So, in both, the game can be re-presented without actual change, in such a way as to avoid mentioning corners (or even sides!) altogether. Whether this is worthwhile is a purely aesthetic matter, but in some game servers the graphics would lend themselves to this quite well, I think. In Atoll, the game itself depends essentially on these pre-set edge colors being there, to help effect connections, a feature which adds that particular piquancy to the game that we fans love. And the fact that the pre-set rims is essential in Atoll, (and its higher-order extensions, as I mentioned once before), suggests that it really "ought to be there" in the two basic cases, Hex and Y, as well. From a mathematically inclusive PoV, at least. And perhaps from a pretty-graphics PoV as well, as I said.
