CORNERLESS Y ============ Corners have always been a slight annoyance in connection games; they are easily dealt with by overlapping the edges, of course, but it seems ever so slightly clumsy. Havannah grasps the nettle firmly and makes them a complete special case. Rather ugly, though. In Cornerless Y, the matter is solved by the opening layout, which also has its ugly side, but is quite cute nonetheless. The board size is flexible, (though can only be of one parity); the one shown below is much too small for real strategic play. """"""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""""" RULES ----- The game is Y, with PIE rule, played on a Y board initialised thus:- x o o . x x . . o o . . . x x . . . . o o . . . . . x x . . . . . . o o . . . . . . . x x . . . . . . . . o o . . . . . . . . . x x o x o x o x o x o _____________________________________________________________ And that's it! It's somewhat of a marvel that this opening distribution guarantees a unique winner! ----------------------------------------------------------------- Y-VERSI ======= The same game, with the same opening layout, but including Reversi-style captures. RULES ----- Board as above. Standard alternating play. Stones may be played on any vacant cell, and any lines of adjacent opponent stones pincered between the played stone and another friendly stone, are captured by colour-reversal. The game must continue until the board is full. _____________________________________________________________ That last rule is crucial - a player may well get a Y-group partway through the game, but does not immediately win, and may indeed lose at the game end! Have fun! -- Beaming Bill
