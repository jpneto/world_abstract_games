I've added a small additional rule to Verto (http://spielstein.com/ games/verto). -- Stones may only entered on corner spaces if there is another stone adjacent. (It follows from the other rules that this other stone must be an enemy stone.) Like in Reversi corners are special, but Verto needed some more work on this. Anyway, I think this exception rule does not lower the elegance factor of Verto (how high it may be :) Dieter

> In fact, I'm wondering if a special rule for corner squares is > necessary at all... My plan was to avoid too obvious/defensive play: As you are safe in the corners, the first 4 moves of the players could be placing stones there. And I didn't want to end up with a Reversi setup (here: the corners preset). > After all, it can't be really that advantageous to > place a piece there, since you won't be able to join more pieces to it > until some enemy pieces are around. You won't gain territory on the board joining your stones, but rather by capture moves. So it can be advantageous to leave a stone in the corner safely waiting alone for the opponent to approach.
