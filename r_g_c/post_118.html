This is an almost-repost from 2.5 years ago, on various presentation formats for "Y". It seems that plain old "Y" can be re-formatted in no less than 3, count them, THREE, ways. And no doubt there can be others as well. Original style:- .". Task: connect the three sides. . . . . . . . . . . . . Corners need a special mention in the rules. . . . . . . : . . . . . : _______________________ Bichromatic style:- x . o o . . x Task: connect three of your original stones, x . . . o one from each line. o . . . . x x . . . . . o Neither corners nor sides need a special mention. o . . . . . . x . . . . . . . o x o x o x o _______________________ Trichromatic style:- - . = - . . = Task: connect the three colors. - . . . = - . . . . = - . . . . . = Neither corners nor sides need a special mention. - . . . . . . = . . . . . . . + + + + + + _______________________ Which you prefer is completely a matter of taste, the game is identical in all cases. Bichromatic seems a bit ugly, though it has certain advantages; but I'd like to see physical boards or (ESPECIALLY!) game sites try the trichromatic board - it could be very cute! Five colors in total, with a 6th for the unplayed cells. -- Boardly Bill
