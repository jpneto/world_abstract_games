On Feb 13, 2:26 pm, camb wrote: > I'm interested in knowing how long the ko rule has been used in Go. > This includes any form of ko or superko in any form of the game. There is Tibetan Go which is supposed to be an archaic form of Go. In Tibetan Go, you may not recapture on a field where one of your stones has been captured in the last turn. The difference to the modern Ko rule is that it applies to the capture of more than one stone, too - no "eternity" in the sense of the modern Ko rule. I cannot remember any source stating when the transition took place. Benedikt
