New game: AYU ============= AYU is a misÃ¨re combinatorial game with the look of a connection game. Definitions ----------- In this rules, "adjacent" always means "orthogonally adjacent". A unit is a singleton or group. A singleton is a piece with no adjacencies to like-colored pieces. A group is a set of like-colored, adjacent pieces. Play ---- The game is played on the intersections (points) of an odd-sized square board. 11x11 is suggested as the standard size. At the start of the game, Black and White pieces are arranged in the following pattern: . x . x . x . x . x . o . o . o . o . o . o . x . x . x . x . x . o . o . o . o . o . o . x . x . x . x . x . o . o . o . o . o . o . x . x . x . x . x . o . o . o . o . o . o . x . x . x . x . x . o . o . o . o . o . o . x . x . x . x . x . Black plays first, then turns alternate. On each turn, players must do one of the following actions: a) Move a friendly singleton to an adjacent empty point. b) Take a piece from a friendly group and place it on a different empty point adjacent to the same group, without splitting it at any moment. Every move must reduce the distance between the moved unit and the closest friendly unit. The distance between two units is the shortest path of adjacent empty points between them, i.e. the number of consecutive moves one would need to join them. If a player can't make a move on his turn, he _wins_. This usually occurs when said player has joined all his pieces in a single group. Draws and cycles are not possible.

As a curiosity, there is a minor, truly combinatorial Ayu variant in which players must join two or more groups on every turn, and whoever can't move loses.

The rule is now as follows: "b) Take a piece from a friendly group and place it on a different empty point adjacent to the same group.
