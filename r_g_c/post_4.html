("Move modifier" is our term for meta move, as mentioned in another thread. e.g. progressive, 1222, etc). I include a partially-played example as illustration. It appears to be the 8th instance we played. I seem to have won the first 7! If anyone wishes to challenge me (the world champ!) by email, please do so. :) (The board needn't be as big, OC, to start with.) SWEETHEART Y [B:7] game 8 ============ Y, with the following modifier. On each turn the player plays a stone of his own, then (if possible) an opponent stone next to one of the opponent's last-played stones, then a stone of his own under group restriction. OK, I'll answer a slightly different question from what you asked. I'll comment, not on the sweetheart itself, but on the simpler modifier of 12222... (i.e. 12* in finite language notation), as applied WITH GROUP RESTRICTION to CONNECTION games. So this would cover 12* Hex, 12* Y, 12* Atol, and so on; the varieties are almost endless. We have found that the 12*-restricted, which was originally introduced more as just an equaliser, (with the restriction inserted to prevent mindless extensions of walls from Switzerland to the sea!), has appeared with a strange and exciting dynamic all of its own! The diamond connection, x . . is still completely viable with the restriction, . . x . so the games still look & feel quite a bit like plain connections, BUT there is a 2nd-order effect, whereby these cannot be endlessly chained. For example, in this group, . . . . . . . . . which would be unbreakable . x . . . . . . in regular connections, . . x . . . . is no longer so in 12*, even . . . x . . under group restriction. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . Clearly O can place his 2 stones as shown, . x o . . . . . and X cannot protect both diamonds! . . x o . . . This leads to all sorts of dynamic . . . x . . tussles as the two players try to . . . . . balance attack, defense, and extension in various different ways - there are often two or three different battles going on simultaneously, unlike usually just the one in regular connection games. I strongly recommend just playing a few rounds of plain Y, with 12* group-restricted, to get the feel of it. Remember - I'm always up for email games with anyone who wants to try! I'm all in favour of playing these things, not just designing them! 12* restricted connection games are FUN. > I'm having a tough time knowing without playing. Wouldn't we all! Again I repeat there is no substitute for actual play. Surely folk have a few minutes per day to spare for some email games?! ............ As to the Sweetheart modifier specifically, it has all the joys of the above, with some extra, rather more minor wrinkles, involved in trying to get the best worth from your opponent's forced gift to you. The adjacency rule is crucial here, otherwise gifts could be just shoved away uselessly into sharp corners. -- Boardly Bill
