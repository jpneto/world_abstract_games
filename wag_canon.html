WAG's 20th Century Canon of Abstract Games

About this page

The number of published games is immense. As an example, BGG indexes around 5000 board games just on the Abstract Strategy category. Interested players might feel overwhelmed and, in deciding to pick something to play, will certainly miss many good games out there that they would probably prefer to experience.

This list proposes a 20th century canon of abstract games for two players. Abstract games are defined as being deterministic and all game information is available to each player when deciding to move. This excludes random elements, hidden information, but also simultaneous turns.

To filter even more, I decided to not consider close variants of classical games, with a big exception for Checkers. Why this exception? Checkers have seen dramatic developments over the 20th century, with games that are objectively better than most traditional variants, and some of those deserve to be in this list. The same cannot be said, imho, about Chess and Go.

The list is the result of choosing games that are in two or more of the following categories: (a) were culturally or commercially relevant, (b) successfully applied new ludemes or rule interactions, (c) have above-average depth or drama, (d) are fun to play.

The list tries to find some balance in the number of games per decade, accepting the fact that average game quality increases with time. Later decades do include more games, but I'm more demanding about newer games.

The list mixes classic board games, that most people expect to appear in a canon like this, and more obscure games that deserve to be more well-known. The goal is to provide something worthwhile to beginners, veteran players, and game designers.

Notes: (a) some dates are estimates, (b) games labeled with two stars would make my Top10, (c) games with one star stand out a bit more than the others.
