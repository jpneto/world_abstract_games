The Rules of Keryo-Pente

Object of the Game

On a 19x19 board, the two players of keryo-pente try to be the first to create a line of 5 or more stones in a row of their color *or* try to capture 15 of their opponent's stones. You capture may 2 or 3 opponent's stones any time you sandwich the stones between a pair of your stones.

Movement Rules

On your turn, you place a piece of your colour wherever you desire on the board.

Tournament rules stipulate that Ohs first move goes to K10, and that Ohs next move *not* be within 2 spaces of the piece at K10.

Eks has no movement restrictions.

Capture Examples

        . . . . . .        . . . . . .
        . . . . x .        . . . . x .
        . . . o . .        . . . . . .
        . . o . . .   =>   . . . . . .
        . A o o o x        . x . . . x
        . . . . . .        . . . . . .

An 'x' piece placed at A results in the capture of 5 of Ohs men. 

        . . . . . .        . . . . . .
        . . . . x .        . . . . x .
        . . . o . .        . . . o . .
        . . B . . .   =>   . . o . . .
        . x . . . .        . x . . . .
        . . . . . .        . . . . . .

An 'o' piece placed at B does *not* result in the capture of any Ohs men. Eks must do the "sandwich"ing to capture the Ohs men.

In the multi-player version, you may sandwich any two or three stones that do not belong to you. They do not have to be stones belonging to the same opponent.
