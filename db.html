World of Abstract Games Database

Goal
  • annihilation - remove all friendly pieces
  • block - one player must prevent the opponent's goal
  • capture - capture the adversary army (or part of it)
  • connect - connection game
  • enclosure - surround something
  • mancala - sowing game
  • moku - make a N in-a-row
  • promotion - promote to something
  • pattern - achieve some shape
  • royal - checkmate/capture a royal piece
  • stalemate - be the last to move
  • score - get N points
  • single-group - make all pieces belong to one group
  • territory - maximize area control
  • zone - reach some part of the board
Ludemes
  • asymmetric - players have different armies/strength
  • auction - there's an initial bid phase to decide color
  • bounce - pieces bounce at the edge or in other pieces
  • chain-reaction - an action might provoke more similar actions
  • colorN - needs N piece colors (default is two colors of one/two types)
  • connect - use connection concepts
  • custodian - capture pieces in-between
  • dagger - player has the option of extra move(s)
  • demotion - pieces can be downgraded
  • drop - pieces can be placed on the board
  • explode - pieces explode and destroy neighbors
  • gravity - game uses or simulates weight and/or fall of pieces
  • group - groups of connected pieces have relevance
  • grow - pieces grow from/to a pattern
  • intrusion - capture pieces at left/right (inverse of custodian)
  • jump - other jumps
  • long-jump - jump more than one piece in line
  • ko - no repeated position rule is included
  • magnetic - attract/repulse pieces
  • move - pieces move
  • multiple-move - moves can be repeated in the same turn
  • multiple-jump - jumps can be repeated in the same turn
  • neutral - use neutral pieces
  • pencil&paper - can be played with just pencil and paper
  • phalanx - a line of pieces can move together
  • pie - balancing rule is included
  • pivot - move by orbiting pieces, use of pivots
  • polyominoes - use polyominoes/tile pieces
  • promotion - pieces can be upgraded
  • push - pieces(s) can push
  • push-off - pieces can be pushed off the board
  • range - move/capture range depends on some criteria
  • remove - remove some pattern of pieces from the board
  • replacement - capture by replacement
  • reserve - number of pieces off-board has a relevant role
  • rotate - pieces are able to rotate
  • setup - has a initial setup phase
  • shoot - capture by line-of-sight
  • short-jump - checkers-like jump with enemy capture
  • slide - pieces slide over a line of empty cells
  • sow - stacks or group of pieces are sowed Mancala-like
  • stack - stacks can be made
  • swap - two or more pieces swap position
  • wall - use wall pieces
