RINGWORM  

Copyright (c) 2009 João Pedro Neto

The game starts at an empty 9x9 hexagonal board:

 

A ring is a shape of six hexagons of the same color around the same cell.

  • MOVE - On his turn, the player drops his friendly stones. Players use the progressive sequence 134* moves
    • When a Ring is made, the drop sequence stops and the moving player must burst it, i.e., it chooses one of its stones as a pivot and place the other five stones in a straight connected line starting at the pivot.
    • Any stone occupying those line cells, friend of foe, is captured.
    • A player continues his turn if a new Ring is produced (by bursting it)
    • If two or more rings are made, the player must choose only one to burst.
    • A player can pass his turn. After two consecutive passes, the game ends.
  • GOAL - Wins the player with more stones.
