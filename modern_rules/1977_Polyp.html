POLYP

COMPOSITION OF THE GAME:

1 Game board (see end of page)

36 wood cubes (9 fish per player)


4 Polyps(1 of each color, yellow, red, green, blue)

tentacle left head tentacle right 		One polyp consists of 5 chained pawns: 2 tentacles (of 2 pawns) and 1 head.
The length of the chain between two pieces must allow place its two pins in two adjacent squares diagonally
 
RULE OF THE GAME:
 
PURPOSE THE GAME
Fish must cross the game board to reach the Diametrically opposite colored zone.

PREPARATION
Place the tray in the center of the table.
Each player receives the fish of a color and the polyp co-responding. Place the pawns as on the diagram opposite.
   
FOLDING OF THE GAME
The first player is drawn at random, and then each player plays in turn in the clockwise direction.
In turn a player must move one of his fish, then move his polyp.

Displacement of fish
A fish can move either by sliding a square (vertically, horizontally or diagonally), either by jump. A fish can jump by on top another fish of any color, conditions that the square which is behind it is free. A fish can also jump by on top the pawn of a polyp, provided that the box behind it be free. He is allowed to chain the jumps.
Note: A fish cannot mix the two kinds displacement: sliding and jumping.

Attention: Each movement must bring the fish closer together from his destination area, he has no right to go back, not by moving diagonally to remain at the same distance .
         

The blue fish cannot perform this sequence of jumps, the box B is further from its objective than box A
   

The blue fish can't make that jump, it would stay the same distance from his goal.
 
This chaining jumps is allowed
  

The blue fish cannot move from one square to the right, This takes him away from his goal.
   
   
Travel of the Polipe
The player must move 2 of the games of his polipe, a tentacle and the head or the two tentacles. During this trip, Each pawn constituting the polyp cannot move further of a square (vertically, horizontally or diagonally). Also the head polyp must remain on the purple axes of the game board. If the player manages to superimpose one of the pawns of his policle on an opposing fish, The latter is captured.
A caught fish remains under the pawn of the polyp. When a polyp has caught 5 fish, he brings them back to his den (starting box of the polyp) and go back to hunting.

Attention: Each pawn of the polyp can "contain" only one only fish. This makes catches more and more difficult at the fure and as the pawns of the polyp "fill". One Polyp pion that already contains a fish can no longer be superimposed to a fish.

END OF THE GAME
The game ends when all the fish have reached their arrival zone, Or when they were captured.
We make the count: An opposing fish caught is worth 3 points, A fish brought to good port is worth 2 points.
The player with the most points wins the game.
ex: The red player managed to get the board through 6 of his fish (12 points) and he caught 2 blue fish and 1 yellow fish with its polyp (9 points). In total it has 12 + 9 = 21 points
