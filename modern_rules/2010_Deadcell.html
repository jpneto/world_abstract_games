Deadcell

An abstract game for two players by Joshua Conroy

Goal:

The goal of dead cell is to have more stones of your color than your opponent when the board is full.

Equipment:

A 7x7x7 hex hex board (Any regular hex board may be used, but the 7x7x7 seemed most manageable between strategy and tactics, I think 6x6x6 might be better, but it's not availible in the iggamecenter sandbox)

127 Black Stones

127 White Stones

127 Red Stones 

(in a physical version of the game it would be best to have the black and white pieces be double sided with the red pieces to be played on top of them)

Game Play:

After determining a starting player in any means of your choosing, play begins. One player plays white and the other plays black, with red representing the neutral or "dead" stones. Each player decides whether to place a stone of their color or the dead color on any unoccupied space in the grid. A dead stone has no effect on the surrounding stone. A colored stone will convert all adjacent enemy stones to its color and will convert any adjacent stones of its own color to being dead and will have no effect on adjacent dead stones. Turns continue to alternate until the board is full.

In the case of a tie, the states the dead cells were in before being converted or placed can be counted to determine a definite winner.


Example Play:

1. The beginning board state.

2. White places a stone of their color in the center space.

3. All adjacent black stones become white and adjacent white stones become red and adjacent red stones are unaffected.
