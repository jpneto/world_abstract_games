> I don't want to insult simultaneous games and say they're less pure
> than sequential games, but suffice to say that in general the notion of
> simultaneous moves doesn't hold an immediate appeal for me.

There seem to be at least two different kinds of simultaneous games: 1)
games, where you decide the move of pieces of your own, and 2) games
(like Ricochet Robot) where all players have to deal with shared pieces.
The latter is a game of perfect information, but strongly contrasting
eg. to Chess (or Byte) for its uncomfortable feeling of constant time
pressure.
