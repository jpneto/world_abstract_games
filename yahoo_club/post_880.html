Another twist on "Knight Pente".

If I recall correctly, the original message about the
topic, on this yahoogroup, was posted a few days ago
by someone using the address "Markthomps". (My
apologies to the real author, if I misremembered.)

Markthomps said, basically, that the _Move_ was
restricted to Knight moves, but the _Goal_ (i.e. the
definition of how to win the game) remained the same
(i.e. you get five-in-a-row _not_ using knight moves).

I suddenly notice you could create yet another
interesting variant by taking the reverse of what
Markthomps said. In other words, say that the player
can move anywhere he wants, as in Orthodox Pente, but
the five-in-a-row that creates a win must be arranged
along Knight moves. In other words, if you had stones
on a1, b3, c5, d7, e9, that would be a win.

Niankhaset.
