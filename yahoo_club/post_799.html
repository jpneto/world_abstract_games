Somebody said:

>
> Clarity:
> Good - Hex, Checkers
> Bad - Othello, Lines of Action

I agree that I am bad at Reversi (or the modern only half so good
plagiarism Othello) because I find the turning over of pieces
confusing. The same for Yinsh. Does that make a game "bad"? -- On the
other hand I find Lines of Action very clear, while Checkers
difficult to play because you have to think ahead so many moves
(which turns my view rather "unclear")! How do you measure Clarity?
When I started to play Go I found the game very unclear, just because
of the giant size of the board, but when I became a 5-kyu and better,
my perception changed.

> Drama:
> Good - Chess
> Bad - Hex

Some people may say: If you claim that the drama is weak in Hex, it's
because you don't understand what is going on, because you are a poor
player.
