I'm not sure what to make of simultaneous games. The first one I found
out about was "Pulling Strings" by Clark Rodeffer. Certainly original,
interesting, and well thought of, but regarding simultaneous games in
general, there seems to be kind of a poker like, "mind reading"
element, where it helps to know your opponent and what he might likely
do. Of course I suppose that's true in all games to some extent.

I don't want to insult simultaneous games and say they're less pure
than sequential games, but suffice to say that in general the notion of
simultaneous moves doesn't hold an immediate appeal for me.
