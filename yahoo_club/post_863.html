Hi Martin,

> What does the group think about the game-logic in the game Unlur ?
> Does it helps with the first-move advance ?

Yes I think the contract phase of Unlur helps address any inherent
first move advantage. The board positions that develop during the
contract are complex enough that I believe it would be extremely
difficult for either player to consistently force an advantage game
after game.

However, my limited experience with Unlur makes me wonder if the
contract mechanism is *too* powerful. It seems that the decision to
call the contract is overwhelmingly critical and in most cases
decides the game; playing the rest of the game out becomes something
of a denouement. This seems to be exacerbated by the game's unequal
goals.

But don't get me wrong, I think it's a most interesting game!

In terms of first move advantage, Chameleon appears to be well
balanced. Chameleon is played as per Hex, except that players may
choose to play a piece of either colour per turn, and players win by
connecting their edges with a chain of either colour. Any move that
is good for the player is also potentially good for the opponent,
hence first move advantage is diminished.
