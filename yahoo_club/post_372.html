Another free way to play Twixt or Y is with AIM's image transfer
and a graphics program. It's awkward to say the least, but a
wide variety of games could be played this way.
The idea is, you send an image
of the board back and forth, using the graphics program
to make moves. At least, in Y, pieces are added to the
board but are never removed. So that slightly
reduces the hassle. Here is where you can get a blank board:
http://www.flash.net/~markthom/html/the_game_of_y.html
