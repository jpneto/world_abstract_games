Ok, what I am curious about here is anyone here actually knows how
Chess960 works. In it, the positions mirror one another. I don't see
any way for someone picking a formation, and it be fair to both sides.
The only issue with Chess960 is that you don't know who is favored.

I think your second approach to it is probably the way it needs to be
run, because they mirror one another. It would be easier to implement
also, and handle any shades of questioning whether or not it is
random. I believe the way you agree to put pieces down needs to be
determined. Do players alternate pushing bishops down, and then
rooks, or do they put both rooks and so ok?

It is still Chess960, but would be closer to the Thompson definition,
and would be an IAGO standard version of it. IAGO gets involved with
Chess960, and has no objections of randomness.

Once we get an agreement as to how to get this to work, it will be the
IAGO Standard Chess960 of the game (Also goes by Thompson Chess960).

IAGO Standard Chess960 is a reduced version IAGO Standard Billion
Chess, where players have flexibility to place their pieces anywhere
in the back row, alternating turns placing their pieces one at a time.
Castling is available, if it king is inside at least one of the rooks.

Anyone want to playtest this to find the best version of the Thompson
Chess960?
