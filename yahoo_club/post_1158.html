I am going to post another message on here with the criterion. As
someone pointed out, there is two aspects to "abstract strategy".
"Abstract" applies to lack of theme, and "strategy" to being based
upon player's cognitive skills (lack of luck). As a result here, it
is standard vs non-standard. The idea of the tight definition is so
that no one would disagree with it. The definition for standard even
goes beyond games that are combinatorial game theory games, to even
exclude teams. Non-standard is to allow for teams, or even some games
that have luck to them. But it is tight enough to prevent morphing
into something else.
