That'a good point.

When testing out a new game it's always good to gauge how well it
resists obstructionist opponents intent on spoiling the game. If a
player can force a draw or infinitely delay defeat, then the game's
rules need some more work.

This is one aspect in which connection games shine. Most connection
games converge to a guarnateed win, so if a player makes negative
moves they will inevitably lose the game.
