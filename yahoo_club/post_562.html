Using an X/Y graph to play by mail. The points on the graph are
considered exactly one quarter of a stone distant orthogonal.

Each placement of a stone is notated by its x/y coordinate on the
playing field. This notation is always in increments of one-quarter
of stone. Thus 1.00 is equal to one stone, 1.50 is equal to one and
one-half of a stone. Stone are noted to occupy a point with their
centers and all measurements are made from the center of each stone.

The relation of one stone to another can be obtained by subtracting
the x/y coordinates one from another.

A stone is considered adjacent if it is locate on the following x/y
coordinates from any other:

0.00/=B11.00
=B10.25/=B11.00
=B10.50/=B11.00
=B10.75/=B10.75
=B11.00/=B10.50
=B11.00/=B10.25
=B11.00/0.00

It can never be located less than these from any particular stone,
and it can only be located adjacent no more than two distinct stones.

The following coordinates are the minimum needed for placing a
solitary stone, this is in relation to any stone on the field. This
will allow that such placements result in a distance of one and one-
half stones between any other stone.

0.00/=B12.50
=B10.25/=B12.50
=B10.50/=B12.50
=B10.75/=B12.50
=B11.00/=B12.25
=B11.25/=B12.25
=B11.50/=B12.00
=B11.75/=B11.75
=B12.00/=B11.50
=B12.25/=B11.25
=B12.25/=B11.00
=B12.50/=B10.75
=B12.50/=B10.50
=B12.50/=B10.25
=B12.50/0.00

Such solitary placements can exceed these measurements, but will
never be less.

When the placement of a stone results in the capture of a string,
each coordinate for the stones of that string are noted in their
order of connection, including the coordinates of the other stone
which aids in the string capture.

Players should begin their coordinate system on the playing field
from the center. If using a circular field, merely disallow those
coordinates which fall outside the arc.
