From Gamut of Games:
"[...]And if he doesn't,
he is still assured a draw.
My answer to someone
who plays in this manner, is not to play with
him."

Indeed, I found that also to be a weak answer to a real
problem.
Another sentence that I don't agree from the Dover
edition (1992),
is when he says that 1982 marks the
end of a golden age of
serious games. It's true
that there is a flood of RPGs, Computer
Games, ...
but there are still many beautiful abstract games

being created.

Have said that, I do love Sackson
work, both his games and also his
almost
archeological work around old games, removing from oblivion

many otherwise doomed games. He has done a remarkable
work!

Of course, in the sense of just play for fun, two
people can
decide not to use the mirror strategy,
and still profit from
the game.

But the
fact is that the existence of the mirror strategy
creates
a big flaw. However, there is always ways to
prevent it, as Sackson
proposed. There are several
ways, ones more artificial then others,
Sackson's
2nd proposal seems a bit artificial.

Another
simple procedure is just to play those games on a odd
board,
say 7x7 or 9x9 for FOCUS. These boards have a Tengen
(center board)
that cannot be reflected, so the mirror
strategy is doomed to fail
sooner or later (like in Go,
as Greg pointed out).
