There are various French and German pages that refer to the game (on-line
game stores, Essen reports, etc.) that can be found through Google, and by
using the Google translator it's possible to see some descriptions of the
game in English. I haven't found a complete rule set however. What I have
found agrees with TruthForum's description. There is also apparently a rule
that gives a player an additional free move after he moves a pyramid onto a
dark space. The pyramids are equalateral and 3-sided just like a D4 die, and
are moved by tumbling them onto one of their three visible faces, landing on
one of the adjacent triangular spaces.

Hello,


> Do you have the rules for Sahara? Can you give a summary of them?

Not easy. I played it about half a year ago:

At the beginning the pieces are in pairs at the corners of the board,
on the left side "white" on the right side is "black".

Photo:
http://www.toysinternational.cc/images/Sahara.jpg


The pieces are pyramides which are moving by rotating to an adjacent
triangular "square" of either color. Pieces cannot rotate over
another piece. A piece is captured when all sides are bordered by the
edge of the board and/or another piece of either color. The first
player to capture a piece wins the game (as in "The Game of Susan").
