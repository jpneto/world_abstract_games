What famous people said about Draughts ...

Author: Ralf

"The game of Draughts is not merely an idle amusement. Several very
valuable qualities of the mind, useful in the course of human life,
are to be acquired or strengthend by it so as to become habits ready
on all occasions."

Benjamin Franklin, Inventor of the lightning rod


"To play chess is like looking over a boundless ocean. To play
checkers is like looking in a well of unfathomable deepness"

Marion F. Tinsley (1980), Three Move Draughts World Champion


Chess Buff: "We can't analyse chess: it's too deep; we just play it."
Checkerist: "We can analyse checkers: we can't play it."

Anecdote from Derek Oldbury (1978), British Draughts World Champion
