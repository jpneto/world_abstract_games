What you wrote first, sounded a little bit like saying scoring goals
is not the object of soccer.

I think, a game can have either an absolute outcome (mate,
connection, reaching the opposite board edge, etc.) or a relative one
(capturing more stones, making more territory, making more valuable
connections). This, however, has nothing to do with the goal itself,
it's rather an (structural or intrinsic) method to qualify the extent
of a victory / loss. BTW, many "absolute" games also permit to
qualify / measure the extent of a victory e.g. Salta, Sleeping Beauty
Draughts and my connection game =C9coute-moi (these rules omitted in
your book) by introducing a meta-rule about point-scoring. In my
experience it is possible to introduce a point scoring system in all
Draughts variants and probably in most connection games. Although
some games may have a structurally defined absolute outcome, most
players "feel" that they have won /lost by a smaller or larger
margin, so it might be quite "natural" to apply such meta-rules.
