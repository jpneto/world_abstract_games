Some people do feel that players should not use that kind of drawish
strategy when playing games (any game). But if a game is sensible to
that type of problem, I agree with Cam, the rules need more work. It
is easy to blame a player, but it is more productive to fix the game.

In some cases that is easy: in Halma just change the goal a bit
(check Halma's webpage at WAG). Others, like Abalone which tends to
favor a defensive play may be a little harder (it seems that
starting in a different initial setup may solve the problem). But
when a game has an obvious drawish strategy that is not easily
solved (i.e., without changing the game's initial purpose), to me,
it is the same as having an obvious winning strategy. In other
words, it is a bad game.
