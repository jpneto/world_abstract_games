Have you read "Maelzel's Chess Player"? It' essentially Poe's take on
game AI. He places a chess playing automaton in a class far above
Babbage's calculating machine since the latter can make decisions based
upon the possibilites it encounters as opposed to following a fixed
sequence of calculation such as that required to produce astronomical and
navigation tables. Surprisingly, as sophisticated as Poe was, he was
fooled into believing the Turk was truly an automaton.

Hi,

I recently read "The Murders in the Rue Morgue" by Edgar Alan Poe and
was amazed that he begins the story with a bit of combinatorial game
analysis. He writes: "In this latter [Chess], where the pieces have
different and bizarre motions, with various and variable values, what
is only complex is mistaken (a not unusual error) for what is
profound."

Poe goes on to say: "In draughts, on the contrary, where the moves
are unique and have but little variation, the probabilities of
inadvertence are diminished, and the mere attention being left
comparatively unemployed, what advantages are obtained by either
party are obtained by superior acumen."

In other words, he's saying that Draughts has greater clarity than
Chess, allowing players to concentrate more on strategic analysis
than on the trivial bookkeeping of working out which moves are legal
or not. This story was written in 1841(!!!) so the concept of clarity
has been around for at least 167 years, even if it has only recently
been given a name.

Later in the story Poe writes: "Truth is not always in a well. In
fact, as regards the more important knowledge, I do believe that she
is invariably superficial. The depth lies in the valleys where we
seek her, and not upon the mountain-tops where she is found." With a
little imagination this can be extrapolated to refer to depth in
games; the good moves all stare us in the face, but it is the amount
of effort required to work out which is the best move (i.e. the
amount of time spent exploring the valleys of suboptimal play) that
indicates the depth of a game.
