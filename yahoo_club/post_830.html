> What is a Stacking Game?

> There are many games in which pieces are put on each other or
> which have pieces on the same board position. [...] stacking isn't
> an essential feature of Draughts. "Kings" should be called "pseudo-
> stacks". They pretend to be stacks, but in fact they are not.

I agree. There is a difference between games that can be played with
stackable material and true stack games. Draughts is one example of
the first case and there are many others, like Ralf said.

>You can play every Chess variant including FIDE Chess just by
>ascribing each piece a stack of a defined height, thus turning
>Chess into a "stacking game".

True. But the main argument would be that these 'stacks' never
change their components *or* their size. They are totally fixed.

>There are many pseudo-stacking games, e.g. all Mancala variants
>(except Rondell), Backgammon, but also Abstract Chess (Joao Neto),
>UnStack (Joao Neto) and Tower of Power (Mike Baldwin). In all these
>games it doesn't matter whether the pieces are stacked or put side
>by side in an arbitrary way on the same board square.

Well, I also agree. But aren't you restricting a bit too much your
definition? Backgammon, AbChess and Tower of Power use stacks in a
different way than Draughts. Stacks on those games do change sizes
(even if they are homogenous in its composition).

We can use the reverse argument. Any game that uses stacks can be
transformed into a non-stack game. AbChess can be played with 3 or 4
Chess sets. Backgammon can be played with 30 different pieces. But
also Byte can be translated into a game with a couple of sets of
2^8=3D256 different pieces. Of course, this translation isn't
practical but that is not the point.

The point is that some games have fixed pieces and others do not.
From these, some use homogenous stones and for others the stack's
structure is relevant to its definition (this one would be Ralf's
stacking games definition).

So I would guess we are talking about three different sets of games:
* Non-stack games
* Homogenous stack games
* Heterogeneous stack games
