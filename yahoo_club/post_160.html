Well, even if the almost tie can get you most points, I don't
think it would be used, for two reasons:

(1) Except for the perfect player who would perfectly knows what is
going on, a winning player would not start to "loose a bit", because
it would risk his own victory, and,

(2) The loosing player would not go with that. After the future
defeat is seen, the looser would try to loose as badly as he can
(considering that the game is not a zero-sum game, and if it is, this
meta-rule makes no sense). Imagine Scrabble, the looser would start
to make as few points as he can, trapping himself with lots of
unusable letters.

Both players would play normally, until the victory-defeat horizon is
seen. After that, and taking these two points, the behaviour of the
loosing player would fasten his own end, a kind of twisted min-max
