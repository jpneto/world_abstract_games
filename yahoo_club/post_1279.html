You can avoid losing by splitting one of your stacks into two of your
own stacks. Your opponent can't capture two of your stacks in one
turn. The only way you can't split one of your stacks into two of
your own stacks is when your last remaining stack has only one of your
checkers in it. In test playing, it seemed fairly easy to avoid that
situation.
Splitting stacks this way is clearly not sustainable unless you also
recapture a mixed stack with your split stack. I agree with you that
death stacks does not clearly progress toward an endgame, but that doesn't
necessarily make it a fatally flawed game. Consider Shogi, where captured
pieces are continuously reintroduced into play.
