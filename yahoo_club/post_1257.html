Anyway, I did see Sagme's "diary", and left a comment there on this,
but it was apparently the wrong place. Following is the relevant
portion of my question on game/chess boards: "what I've been thinking
about, in terms of chess variants, is the effect of board geometry.
I've come to the tentative conclusion that at least some boards are
mutators, especially non-two-dimensional boards. Play chess on a 1-D,
3-D, 4-D... board, and you've substantially changed the game, without
necessarily changing any other rules."

Further thinking has convinced me 2D boards are just as much mutators;
put orthochess in the middle of a 10x10, and now the rooks can step
out 1 square to the side and zip along an edge to the opponent's back
row, where they're promptly taken by an unmoved knight; move the
knight and you lose the defense. A rook can drop back one to guard,
but then the opponent's rook can line up on the piece rank. This is
clearly a substantial change.

Does this concept of boards as mutators make sense?
