There are a few mistakes in the description of Dvonn on
http://www.di.fc.ul.pt/~jpn/gv/dvonn.htm

First of all, the game has just two phases. What Joao calls Phase 1
and 2, is actually (logically and officially) one phase which
consists of putting pieces on the board, one by one, first the Dvonn
pieces, then the other pieces.


Joao Neto writes:

"After each move, any stack not in contact with a DVONN piece is
captured by the player that made the move. A stack is in contact if
it has a DVONN stone, or is adjacent to another stack in contact."

The second sentence is wrong. A stack is in contact if it has a Dvonn
piece, or if it is connected with a Dvonn piece (or a stack which
contains a Dvonn piece) through an unbroken chain of pieces.

He also writes:

"GOAL - When - in the board - are only stacks with at least one DVONN
stone, or both players pass, wins the one with more prisoners (of
either color)."

This is wrong too. The game simply ends when both players can no
longer move. But, almost always, there are still stacks which contain
no Dvonn stone at the end of game. Also, there are situations when
only stacks with Dvonn stones remain, but the game is not yet
finished. The player wins who controls more pieces. You control all
stacks which have your headstones on top. For that reason, prisoners
(no matter which color; even inncluding Dvonn stones) as well as
headstones (the pieces on top of stacks) are added together (not only
the prisoners are counted).
