Desertion is a fascinating modern Draughts variant invented by
Professor Dr. Trevor Truran, an Englishman born in 1942 during an air
raid. (that's about all I know about him)

The game can be played on a board with 8x10 checkered squares
(actually it is played on a board with intersections and lines). The
double corner must be on the right side of each player. Each player
has 12 pieces, which are initially filling the first three rows of
each player.

The men move diagonally forwards as in English Draughts.
They capture exactly in the same way as in English Draughts.
Capturing is compulsory. I suggest to play without huffing. (there
are special rules, but they are childish and expert players don't use
them anymore in Draughts). However, pieces which you have captured
are not removed from the board but replaced by pieces of your own
color.

If you reach the last row of the board, you take your piece off the
board. In the next move you must put this piece on the board again,
on any unoccupied square of your first row. If you can't do that
because all squares are occupied then do another move.

Putting your piece back on the board takes precedence even over
capturing. It counts as a full move.

Draws are impossible. The player who moves last wins. A player who
can't move loses.

The game is reminiscent of Gorgon (which should be played with
compulsory capturing in spite of the inventor's opinion that you
should not)

There are only very few Draughts variants which cannot be drawn:

Sleeping Beauty Draughts (to be described in AG13), Kavkaz Dama,
Gorgon and Desertion.
