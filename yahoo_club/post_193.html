I believe that TruthForum is attempting to forge the term 'abstract'
into the term 'concrete'. By consistantly insisting that abstract
games conform to pre-defined conditions, this will stifle creativity
and eventually lead to stagnation.

There are many developers who wish that their games will be
recognized as the epitome of its genre. Often they attempt to
achieve this by completely eliminating others from such competition.
The easy process is to define them out of the running.

---------------------------------

By the way, Chinese Checkers is the common name for the game and is
easily recognized by most players. It is neither derogatory to those
of Sino-descent nor is an inappropriate reference for the game.
TruthForum should spend more effort defending his original premise,
rather than nit-picking others. Next, he'll be asking that we define
the meaning of the word "is".
