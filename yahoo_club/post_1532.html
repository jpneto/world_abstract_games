New Game -Tyr

Author: hartunga66

Date: 2009-04-18 21:05:10

Post ID: 1532

Tyr grew from my dissatisfaction with Hasami-Shogi. The "5 in-a-row" version tends to be too short. And the "War" version tends to be too long. So I sat down and tried to see if I could come up with some tweaks to the game that would allow for a more happy medium.

The biggest problem with the "5 in-a-row" version is that it is much too easy for a player to build a line on their side of the board. I moved up two rows the area where a line of 5 would count. This would force the player to build a line within the five rows on their opponents side of the board. It made the game a little bit more challenging, but I still was not satisfied.

I then turned to the "War" version. I knew I could make the game more dynamic if I gave a few pieces the power to move and capture diagonally. In exchange these new "royal" pieces would allow for a secondary objective. The player could win either by eliminating most of a large number of plain pieces or by eliminating all of a small number of special pieces. This did improve gameplay, but again I wasn't satisfied.

I was running into the same issues with this new version as I was with the classic version. Most games of elimination, such as Chess or Checkers, only require two pieces to complete a capture - the attacking piece and the captured piece. However, Hasami-Shogi is a game of coordiantion, so three pieces are required for a capture. By mid-game most of the pieces have become spread all over the board. So to arrange three pieces together to make a capture becomes a long and tedious process. I needed a reason to give the players to risk bunching up their pieces, thus making captures easier.

That's when I decided to combine my modified "5 in-a-row" version with my new "War" variant. I think this makes for a richer game while still retaining the essence of the classic.
