When I bought the book several years ago, it made me very happy.
However, after I checked the rules in many primary sources (the
ethnological literature) I became increasingly dissatisfied, because
probably more than half of the rules are explained incorrectly.
Moreover, Russ does not mention a single modern Mancala variant in
his "complete" games book. You'll find a collection of 35 modern
Mancala variants at

http://games.groups.yahoo.com/group/mancalagames/links/Modern_Mancala_
001076091057/


There must be more than 600 Mancala variants and I know the rules of
at least half of them.

Kalah is really an invented variant and was created by William J.
Champion (USA)in 1958. It is one of the few solved Mancala variants,
besides Dakon, MiniMancala, and Oware.
