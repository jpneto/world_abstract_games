Regarding Draws

Author: whyld2000

Date: 2005-01-13 10:41:00

Post ID: 688

Hello, I'm new to this group. There's something I've been wanting
to get off my chest regarding strategy games, and I thought this
would be as good a place as any.

First, a little background. I have a friend who is a big Star Trek
fan. The other day we were discussing an episode of "The Next
Generation" that focuses on a strategy game. In it, a grandmaster
of the game is visiting and the android, Data, challenges him to a
match. Data, who is in effect a living computer, is surprised when
the grandmaster manages to beat him. Data then challenges him a
second time. After playing for a bit, the grandmaster becomes
increasingly frustrated and ends up leaving in a huff. Data then
reveals his "winning strategy." Instead of playing to win, he has
deliberately set off to force the game into a draw. My friend said
next time we played a game, he wanted to try that strategy out on me.

But let's think about this for a bit. This kind of play reminds me
of an old slapstick routine. A little guy enters a boxing match
against a big guy. When the bell rings, instead of exchanging
punches, the little guy runs away and the big guy chases after him.
Rather than a fight, the two run in circles around the boxing ring.
Now, from a strictly technical standpoint, the little guy has
achieved the same thing that Data did, forcing the game into a draw
by deliberately setting off to do so. But in reality the whole
situation is absurd, because no one would ever hold a match like
this.

The essence of gaming is competition. When players enter into a
match, there is an implicit agreement that all sides are playing to
win to the best of their abilities. If, instead, there was a player
who deliberately set off to force the game into a draw they would be
failing to perform their role and degrading the game for the other
player. They would be going against the very spirit and nature of
gaming itself.

Let's face it, if you set down to play a game of Halma with someone,
and all they did when it was their turn was to shift the exact same
piece back and forth, you would get up and leave in disgust. Not
because of any brilliant strategy on their part. Not because the
other player is insightfully exploiting an inherent flaw in the
game. But for the simple fact that the guy is being a jerk.
