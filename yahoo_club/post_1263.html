Well, not really. There *are* a lot to choose from, aren't there! :-D
I'd start off slow; pick a dozen or so, and try to make them totally
independent, not interacting with each other in bizarre ways. The
advantage to this is that it's doable, and can easily be made at least
playtestable. Real easy would be to let each player pick 4 of 12 total
mutators.
You'd want them very general. For example, Fluid subsumes Amazonia
with the right rules subset, and is more general - suppose there are
no queens in the game?
Another more general way that acts more like your enchantment would be
Add A [short range] Move: add to any piece the move of a wazir, or
ferz, or dababbah, or alfil, or knight.
Fluid's opposite is Frozen [obvious how it works], Add A Move's is
Subtract A Move. If you wanted to combine them, you could call them
Stacking? and Change A Move, giving 2 very, very general mutators that
don't. really.. interact..., or do they?
At this level of generality, you can at least add one knight to and
stack one knight with any friendly piece, including a knight. This has
to give at least a 3-step knightrider, something not found in either
Fluid or Add A [Knight] Move alone.
Convert/Drop is a nice mutator that is general and doesn't interact,
as far as I can see - but we're only up to 3 mutators now.
Grow is 4, another non-interacting mutator.

We've hit our first crisis here. Why? Because these are 4 easy and
decent mutators, and I'd like to use them, but I gotta ask just how do
we apply them? Can I grow the board every turn? Or only once? If, when
I grow the board, I have used up that mutator for me, can I pick it
for all 4 of my mutator picks, and grow the board 4 times? Does my
picking it keep my opponent from picking it, or not? How about I can
use it any and every turn I want? Then what happens to knightriders? A
thorny problem that could grow a Rose [Rose: a circular knightrider].

Enough for this post.
