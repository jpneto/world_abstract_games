I'm sorry if it now
looks like a big restriction. It was really
too much
different from Go. You could hardly capture any chain
-
not to mention isolated stones - because your
opponent would
increase some end of it before getting
captured. You would then
need more moves than her to
threaten the chain again. I'm afraid
the game would
turn into an algebra of unlimited increases.
Maybe
territories would only exist through some mathematical
analysis.
This all reminds me too much of financial
theories.

We could allow increases but forbid any fraction
bigger than 1. I
don't like it because fractions
would still only exist through
their potentiality to
become whole. It keeps an aftertaste of the
previous
problem. Instead of that I would like fractions
to
behave much like whole stones at their level and to be
more
fragile than whole stones.
