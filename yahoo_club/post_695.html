I would think then that is the complexity, and ease of the draw that
is the factor. In tic-tac-toe for example, the human mind can
easily draw that game indefinitely, kinda like in the
movie 'Wargames'. However in Chess, even the highest level
computers yet, and humans playing for a draw can not sucessfully
achieve it in every case. I don't know the game Halma, but I would
agree if all it takes is to move a piece back and forth to draw,
that there is a problem with the rules. If there was a tournament
against a grand master Halma player, I could get a 1/2 point, and
yet never have played the game. However in chess if all you did was
move your knight around, you'd be busted as soon as you got to the
middle game. Maybe part of the rules for Halma should be to make it
a loss to move a piece back and forth 3 times. (sorta similiar to if
both players had done so in chess to make it a draw).
