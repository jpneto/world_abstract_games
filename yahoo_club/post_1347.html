To clarify my earlier point, I don't see computer solution spoiling the

game for human competitive play. In fact, it could even enhance human

play. It may lead to useful insights about the game, but those insights

are available to all human players. For instance, now that C4 is solved,

it may be easier to compile an opening book for the game, and if anything,

that would have the effect of increasing the sophistication of human game

play. I believe that's what is occurring now with Checkers.
