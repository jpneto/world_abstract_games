Unless there is a "metagame" tournament structure, that directions to
what end players are meant to go, you are going to get a wide range of
unexpected approaches towards play. Some players may play to form
patterns in their games with the pieces, others to grief others, and
others to just do randomly to see what happens. Maybe they player you
played Mark was expecting you to hit him with a witty, Simon Cowell
style knockdown insult for playing stupid, so they can get a laugh
from it.

What could get people to play more seriously is "gamer points" like
they have on the XBox 360, where players get rewarded for playing
correctly.
