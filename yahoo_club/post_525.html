My guess would be that the reasons for color choices have
historically had more to do with pigment technology available at the
time than with symbolism attached to colors by different cultures. In
fact, the symbolism attached to colors often derived from the
convenience of coloring something: as for example, the expense of
purple dye made purple significant of nobility; in Renaissance
Europe, black cloth was the most expensive and was worn by royalty,
etc.

Perhaps something similar applied to the colors used for game pieces.
However, remember that I'm factically analphabetic, so it could have
been something else entirely.
