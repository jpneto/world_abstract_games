This may seem trite, but I think one of the key features of whatever
variant arises to unstick Chess is that it will be a "usual-equipment"
variant. That way, people can try it out, toy with it, and settle on
the evolved rules gradually, and without commitment.

I personally have a fondness for Ralph Betza's Chess with Different
Armies, about which there was considerable discussion on
chessvariants.org a few years ago. Ralph defined four specific
armies, but many people came up with experimenal armies, and trying
them out against one another was a lot of fun. If such
experimentation propagated across the general chess-playing
population, change might be swift.
