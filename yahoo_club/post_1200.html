I received this from Joe Joyce from the Chess variants website
regarding this:
Well, there's a website that will give you random positions here:
http://www.chessgames.com/perl/fischerandom
But if you want to do it manually, there are controversies. Following
are 2 URLs, one for FRC, and one for the FRC comments:
http://www.chessvariants.org/diffsetup.dir/fischer.html
http://www.chessvariants.org/index/listcomments.php?itemid=3DFischerRandomChe

To do a free manual setup, you can let one player place the 1st B, the
other the 2nd B on a different color square, first place 1 R, 2nd the
other R, 1st the K, 2nd the Q, and the 2 Ns are forced. This is just my
own sense of what would work more or less okay from reading about it.
I'm no expert, I'm 2-1 in FRC, with computer-generated positions, so
that's no experience. This would not give you an even chance for all
960 positions, I "strongly suspect", because there's psychology in
the placement of the pieces when allowing the players to choose the
positions of the pieces.
