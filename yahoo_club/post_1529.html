New Game - Harlequin Checkers

Author: hartunga66

Date: 2009-04-04 20:28:02

Post ID: 1529

In Harlequin Checkers pieces move diagonally but capture orthogonally. This is an attempt to create the "best of both worlds" - the forced progression of the diagonal game combined with the use of the full board and range of captures of the orthogonal game.

Draws are very rare and can only occur in endgame situations involing a lone king vs. 2 kings that are both on the same diagonal plane (e.g. the one side's 2 kings are both on black spaces). In endgames where one side's pieces all occupy the same diagonal plane then 3 kings are the minimum to capture an enemy lone king. Otherwise only 2 kings are needed if they each are on a seperate diagonal plane to capture a lone enemy king.

Because pieces don't capture in the same direction they move, this creates unique situations not seen in other forms of checkers, such as a single piece blockading a corner piece.

The nature of play remains consistent throughout. The diagonal plane is used strictly for movement; the orthogonal plane strictly for captures. This is different than Croda which is essentially orthogonal checkers with a temporary slight diagonal movement forced upon the men. Or Armenian Checkers which mixes diagonal movement with orthoganal movement & capture. Armenian Checkers suffers from offering the player too many possibilities and not enough limitations.
