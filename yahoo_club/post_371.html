If you find the new improved price tag for the new improved
Zillions a bit too steep, there is also a free Twixt jar file
from http://www.twixt.de
It's entirely in German, link removal is not supported,
neither is the pie rule, and you have to download Sun's JRE
(also free) to use it, but it does actually play the game,
albeit poorly. It has Internet connection capability,
although I haven't tested that feature out yet.

Another free way to play Twixt or Y is with AIM's image transfer
and a graphics program. It's awkward to say the least, but a
wide variety of games could be played this way.
The idea is, you send an image
of the board back and forth, using the graphics program
to make moves. At least, in Y, pieces are added to the
board but are never removed. So that slightly
reduces the hassle. Here is where you can get a blank board:
http://www.flash.net/~markthom/html/the_game_of_y.html

We might also try some weird Hex variant. Some blank
board images for that are available here:
http://www.geocities.com/twixtplayer/pex1.html
