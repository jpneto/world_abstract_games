Dvonn is mentioned, in fact (but not given in your "List of Games").

Let me compare it to Gonnect which you've included as a connection
game.

Dvonn is often won by an experienced player who plays against a
beginner by cutting off his opponent's stones, so "connection" can be
a dominating / decisive theme in Dvonn. Limiting or killing your
opponent's pieces becomes the goal only when Dvonn is played by
advanced players (and even they would rather say, that Dvonn is about
mobility).

Gonnect is a connection game in the "race stage" (hot), and a
territorial game in the "territory-filling stage" (cold). Experienced
players should usually survive the hot stage, so that Gonnect mutates
to a territorial game, IF played by two advanced players, but could
often remain a connection game, IF at least one player is a beginner.

Whether a game belongs to a certain class or not might in some games
also depend on the strength of those playing it.
