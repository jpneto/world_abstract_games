What I meant by my comment was, since the equipment
provided with Tile Chess is simply six complete Chess
sets, each printed on tiles of a different color, if
players didn't want to play your game sitting in front
of a computer, they could, instead, draw random pairs
of pieces from six differently colored Chess sets
(such as those from Tile Chess) and play your game on
a standard Chess board. Piece ownership could, as in
Shogi, be indicated by the tile orientation instead of
color.

So my comment had nothing to do with the rules to Tile
Chess, just that you could use the Tile Chess
equipment to play your game sans computer. I think
you'll find that many people prefer to play games with
physical pieces rather than on a computer. I know that
I do, because it hurts my eyes to stare at screens for
long periods of time.
