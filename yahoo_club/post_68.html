From a Gonnect game I'm playing with Joao, I

thought of a twirling way to move pieces over a

board. On the boards below, the squares marked
with
a '-' shows the possible destinations of
hite's
stone 'o'. Other stones on the same row or
column
play the role of pivots. They can be white
or
black, it doesn't matter. What matters is the

distance from the moving stone.

. . - . . ||| . . .
. . ||| o . . x .
. . . . . ||| . . - . . ||| .
. . . .
o . x . - ||| . o x - . ||| . . . .
.
. . . . . ||| . . - . . ||| . . . - .
. . - . .
||| . . . . . ||| . . . . .

There must be no
enemy on the diagonal path(s),
but maybe we may
allow friendly stones. Maybe
not. On the other hand
there can be any stones
between the moving stone
and the pivot, it
doesn't matter. Multiple twirls
using different
consecutive pivots are possible in
one move:

. . . . . . . .
. . . . . . .
3
. . . . 2 . . .
. . . 1 x 2 . .
. . . . 2 . .
x
. 1 . x . 1 . .
. . . . . . . .
. . . o . . .
3

But now I don't know what would be an original

objective. There could be captures, either on an

occupied destination square or, better, by a 360

degree twirl around an enemy. Well, as a start we

can have a Checker-like game with the objective

if capturing as many opposing stones as possible

(though they may not all be capturable in the
end).
But it would be nice to find something
else.

Hey, what if the winner was the first player who

manages to get a stone to twirl 360 degrees
around
itself? I mean, once the 'o' above can
reach the '3'
in the bottom, we might consider it
can uses
itself still on the origin square as a
pivot, a la
quantum interference! It wouldn't
result in a win
here because a full 360 degrees
twirl is not
possible due to the board edge.
