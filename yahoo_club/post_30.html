Hi

Your game rule basics seem very
interesting and simple. However there are some
questions:

a) Isolated stones cannot be captured! You already
said that is to give some protection to them, but I
wonder if both players will not place stone after stone
isolated from each other, while that is possible. In that
sense, a 19x19 board seems to big. Perhaps a 13x13 or
even a 9x9 if the game is deep enough. However, I
would say to drop this restriction.

b) Perhaps
the move part should not be restricted to any other
rule. A player just moves one stone if he thinks it's
good to do so. That would eliminate all recursive
problems that Claude mentioned. The double move (move and
deploy) seems good in order to get a faster
game.

c) why restrict the range of the next piece? Why not
let players use Go openings to deploy their armies?
It seems more versatile that way.

d) "The two
sides get one chance in the game to switch
sides."

This is my main objection. You should restrict this
rule! Just give a 1-move or 3-move equalizer to White,
or state "The two sides get one chance in the game
to switch sides, ***while there are no captures
made*** ", but even that seems too long-term. It destroys
any willingness to create winning
strategies.

I hope I'm not intruding too much on your game.
Just trying to spot and clean some strange exceptions
:-) But "The Way" feels like a very nice game. After
the rules are 'settled', I can play a game with you,
if you want!

Cheers,

Joao Pedro Neto
