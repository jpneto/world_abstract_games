The question just made me think of a non-random version of Chess960,
(which I suppose could be called Thompson Nonrandom Chess).

Player 1 takes the list of 960 possible openings and divides it into
two lists of (perhaps) 480 openings each. Player 2 selects one of the
lists, and divides it into two lists of 240 openings each. Then Player
1 selects one list and divides it into two of 120 each. Then Player 2
selects and reduces to 60, Player 1 to 30, Player 2 to 15, then Player
1 separates into 3 lists of 5 each, Player 2 selects one of those
lists, and Player 1 finally chooses the opening from the list. Then
Player 2 begins playing as White.

Alternatively, you could dispense with the requirement to use lists of
equal size, and just reduce until a single opening setup is chosen.

This procedure is something like the opening maneuvers in The Game, as
described in a fantasy series by Piers Anthony.
