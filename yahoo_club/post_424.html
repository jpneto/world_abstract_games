Interesting! Paul van Wamelen and Cameron Browne found a position (a
variant line in one of their games) where neither player could play
either color in either of two different cells, yet each player
appeared (as it stood) to need one of those two cells in order to
win. If the line were played out, developments elsewhere on the board
might have changed the balance, so evaluating the game was NOT as
simple (Paul explained to me) as counting the remaining non-forbidden
moves and awarding victory to the player of the right parity.
