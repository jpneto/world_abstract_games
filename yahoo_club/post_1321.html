You know, initially I suspected that was the case. I feared that a
few individuals were f'ing with me by pretending to be unbelievably
stupid. Then at some point I was actually hoping they were goofing on
me, because the only alternative explanation is pretty bleak.

There was a scene in Jacob's Ladder, I believe, where a guy was in a
cell in some institution. He was banging his head over and over again
against the window in the door and there was this blood spot on the
glass. I heard later that this was actual footage of some
institution. Not to make fun of the handicapped, but this is what
some of these players remind me of.

Now I've come to the inescapable conclusion that hoards of mindless
people are drawn to online abstract games. I've seen too many people
playing mindlessly, not just against me but against each other. Like
I said earlier, one lady played Dipole 150 times with virtually no
understanding of the rules, except perhaps that bigger stacks capture
smaller stacks, and she actually won 30 percent of her games. If you
can win 30 percent of your games not knowing the rules, that's a clue.
That tells you something about the population.

It's disheartening. Many of them are friendly people who genuinely
enjoy what they're doing, but it starts to grate on you. You play
game after game with them, and you can see there's no inclination to
actually learn the rules. Playing against zombies, even if they're
happy zombies, is really aggravating. I hate to be snotty with them
but I can't go along with that program. I have to tell them they need
to learn the rules first before we can play again. Then later, true
to their nature, they challenge me again not having learned the rules.
