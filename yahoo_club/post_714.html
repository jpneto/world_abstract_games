A couple of people have questioned the importance of "connective
play" in determining whether a game is a connection game. This is a
much more elusive quality to pin down than "connective goal", but I
believe that it sheds a great deal of light on the basic nature of
some games. Star is a prime example oof thisa much more concrete
example.

Anyone familiar with Star will probably have little hesitation in
stating that it is clearly a connection game, yet the goal of the
game is to score points; the goal is a by-product of connection, not
a specific connection itself.

This is what is meant by connection being either a means or an end
to a game (p69). In this case it is the means (connective play).

Go is a weaker example. Connection is the means (surrounding pieces
and enclosing territory) but not exactly the end of the game. Again,
the goal is a by-product of connection.
