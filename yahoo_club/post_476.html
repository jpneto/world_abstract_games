Jade development update

Author: markthomps

Date: 2003-09-04 02:23:33

Post ID: 476

The latest development in the rules of Jade, which
I described in some posts awhile back, is the
realization that there's a very simple tweak that
appears to defeat the symmetry strategy (on odd-order
boards) AND provide an adjustable way of balancing
the game.

The game as described previously is played on a
"square" grid of hexagons, similar to Hex. (In fact,
I think of Jade as a Hex variant.) But the two players,
Cross and Parallel, have different objects, and
that game seems to tilt fairly strongly toward Cross.

What I realized in the last month or so is that by changing the board
to a more general "rectangle", for example a 9x11 or an 11x13 grid,
Cross's advantage will clearly be diminished if not completely
erased. This is clear when you consider the "degenerate case" of a
board only 1 cell thick: clearly Parallel wins immediately. Even a
board just a few cells thick will obviously be Parallel's in a very
few moves. Therefore, since we know (or believe, based on experience)
that Cross has the advantage on a "square" board, there should be
some intermediate shape where the imbalance is minimized.

I'm hopeful that when we find the ideal size and shape of board it
will prove to be balanced enough for most purposes, though experts
and other enthusiasts would probably still want to play with some
form of swap rule.

Sometime in the next couple of months I hope to begin a tournament to
test some particular size and shape of board. The players will divide
into round-robins and every pairing will play two games, reversing
their roles. In the end, besides having a winner, we'll have records
of how each person fared both as Cross and as Parallel. If the size
and shape of board was "balanced," then there should be about the
same number of people who did better as Parallel as people who did
better as Cross. With a succession of such experiments, we should be
able to find an ideal board. Plus, it should be a lot of fun.

When the tournament details are settled I'll announce it here, in the
hope that people will join it.
