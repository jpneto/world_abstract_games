By the way, anyone sufficiently close to Ann Arbor, Michigan might be
interested in attending the U-Con this November 17-19. Most of the
U-Con is RPG's, but on Saturday, Clark Rodeffer is sponsoring his
annual "Abstract Games Tournament", where some of us compete in
various games chosen mostly at random. I think there have been from
7-10 players in recent years. Last year I played Emergo, Yinsh, *Star,
Twixt, Byte, Hexmeister, Gyges, and Domina. Other players played
different games.

This year I'm also going to sponsor on Saturday, for the first time, a
tournament in Chess Variants. I plan to model it on the AGT, and
expect to have homemade equipment available for Quickchess, Tori
Shogi, Alapo, Flip Shogi, Whale Shogi, Caissa, Congo, Alice Chess, and
Fergus Duniho's Extra Move Chess. The selection was intended to be of
several CV's that look promising and quick-playing, so people can get
as much variety as possible in the short time available.
