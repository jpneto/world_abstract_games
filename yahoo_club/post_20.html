Hello there. I would love to see simple strategic
board games
like Go, Awele, etc. with a smooth time
or space instead of
finite discrete ones. Any
idea?

About Go, its discrete space could be made smooth as
follows:

- Play with closed discs about twice as large as
the usual
stones. Allow them to overlap. The parts
of discs getting covered
don't exist anymore. The
center of the disc that is going to be
played must
lay inside or at the frontier of an empty area.
(An
idea of Lionel Bonnetier, if I remember well). A disc
lives until
the connected component of friendly
discs it belongs to has got
a frontier of length 0
in contact with free areas.

- Or play with
squares or any fixed other shapes without allowing
any
overlap. A group is dead if no room enough is left to
add
another piece touching it.

- Or let the players
add a new piece with the shape they want at
every
move, provided it always has the same area. A group
lives
under the condition of either variant above. I guess
some other
topological restrictions are needed. For
example it should have
no hole inside and should have
some strictly positive area
everywhere. I don't
know whether it also needs to be convex
and
connected.

My dream is to have an oracle showing the best
strategy for this
last class. It must draw beautiful
pictures.

Claude Chaunier
