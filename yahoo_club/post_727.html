Given that a game of Hex:

. . . V .
H H H V .
. V V V .
. V H H H
. V . . .

can be converted into a game of Y by padding the board and filling
the new areas with pieces:

v
v v
v v v
v v v v
. . . V .
H H H V . h
. V V V . h h
. V H H H h h h
. V . . . h h h h

then it follows that the "N-1" Y reduction rule can be used to
determine whether a player has won a game of Hex or not. The actual
reduction for this sample game of Hex-converted-to-Y follows:

N=9 V
V V
V V V
V V V V
. . . V .
H H H V . H
. V V V . H H
. V H H H H H H
. V . . . H H H H

V V V
. . V V
H H . V .
. V V V . H
. V H H H H H
. V . . H H H H

N=7 V

. V V V .
. V H H H H
. V . H H H H

. V H H H H
