The name of that Chinese game is Tezhi Luzhanqi, or Chinese Army
Chess.

You can see an incomplete article about it at:

http://chessvariants.com/oriental.dir/tezhi.html

I've always seen the use of random generators as a challenge,
especially between very strong players. Of course, I do agree that
there does reach a "point of saturation" when random generators
dominate the game. So, random generators should not dictate the
entire play of an abstract game.

Another example of an abstract game which utilizes random generators
is a game called "Mozaic". This was featured on page 26 of Abstract
Games Magazine #8. The players blind-draw tokens and play
accordingly. This is a really cool game.
