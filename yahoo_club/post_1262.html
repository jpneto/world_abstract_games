I will do a chop here, because people can read the other posts.

I personally think that that having a near infinite possible game
conditions break out at start is a strength of using them. However,
there is distinct risks of things breaking down. I believe there very
much would need to be handled the way Magic:The Gathering is handled
and the mutators are playtested together.

I also believe that the following should be done with them:
1. All potential mutators that can come into play are exposed before
the game starts. This is not Magic where you suddenly break out
something you don't expect.
2. Players alternate turns picking which mutator they want to possible
use during the game.
3. Players are each given a certain amount they can bring into play.
These mutators will have a value for them potentially.
4. There can be a nullify mutator which causes a mutator in play to go
out of play.
5. A mutator acts as a global enchantment in Magic and affects both
players equally.
6. For balance purposes, mutators are determined to be brought into
play before or after a player's turn, depending on when it is brought
it aids their opponent more. If a mutator causes an opponent to get
an edge after your turn, that is when it comes into effect. If it is
advantageous to them for it to be played before you do your turn, that
is when it comes into effect.
7. Mutators are by class, or type, and there can be restrictions on
how pieces move.
8. To start out, a mutator could merely be rules that are from other
games. If you were doing chess, for example, you can have a Shogi
mutator which allows you to cause an enemy piece to convert to your
side. You can also have an Amazonia power which turns a queen into an
Amazon.

Actually what was said about mutators ended up being changed a bit by
me to be something that would be like an enchantment in Magic, rather
than a very generic rule.
