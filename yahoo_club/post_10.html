Universe is an old abstract game for 2-4 players.
It was marketed by Parker Brothers around the time
that "2001: A Space Odyssey" first came out, which was
1969 I think. The box features publicity stills from
the movie, including a shot where Frank the astronaut
is playing something that looks like Universe with
Hal, which you won't see if you watch an "edited for
TV" version, or if you blink at the wrong
moment.

Anyway, here are the rules for the 2-player version: the
board is a 10x10 grid of squares. Each player has a
collection of 12 pentomino shapes. If you've played Tetris,
you know what a tetromino is. A pentomino is 5
squares joined flush edge to edge. There are 12 pentomino
shapes, not counting rotations or reflections. The board
is empty to start. Players alternate placing one
piece on the board, aligned with the grid, so that 5
previously vacant squares are now filled. YOU MAY NOT "fence
off" any region of 1 to 4 squares with your move. For
example, If White starts by placing the + shaped pentomino
centered
on E5, the 2nd player may not place his +
shape
centered on G7, because that would fence off a
region
of 1 square at F6. One consequence of this rule is,
any region of 6 to 9 squares is a "dead zone" no one
may play in. The last player to move wins.

It
is unclear if the first move is an advantage or a
disadvantage, although it is possible to throw away the game on
the first move. If the first move occupies 1 or 2 of
the 4 central cells, the opponent can usually respond
by making the rotationally symmetric move. Once the
central region is closed off, the 2nd player will always
be able to respond symmetrically, and will therefore
win. This is called the "symmetric strategy." The
exception is if the first move uses the U-shaped pentomino,
it may be illegal to respond symmetrically, because
that would fence off two regions of 1 square
each.

So, here's the puzzle: suppose the board were 9x10
instead of 10x10. Now there are just two central cells
instead of four. The first move occupies 1 of the 2
central cells. The 2nd player replies with the symmetric
strategy as long as possible, and if this strategy becomes
impossible, the 2nd player would make the strongest possible
sequence of moves. Nevertheless, the 1st player wins the
game. How?
