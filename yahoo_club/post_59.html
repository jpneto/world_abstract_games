After rereading Epaminondas and thinking more
about it I decided to revise the irreversible gain idea
a bit more. The idea is embodied in the rules
below:

1. Anbody who repeats a turn in a capture sequence
must announce the repetition after the turn. After
which the opponent cannot respond the same way as was
done before to any of the previous incarnation of this
turn in this capture sequence. So within a capture
sequence, the responses to every repetition of a turn must
be different.


2. The person that starts
and ends a capture sequence gets one stone toward the
fullfillment of their contracts. Otherwise no stones is
rewarded.
