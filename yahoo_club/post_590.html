I already tried the game and find it truly amazing.

Even the two-person variant is quite playable. At first I thought the
second player could simply imitate the moves of the first player,
until I realized that the first player would win by 10 points. The
second player changed his tactics and did a move of which he thought
it would loose the game. But the second player won by 20 points!

The four-person variant is even more fun. Probably the two players
which sit across should form a team to prevent unfair coalitions.
There are only very few abstract games for four players which I
enjoy: Schachmati Kreposti, Vierschach, Pair Go, Blokus for two
Teams, and now Swahili.

I am still looking for the rules of "Pass it on".

BTW, everybody should try Dan's Cross-Wari (rules at Zillions). It is
one of the best Mancala variants ever invented.
