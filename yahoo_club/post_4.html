Some years ago, I read an article that said
something like Chess having between 10 and 20 different
playing levels, and Go more than 30.

In the same
article, they defined that a player is above another by
one level, if at every 3 games, he would win 2. So, a
2 level player difference would mean 7 out of 9
games, and so on...

[In general terms, a player N
levels above another would win a fraction equal to
1-(1/2)(2/3)^n]

I wonder if this type of definition is interesting
and it may be found to some set of abstract games,
besides empirical data (like the ELO rating of
FIDE)

I mean, how many levels are between the worst
player (random play?) and perfect play?

Could
this number be found by theoretical analysis at least
for simple games, like Tic-Tac-Toe?
