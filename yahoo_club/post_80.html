I would distinguish two kinds of "solved"
games:

1rst: You know a perfect strategy or even a
complete sequence of moves that guarantee you a draw or a
win.

Examples: Nim, Bridg-It, Outwit, Gomoku
narabe,
Abalone, Wayne Schmittberger's Halma




2nd: You have a theoretical proof that
the game
ends in a draw or a win, but you don't
know
the exact sequence of moves or the strategy

that you would have to play to "implement"

this theory in real life.
These games are still
very playable, there
are even tournaments.


Examples: 9-Men-Morris, Teeko (not clear if this
is
also true for the tournament variant), Hex, Sprouts
(not affirmed)


Sometimes this kind of
proof is called "ultra-weak".

As I am not a
mathematician, I don't care much about the theoretical
"solutions" as long as they don't affect the real play.
