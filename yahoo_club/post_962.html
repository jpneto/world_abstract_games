The pictures are correct, the description is incorrect. And the English
translation is incorrect too. Here is the corrected version:



Two players (White and Black) have armies of 21 checkers, arranged in

all the cells on the first 3 rows of an 7x7 board.



Players decide who starts.



A checker may move without capturing to any of the three
forward-adjacent vacant squares (diagonal or orthogonal).



A checker may capture in any of the eight directions by means of the

short jump (jumping over an immediately adjacent enemy checker, to a

vacant square just beyond it). Multiple captures are allowed.



There is no rule stating that capturing is obligatory or mandatory.



There is no promotion. Checkers reaching the last row can still
capture or be captured, but cannot move otherwise.

The goal of the game is to block opponent's checkers. The player who blocks
his opponent wins. If none of them was able to block his opponent, the
player who captured more pieces wins.

The board can be the size of 9x9 with 27 checkers for each player.

board. Then the checkers are placed in the middle of 4 checkers, i.e.
between the two rows - 7 checkers on the 1st row, 6 checkers between 1st and
2nd row, 7 checkers on the 2nd row, 6 checkers between 2nd and 3rd row, 7

Here then is apparently the first English rendition of the rules to
Kavkaz Checkers, except that I may not have it exactly right. Thanks
Ralph.

Kavkaz Checkers.

Two players (White and Black) have armies of 24 checkers, arranged in
all the cells on the first 3 rows of an 8x8 board. White moves first.

A checker may move without capturing to any of the three
forward-adjacent squares (diagonal or orthogonal).

A checker may capture in any of the eight directions by means of the
short jump (jumping over an immediately adjacent enemy checker, to a
vacant square just beyond it). Multiple captures are allowed.

Capturing is obligatory, and a player must choose a move that captures
the largest possible number of enemy checkers.

There is no promotion. Checkers reaching the last row can still
capture or be captured, but cannot move otherwise.

A player loses when it is his turn and he has no legal move. It
follows that draws are impossible.


This strikes me as a "standard" checkers variant, the one novel
feature (certainly a significant one) being the lack of promotion.
