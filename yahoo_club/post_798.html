Mark Thompson's article 'Defining the Abstract' refers four
qualitative features of abstract games: depth, clarity, drama and
decisiveness.

I would like your opinion to make a list of good and bad games
concerning these features. I would like them to be easy to explain
the rules as possible, and as known as possible.

Good - Go
Bad - Tic Tac Toe

Clarity:
Good - Hex, Checkers
Bad - Othello, Lines of Action

Drama:
Good - Chess
Bad - Hex

Decisiveness:
Good - Gomoku
Bad - Abalone
