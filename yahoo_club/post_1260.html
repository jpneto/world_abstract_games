Wow, that's a tough order, and I'm not well-versed in the area, but
I've had a few thoughts on mutators for tournament play.
First, there has to be a very limited number, though this could easily
go into the hundreds [or thousands!]. Mutators, as I understand them,
seem to asymptotically approach infinity. As interactions get complex,
unlimited mutator lists would lead to chaos and worse, worse being the
inability to define the rules in a consistent manner.
Second, I suggest effectively eliminating mutators of certain "types".
The "switch sides" mutator should not be used, as this would encourage
poor play with the expectation of gaining later. Neither should a
"move opponent's pieces" mutator be used. Without severe restrictions,
these kinds of mutators pretty much have the effect of cheating, it
seems to me. Certainly, they would work against the precept of how
well one plays should determine the outcome. Yes, with them, how one
plays using them would determine the outcome, but they force
suboptimum game play from the initial state, which I think would be
inimical to IAGO's purpose.
Third, how, when, and how long a mutator or several mutators take
effect would need to be strictly determined in advance.
I can see this being doable for a very restricted set of mutators, and
it would be an interesting and somewhat novel approach to most
abstract games, but it would be an amazing series of headaches for the
people figuring it out.
