Don't you think Bombardment is better described as a classic war
game in which nuclear powers face off with ICBMs over an ocean, the
sort of thing the Rand Corporation might have put out as an
application of game theory? To the extent it models the invasion of
Iraq, it models numerous other conflicts equally well. If you want
to see a game invoking imagery of the "war on terror", try Luiz
Carlos Campos' Unconventional Warfare Chess, which I implemented for
the Chess Variant Pages' 84-square contest. It explicitly uses
chemical and biological weapons, tactical nuclear weapons, special
forces, etc. It's even more tasteless than Bombardment, and almost
as tasteless as chess.

Yearout didn't address the board size issue. I wrote a response to
his letter but never sent it, because I wasn't sure of myself. My
opinion, ultimately, is that openings matter but that they are not
as interesting as I would like them to be. 7x7 promises quicker
engagement and the presence of a unique middle row counteracts the
tendency to hang back on one's side of the board, and allows more
potent threats. Or so I hope. I haven't played on that size enough
to be confident in my assessment.
