On Sun, Oct 12, 2003 at 09:08:58PM -0000, markthomps wrote:
> I don't have Blokus yet, but am considering getting it. Can anyone
> tell me whether it can be played as a 2-player game, or do you have
> to have 3 or 4? It looks to me as if all the pieces from just two
> players would fit easily on the 400-square board, which would mean
> that (in principle) you could have draws, if I'm remembering the
> rules correctly. Unless you play a 2-player game on a smaller board,
> perhaps a 12x12?

Mark, all four colours are always used. In a two-player game each
player takes two colours. There is a fixed turn order for colours. I
don't remember the details, but suppose the fixed order is Red,
Yellow, Green, Blue. Player #1 takes Red and Green and Player #2
takes Yellow and Blue. (In a three-player game all three players
stand in for a "phantom" fourth player in turn.)

By the way, the first player has a terrific advantage in Blokus, the
second player somewhat less so. My game group Seattle Cosmic tends to
handicap the first and second players by only letting them play small
pieces (fewer squares) on the first turn. That way the early players
don't gallop to the center ahead of everyone else and dominate the
board unfairly.
