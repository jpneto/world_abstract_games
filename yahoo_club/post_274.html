Hi TruthForum,

Yes, Orbit lends itself pretty well to variants. I've designed 29 myself. Many of them are not very good, but several of them are.

Chinese Orbit (if I've understood the rules correctly) most closely resembles the "orbits capture and prohibit, half-orbits capture and prohibit, quarter-orbits capture and prohibit" variant. There are two differences: 1. The scoring is Chinese-style rather than (almost) Japanese-style. 2. In Chinese Orbit, it is permitted to play inside an opponent's structure if at the same time you are completing your own structure. The second difference is of course more significant.

Hi Steve,

I think, I've now understood your standard rules. Many "non-standard"
variants are possible. I would like to call the following one the
Chinese variant because it bears resemblance to the Weiqi rules of Go:

"Chinese Orbit" is played on a board with 15x15 points.

An "orbit" is a chain of stones of one color (diagonally or
orthogonally adjacent) which is either connected to itself or to one
edge or to two adjacent edges of the board, encircling empty points
and/or opponent's stones.

After an "orbit" is formed any opponent's stones inside of it are
removed.

It is not permitted to play inside an opponent's orbit unless
an "orbit" is formed in the same move.

It is not permitted to repeat a board position in a Ko fight.

"Territory" is counted as follows: your own points inside your orbits
plus the number of your stones on the board at the end of the game.
The sum of all points equals the numbers of board points. "Seki"
(local stalemate) or drawn games are impossible.
