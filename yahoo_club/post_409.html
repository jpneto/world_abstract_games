> if you move the largest gobblet again, will you
not uncover the smaller one beneath ?

Is that a genuine, as opposed to a rhetorical,
question? Anyway I think the answer is yes. I
can't make the link to the pdf work right now,
but I'm certain I remember seeing in the rules
(back when I could get at them) that moving a
gobblet uncovers any smaller ones it has
captured.

As a digression, this introduces the element of
"memory" into the game: you have to remember what's
under the pieces you can see. Technically that
doesn't disqualify it as an abstract game, but I feel that in keeping
with the "spirit" of abstract-gameness, one should be allowed to peek
under the top gobblet(s). It would be even better, IMO, if the
gobblets were topless so one could simply see what pieces they cover.
Similarly, I feel one should be allowed to count stones in a mancala
pit, even if you have to touch them.
