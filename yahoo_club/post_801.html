> I agree that draughts might not be the best example
> for clarity, and hex might work better as a good
> example for decisiveness than bad for drama.
>
> Speaking of drama, all positions in abstract games
> (meaning lacking in both hidden information and
> randomizers) are definitively 'winning', 'losing' or
> 'drawn'. The only sense in which finer-grained ideas
> of a 'weaker' or 'stronger' position are meaningful is
> through failure of the players to completely perceive
> the structure of the game, either through strong depth
> or weak clarity. So I suppose that a game with good
> 'drama' is one where there is an easily-perceived
> indicator that correlates strongly (but not reliably)
> with won positions. Chess is definitely a good
> example for this...'material advantage' can be defined
> and perceived much more easily than whether a position
> is won. Your mileage may vary on what qualities of a
> position are easily perceived, though...I'm unaware of
> any similarly obvious indicators for Hex, but I'm a
> newbie, so maybe I just don't know what they are.
>
> -Andy
