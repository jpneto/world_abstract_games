Oops, you're right! I think, however, I pointed to the right
direction. The goal was to re-arrange the player's pieces on his
first two rows

1. so that the opponent can't capture any of them
2. to do that without losing any pieces
3. to have at least one secure Auxiliary behind the wall (could be
called the "Berlin" wall) which can move back and forth

My "solution" fails in regard to (3)

which means: the player still must re-arrange the Auxiliaries which
stay behind the wall so that at least one of them can move.

An abbreviated solution would be:

Archer: c/d2-c/d3
Aux.: d1-b2
Archer: c/d3-c/d2 (left edge is protected)

Archer: e/f2-e/f3
Aux.: e1-g2
Aux.: g1-e2
Aux.: j1-f1
Aux.: e2-g1
Archer: e/f3-e/f2 (left center protected, the Aux. at g1 can later
freely move to k1 and back to g1, that is, back and forth)

Archer: k/l2-k/l3
Aux.: l1-j2
Archer: k/l3-k/l2 (right center protected)

Archer: m/n2-m/n3
Aux.: m1-o2
Archer: m/n3-m/n2 (right edge protected)

15 moves

This sequence can be protracted by the opponent, but not prevented.
The opponent cannot move an Archer to the third row, to block Green's
Archer of the same column, because he would be captured by the
diagonally adjeacent Archer in the next move. The sequence can also
played in a different order.
