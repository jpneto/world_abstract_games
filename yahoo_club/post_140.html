--- In abstractgames@y..., dtroyka wrote:
> So, poll question: What other single-shape tilings have been used
in
> boardgames? There's square, hex, and triangle of course, but are
> there others?

I can't think of one, and I think if we find one, the game will be
very obscure. I verified in the book, Introduction to Tessellations,
Dale Seymour and Jill Britton, Dale Seymour Publications, 1989, that
triangles, squares, and hexagons are the only three regular
tessellations. Of course, there are games that use multiple shape
tessellations, like Octiles and Kensington. I also discovered a
website, http://www.mathpuzzle.com by Ed Pegg, Jr., that displays
many very interesting tessellations, some of which might start a
creative person thinking. For instance, one page shows the 14
different types of pentagon that will tile a plane.
