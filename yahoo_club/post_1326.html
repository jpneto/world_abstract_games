The smaller sites are primarily game sites where socializing happens
to take place, and the larger sites are primarily social sites where
games happen to be played. There's nothing too amazing about that.
But the group mindlessness I've described isn't just "less skilled"
players interacting. They might play tens or in some cases hundreds
of times not knowing the rules and, more importantly, not having any
interest in learning the rules - just randomly clicking on squares
until they accidentally select a legal move.

I don't want to belabor the point. There's nothing I can do about it
in any case. I'm just commenting that this seems like a social ill to
me. You have adults engaging in an activity not more sophisticated
than a six month old baby smacking two wooden blocks together. It's
not just sheer stupidity although that does seem to be a factor.

The ratings don't really help with a relatively new and obscure game
like Dipole. There are only a handful of skilled players. I play
anyone and everyone just to be a good sport but I can't do the zombie
thing. It's torture. It's like the dice race endgame of Backgammon.
No can do.
