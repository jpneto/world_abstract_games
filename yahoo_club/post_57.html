In play testing the way of go. I started
wondering whether I should add an "irreversible gain" rule
similar to the one in Epaminondas
which basically says
that if the opponent can match the gain one makes then
the gain is wiped out. In my game that means if one
side makes a capture in one turn and the opponent
immediately makes another capture in the following turn then
the captures doesn't contribute to the contract. This
of course opens the game up to possible draws but
might create a deeper game. The question than comes
down to the weighing of the disadvantage of having
draws with the advantage of depth. And which side is
heavier and more likely.

Any response appreciated.
