Well, Chess has issues with Drawishness. The issue of a game being
drawn-prone needs to be resolved somehow. A draw condition shouldn't
be an issue. There are uni-sided escape games where a draw condition
is considered a win by side with superior numbers (aka, one side is
prevented from having an escape happen). Not sure why similar can't
be done for other games. It does force a player to play defensively,
but then that is the nature of the game. They do this in chess anyhow
today. Guy gets the lead, and then pushes things towards draw state.
