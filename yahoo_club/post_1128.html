I also want to add several comments here:
1. If you want to appeal to women, you focus on solving problems,
rather than beating someone.
2. Second, the reason for the world champion, and so on, is to be able
to come up with a spokesperson/icon for abstracts, that people can
relate to and act as a face to promote abstract strategy games.
3. Abstract strategy games ARE about competition. They are about
beating people in a battle of the mind. It is all about the
competition here, and that is a sales point. If you to spin it as a
who is the brainiest, then people get bored. You spin it as who is
the best of the best, and the challenges you overcome, then it works.
3a. You want to get interest, you need to have interesting stories
people can follow, that is of interest. I mean, take the movie and
graphic novel 300. Who is normally interested in learning about a
battle where a bunch of Spartans go off to get slaughtered by
Persians. That is NOT the story. And for people not into abstracts,
the game itself isn't the story, it is the players and what they overcome.
Check out how a little spin can get someone interested in Go:
http://www.boardgamegeek.com/thread/210058
4. If you want to draw women in, I would go with team play for
abstract games, so women can team up and do teamwork and cooperating.
This isn't normal to abstract strategy games, but can get there.
