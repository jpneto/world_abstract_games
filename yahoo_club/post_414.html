While Gobblet may be a draw with perfect play, I don't think the
reason given is sufficiently convincing. You must have at least
four pieces on the board to win. If a larger gobblet "captures" a
smaller goblett, but moves away, the smaller goblett is exposed
again. This can serve to "pin" your opponent's large gobletts and
deprive him of moves. If one player just tries to move his large
gobblets around the board, and the other player keeps placing
smaller goblets, eventually there will be a threat that can't be
convered.
Can you provide an example of a game where one player with just the
three large goblets (plus the threat of placing a fourth) draws
against a more typical style of play? It need not be perfect, just
an example of an ordinary game, so we can see how it might be done.
