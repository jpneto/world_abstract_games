My previous posting got me to thinking about curvature. In this 1D
game, it is taken that each stone has exactly two liberties. These
liberties need not be located 180 degrees from each other but can
take place anywhere around each stone.

So stone can be place anywhere on a 2D field, they just would not be
allowed to occupy spaces adjacent stones which have both liberties
taken.

This could create a 1D game of extreme depth and complexity. As
captures are made, new and different lines could form. And thus the
game could loop back on itself, just never cross an existing line.

Now imagine the same 1D game played in 3D space. Maintaining the two
liberty restriction. Those line segments could dip and curl,
creating an awesomely mind-boggling game.
