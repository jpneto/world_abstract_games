Hey Mark, I want to add something about Dipole. When I had first read
the rules, a few things didn't click with me. I didn't play, just
read the rules, and read certain things into it. I ended up reading
into the rules how other games work. In my case, I just didn't get it
until I just read the rules, and stop reading things in, and then it
clicked.

What you could be seeing is people PRESUMING things are there, and
they play the game that way, like no backwards moving. Also, they
presume the game is in the stacking game family and works like Focus,
where the objective is to capture the pieces of the other player.
Dipole is an outlast your opponent game, with capture as a means of
doing this. It is a bit unique in this regard, and people end up
missing something.

In other words, it is "A little knowledge is a dangerous thing".
People may be doing this with Dipole and missing the game itself.
Like, they wonder why you can't move backwards, and so on. I found
that the best thing is to PRESUME people are idiots (In other words,
they will not have the same blatantly obvious assumptions you have),
but don't ACT with them they are. If you do the reverse, PRESUME they
are moderately intelligent and then ACT like they are idiots when you
relate with them, you end up disappointed at life, AND also have very
poor relationships with humanity.
