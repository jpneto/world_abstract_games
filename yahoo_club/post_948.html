Thank you for the update, Ola. A link verifying your claim:

http://www.educationallearninggames.com/pylos-game.asp

Since Gigamic had a contract allowing them to do what they wish with Pylos,
it obviously is not plagiarism. I apologize for jumping to that
conclusion.

I suppose it's possible that once Gigamic had the rights, they made
unfavorable changes to the game. But that would presumably be their right
under the terms of the contract. If that's the case, I'm not too thrilled
with that process, but business is business, and it's not nearly as
distasteful as unauthorized copying with modifications.
