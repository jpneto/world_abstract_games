No, that's not true. At the end of the game there is usually a clear
threat and not many ways to counter it. So even rather long endgame
problems appear to be common in LOA. In early stages it's not necessary
to consider every move as LOA is at this time very much a game of
position. The same is true for Go: You don't think about every move in
the Fuseki stage, but later -- in the endgame (Yose) or when you have
to find a tesuji (life & death, cutting, escape) you just consider very
few moves.
