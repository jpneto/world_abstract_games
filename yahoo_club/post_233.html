> Phalanx is described in Abstract Games 11, page 10.
>
> Drawing strategy
> ----------------
>
> The game is dead (for serious competitive players) if it's possible
> to move your pieces to a position where they can't be captured,
while
> it is possible to move a piece back and forth, if none of your
piece
> has been captured so far.
>
>
> Solution:
>
> 1. Move the Archers forward.
> 2. Move the Auxiliaries just behind them forward along the
diagonals
> just one step (to the right or left).
> 3. Then move the Archers back to the initial position. (8 moves).
> 4. Afterwards you can move the central Auxiliaries indefinitely
> sidewards.
>
> The opponent can't stop you to form an inpenetrable line which each
> piece being secure at the front line. If he tries to prevent the
> drawing moves with one of his own pieces, it is captured and
> afterwards the inpenetrable line is formed anyway (but now you win,
> because you have captured pieces!).
>
> A rather primitive game.
>
