I just played a game of Dipole with someone, if you can call it a
game. He removed all 12 of his checkers from the board on his first
turn. That was a new record. The next highest number of checkers
removed by one of my opponents on a first turn was six I believe.
This was the tenth time this particular individual had played Dipole.

People who have absolutely no understanding of the rules of Dipole
constitute the largest group of repeat, online Dipole players,
paradoxically. They simply enjoy making program allowed, though
otherwise random moves and watching how the game plays out. I looked
at the record of another individual who had no understanding of the
rules, and she had won about a third of the 150 games of Dipole she
had played.

I don't understand how playing a game over and over again with no
understanding of the rules and no interest in learning the rules could
possibly be entertaining to anyone, never mind the largest group of
players. Maybe I'm just not appreciating something.
