I'm glad you liked the Orbit article. The puzzle at the end is
pretty fun to work through, if you've got some extra time to kill.

According to Mr. Schmittberger (p.66), Rosette was invented in 1975.
It's played on a hexagon-shaped array of hexagons, with 7 hexagons
per side (127 total). (Of course, play takes place on the
intersections of the hexagons' lines --- I'm not sure how many
intersections that works out to. The game could alternatively be
represented as taking place inside the triangles of a hexagon-shaped
array of points, but it probably looks nicer the way it is.)

It occurs to me that there probably aren't a whole lot of games out
there that use 120-degree space. Rosette is the only good example I
can think of offhand that does. Surely there are others?
