I have been playing partial games of TWOG over a
quarter of my Go board. It makes a good 10x10 arena. I am
playing it toidally ie the sides wrapback on themselves.
Some very interesting tactics have came out of it.
They are all seemed to be guided by the pricipal of
channeling, that is one tries to channel your opponent's
moves. It reminds me of the martial art cliche of
turning back the opponent's energy against it.

Two
tactics seems to stand out right now, though I'm sure
there are many, many more undiscovered ones. They are
veiled threats and capped groups. The following sample
will illustrate the principles:

e6 is a veiled threat on e3. If white puts a piece
on e5, d6, f6, e7, then black will pounce on e3
because then the piece on e6's range will change from 4
to 3 and e3 will be in range. Similarly h6 is a
veiled threat against e6 which prevents black from
putting pieces on h5, g5, f6, h7.

d3 caps the
group at e3 and f3. which prevents white from extending
the group to g3 because then d3 would capture g3.
Also it prevents white to place pieces at d2, c3, and
d4, for similar reasons.

At this point in the
game there is escapes from these dilemmas but as the
game goes on it will become more and more difficult,
and the one that lay the best traps and can best
redirect the opponent surely would win
