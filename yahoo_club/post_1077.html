Where My Games Are Played

Author: Mark Steere

Date: 2007-06-21 18:17:23

Post ID: 1077

SuperDuperGames.org First and foremost. Aaron took a serious
interest in my games, programming all of them within a day or two of
release, a full two years ahead of the pack. A friendly,
knowledgeable group of advanced gamers. Best thing about SDG? Aaron.
Good guy, and wholly dedicated to providing unique abstract games,
free of charge. No Chess variants here.

ItsYourTurn.com Simply massive. Play Tanbo 'til you die. There's an
endless supply of willing participants. Internet response time is
instantaneous. Best site around if you're a hardcore Tanbo fan.

GoldToken.com No shortage of players here either. Dipole just came
online, and in the owner's words, "the first of our Mark Steere line
of games!" At GoldToken I have my own game section, my own discussion
group, my own icon (a black hat), and friendship with a whole bunch of
nice people. It'd be hard for me to think of something bad to say
about GoldToken. Real hard.

BoardSpace.net My personal favorite. Dipole, my favorite game, is
played *live* at BoardSpace. You don't make a move and then get
dressed and go out to dinner. Once again, gameplay at BoardSpace is live.

Gamerz.net Last but not least. Richard was the first, many years
ago, to program Quadrature and Tanbo for his email server. This put
me on the map. Granted, a teeny, tiny dot on the map, but still on
the map.
