It depends of the time during which a game was created. Pre-1880 very few
abstract games used anything else than "black" (or rather: dark brown) and
"white". Beginning with Halma, Reversi and Salta even the universe of
abstract gamers has become more colorfull. Obviously our cultural attitude
towards colors has changed significantly during the last 100 years or so. To
explain this change might be quite interesting for social scientists ...
(such as the people who publish the magazine "Board Game Studies" in the
Netherlands)

Ralf
