The winners of the annual Mensa Mind Games
this year included 4 abstract games (though
Cityscape is abstract only under the "broad"
definition). The winners were:

Blokus
Cityscape
Fire & Ice
Octiles
Trans America

Trans America I haven't played, but it sounds like some kind of
geography trivia game. Cityscape fails to be abstract in that each
player sets his own winning goals (based on the final appearance of
the "city" being built over the course of the game) without the other
player's knowledge. ... I suppose you could play Cityscape as a
purely abstract game, by making the winning goals common knowledge
and having the players set them one piece at a time, alternating. Not
sure whether that would damage the game or not.

On the whole, I'd say the choices were pretty good, among the games
that were submitted for review. There weren't many abstracts
submitted other than these winners.

This was the first year I attended the event, and I found the review
process extremely tedious -- most of the games submitted were quite
hopeless, and must have been submitted simply so the manufacture
could get our comments on their games. Not my idea of fun.
