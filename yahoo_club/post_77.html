The
closer to the center, the easier. A piece along the edge

cannot twirl around itself. A piece one square away from
the edge
could twirl around itself only by
landing onto an adjacent square
after some
intermediate twirls. But this isn't possible because

twirls are diagonal moves, like bishops, and will never
bring a
piece from a black square to a white square
for example. Well,
this isn't a nice feature.
Half the pieces will never meet the
other half.

symmetric to each other:

To explain the trajectory, we may
