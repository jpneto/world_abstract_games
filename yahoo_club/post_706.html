This new book written by Cameron Browne is absolutely a must for
anybody interested in abstract strategy games. 402 pages are
providing complete rules for over 200 connection games and variants
including several games of territory in which connections play an
important role (e.g. Go, Tanbo, Anchor, Orbit, Blokus). It is,
indeed, a "comprehensive study of the connection game genre",
although a few connection games are not included, such as the three-
dimensional game of "Blox" (Daniel LaLiberte, 1994) "Gomullo" (Roland
Johansson, 1999), which has the object to make the shortest
orthogonal connection between two opposite board edges that is
possible (ie a row), and "Connections" (Tom McNamara, 1972), a
variant of Bridg-It (not the same game as erroneously stated on page
160), but much deeper and, in fact, one of the most successful
connection games ever marketed. On the other hand the book describes
many games I didn't know and it will probably take me years until I
tried all of them.

A very good book!

Ralf Gering
