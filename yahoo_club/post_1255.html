> I'm interested in mutators, and get the idea that "mutator" is just
> another name for "game rule". Is this the case? In chess variants,
> mutators can change any variant into any other, so is there really a
> difference between a mutator and a rule [other than the spelling and
> pronunciation]? My original question was: "can gameboards be mutators?"

I think a mutator would be a game rule that makes no reference to
equipment specific to the game. For instance, the rule specifying how
a Knight moves on a square-grid board has no applicability to a game
where there are no Knights or in which the board is not square-grid.
But the "double move" mutator could apply almost anywhere.
