The "Mancala Gallery" at

http://www.abstractstrategy.com/mancala-g.html

shows several modern editions of Mancala variants.

1.
"Conga" is a well-known German two-dimensional Mancala variant.

2.
"Mandinka" appears to be identical to Oware / Warri.

3.
"Oh-Wah-Ree" is still very popular and its rules are published on the
web.

4.
"Sahara" by Pelikan is a Kalah variant with three seeds per hole
initially and played in rounds until 30, 40 or 50 points are reached.
The other rules are as in "Kalah".

5.
=84Kalaha" (Schmidt Spiele) is just German for =84Kalah", an American
game that was solved recently.

6.
The "Swahili Game" (Milton Bradley, 1968) and "Pass it on" (Selchow
and Righter's, 1978) are two mysterious games. I have no idea how to
play them, but they appear to be rather unusual.
