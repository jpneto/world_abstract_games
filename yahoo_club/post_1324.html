That would be a plausible explanation except I chat with them. Like I
might say "Looks like you're not getting it," after several games with
random play. And they'll respond "You noticed - LOL". I also get the
same questions again and again. "Why couldn't I capture your two with
my three?" "Hey, you cheated! I read the rules five times and
there's no mention of backward moves." The comments seem to match the
skill in most cases.

The problem isn't nearly as evident when playing Tanbo. You can kind
of fake it in Tanbo and survive for quite a long time. In Dipole
though, random moves usually just get you wiped out relatively
quickly, especially if you remove large numbers of your own checkers
for no reason.

This whole zombie culture thing really only exists on the larger
sites. The inverse relationship between membership size and mental
capacity is obvious, but it took me a few months to fully realize and
accept that there's a rampant epidemic of zombyism. It should be
noted that Jijbent.nl defies this principle, having simultaneously a
very large membership and a very high quality of players on average.
Unfortunately I think we may be dealing with a largely American
phenomenon here.
