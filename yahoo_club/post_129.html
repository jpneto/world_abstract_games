in fact, I have some more on my to-do
list. There are about 100 games yet to put on the
website. Of these games, the pre-90's games are about 20
or so. I would guess there are about 50 pre-90's
games available on the net in straightforward URL's,
that enter on my very specific criteria.

The
goal of the WAG site is twofold: first, to collect
these games as a support for our little community; and
second, I expect that newcomers (and everyone else)
entering the site, see that some game forgotten on their
shelf is not on the list, and then send the rules to
me. It already happened for a couple of games.
Perhaps Lawson would be able to find some more on his own
shelf!

The net opened an huge channel for us. For e.g., here
in Portugal, I don't know personally no one that is
interested in playing strange boards with new rules. There
are a lot of people playing Chess and Draughts, very
few playing Othello and GO, and the remaining
abstract games are virtually unknown. Without the net, I
would had a very hard task to find someone to even talk
about these subjects.
