About Sid Sackson: A Gamut of Games (1982, page
134).

"After Focus was printed in Scientific
American Magazine, several correspondents called my
attention to the possibility of the second
player
initiating the moves of the first player. (...) White can
continue until he sees a chance to profit by deviating.
And if he doesn't, he is still assured a draw.
My
answer to someone who plays in this manner, is not to
play with him."

My answer to Sid Sackson:


"You are a foolish guy who is just offended that his
game doesn't work as pointed out in a renowned science
magazine. You ought to be ashamed to
coquet with such a
childish behavior! If you are a bad loser, you shouldn't
play at all!"

Even Sackson didn't feel
comfortable with the solution given in Scientific American
which threatened to kill his game.

For that
reason he wrote:

"One of the following rules can
be adopted:

1. If neither player can
immobilize the other, the
one who played second is
considered the loser.
2. Before the actual play of the
game begins, each
player in turn switches the
position of one of
his pieces with one of his
opponent's pieces.
The second player is not permitted
to make a
switch which will restore the
symmetry.


THE EXPOSITOR
