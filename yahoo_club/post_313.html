> Dispatch isn't a new game, but a variant of Pentamino Go which was
> described by Wayne Schmittberger in his book "New Rules for Classic
> Games" (in: "Omino Go").

The original idea was very different from any Go variant.
The problem is that its playability was not good, and while
changing the rules to improve the game, it turned out like
a kind of Omino Go (in fact, none of us thought about
its similarities with Omino Go until your post), but...

> Schmittberger's original game is more elegant, since it goes without
> artificial and unnecessary rules such as "dispatching" while they
> result in a game which is pretty much the same as Dispatch, except
> that it has more combinatorial variety.

...the dispatch restricts the placements of stones: your
next placement depends on where you drop your previous pieces,
and in this point it's very different from a typical
Go variant. You can secure a territory by preventing
the enemy to dispatch a platoon inside it, you don't
need two safe holes for that, like a true Go variant.
