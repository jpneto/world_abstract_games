Just in message 1167 of this forum. I thought of it moments before I
posted that message, and named it by tongue-in-cheek analogy with FRC.

I suppose the best way to play it would be something like this: have a
"deck" of the 960 opening setups, and have each player separate the
deck into at least 2 and no more than N piles, each pile containing no
fewer than 1/K of the remaining cards in the deck. (Tournament
director will specify the values of N and K beforehand. K would have
to be greater than N.) If the pile chosen contains more than one card,
the player repeats this step. The other player inspects the piles and
chooses one of them. If the pile chosen contains only one card, that
setup is used for the game, and the player who chose it plays Black.

I'd guess (only a guess) 5 and 10 would be suitable values for N and K.

The concept of each player placing one piece in the back row for both
sides until everything is populated according to the Chess960
constraints is probably a simpler and clearer way of doing this,
though TNRC would be "freer".
