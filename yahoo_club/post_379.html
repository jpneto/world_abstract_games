I'd be glad to play some Diagonal 45 Twixt and some Y after the holidays. I've been playing solitaire games of Y on the base-11 board, which works extremely well, though I haven't figured out how to notate it. (Surely there's some easy method?) To anyone who hasn't tried it, I can't recommend curved Y highly enough.

There's actually no racing element in quadrant Hex. Consider that each of the four small games is subject to the same laws that govern the large game. Therefore if the board is completely filled with stones, one and only one player will win three or more out of the five games. However I agree with you that quadrant Hex isn't a pure connection game, in the sense that it lacks a single focused goal. (In a way it's a bit like a territorial game.) Connection games derive some of their undeniable charm from the great simplicity of their objectives.

Steve
