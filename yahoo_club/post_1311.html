Hi, Mark. I would be interested in discussing various aspects of
abstract games with you, and would work at being polite. So, if you do
start a group, sign me up, even though I won't abandon this site, and
will ask you to look in at the IAGO site, also.

Something I'm looking at is high levels of organization to control
multimove large chess variants. I've restricted the ability of a piece
to move by requiring it to start in proximity to one of a few unique
activator pieces.

I've done some work on 12x16 boards which is at least playable. And it
indicates 2 ways to extend chess variants to much larger board sizes
while still leaving the game playable by humans. It involves a certain
hierarchical control of individual [single-square-occupying] pieces,
and moving pieces in groups. Guess I'm working on practical ways of
making metapieces for giant chess games, from arbitrary group movement
rules [SpaceWar] through multiple leader units [Chieftain Chess] to
chesimals, "chess-animals", that contain around 10 pieces and move as
a group.

If any of this sounds interesting, sign me up. If not, make another
suggestion of topic, I'm flexible.
