Korneliussen's pie rule, if I understand it right, gives the second
player the option to switch sides. There is the possibility that the
second player can win (easily) no matter what the first player does,
but I don't know how often that would happen in practice (probably not
often). Hutnik's rule would cope better with this case, assuming the
second player's winning strategy isn't obvious.

When you speak of "the pie rule moving pieces", is that the idea of
having the first player rearrange some of the pieces (rather than
moving by the rules)?
