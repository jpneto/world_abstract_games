> Not to be a nitpicker, Maurizio, but Star and especially Fire and
> Ice seem to share Hex's vulnerability to first move advantage.

No problem. I like precision, so I don't give a negative connotation
to nitpicking. Star and *Star (pronunced "starstar") are actually two
(slightly) different games. I never played the former, so I don't have
an opinion on it.
I consider *Star, on the other hand, to be only weakly influenced by
first move. There is a central position which can be used by both
players for connection, and this dramatically reduces the advantage of
claiming the center first. Of course having "sente" helps, but I would
compare *Star to Go without komi: on average black is advantaged, but
the great great majority of times who played better (also slightly)
wins regardless of starting order. If I were to estimate a Komi for
the 51-points *Star board it would be something like 3.
Of course take everything I said with a grain of salt, because I
played *Star less than 10 times.
Regarding "Fire and Ice", I played the "Abstracta Marathon" e-mail
tournament with good results (I had to forfeit from the final group
due to lack of time). I didn't notice a great first player advantage,
altough some other players tought it was there. Somebody also proposed
a variant for solving this "problem". If I recall correctly it
consisted in awarding Ice (the second player) a double move to be
performed at his choice, but only once in the whole game.
