This is one of those terms which has been bandied about for years and
many have varied definitions for it. And can be a great topic for
further discussion.

Are random elements forbidden in abstract games?

What are the allowed pieces for an abstract game? Tokens, tiles, etc.

Must the abstract game be played upon particular fields?

I know of several games which I would term 'abstract' which would
stretch the preceeding questions.

There are various games which utilize dice.(i.e. Hounds and Jackals)
Some even cards.(i.e. Risk) Others have no established field of play.
(i.e. Trax)

Let us agree that abstract games should challenge the mind.

If there are random generators in a game, these should not be the
only determining factor of play. The player must make some form of
choice, some decision during the play, or else the game is merely
subject to luck and a game of chance.
