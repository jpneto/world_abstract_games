Hi Mark,

Yes, Trellis has done some evolving. The original version, described
in June 2000 GAMES, took place on a 17x17 board (counting the
points), which was divided into four 9x9 quadrants (note that some
points belonged to two quadrants and the center point belonged to all
four quadrants). Five games took place at the same time --- one in
each quadrant and one on the full board. The object of each game was
to connect the top to the bottom (for Black) and the left to the
right (for White). The person who won at least three of these five
games was the winner. You played just one stone on your turn.

Shortly after publication I tried applying the quadrant idea to the
game Hex (i.e., Black tries to connect the upper right to the lower
left of each quadrant and the full board, and White tries to connect
the upper left to the lower right of each quadrant and the full
board --- three or more out of five wins). I expected it to work
comparably well but was surprised --- it was considerably more
interesting, I thought. (I think this results from the tension
between the two "acute" quadrants and the two "obtuse" quadrants).
Not long after this I decided the tactics on the checkered grid could
use some spicing up. This led to the idea to play two stones at a
prescribed distance apart.

So the original version of Trellis split into two games: Square
Trellis (checkered grid, no quadrants, two stones per turn) and
Quadrant Trellis (rhombus board divided into quadrants, just one
stone per turn). Both games were described in October 2001 GAMES.
Since then I've chosen Square Trellis to be the standard game and
changed the name of Quadrant Trellis to Quadrant Hex, since it is
really a Hex variant rather than its own game.

Steve
