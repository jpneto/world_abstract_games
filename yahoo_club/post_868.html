> These are not true stacking games! Physically they appear like stacks, but

I'll grant you that the stacks in Sprawl and Stack Chess are not true
stacks: the stacks can only be one color, and must be sorted from
smallest at the top to largest at the bottom. So they're essentially
just sets, with constraints about what can be added to or removed from
a set.

However, I don't see the distinction between Moscow Ice and E. They
are both variants of Chinese Checkers, and both involve stacks of
pyramids of different colors, with only the top piece being able to
move. E does have a way to swap pieces from inside stacks if the
colors are appropriate, but the stacks are otherwise last-in-first-out
structures.

is enough for it to qualify as a true stacking game.
