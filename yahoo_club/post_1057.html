Has anyone tried Pentago yet? I haven't, but if I've understood the
rules then they're quite simple. The two players are Black and White,
the board is 6x6 and has four 3x3 sections, a move consists of placing
a stone of your color and then rotating one of the 3x3 sections, and
the object is to have a line of 5 stones in your color at the end of
your turn. As far as I know any rotation of any section is legal, and
I haven't heard that there's any special rule about overlines (lines
of 6 stones). AFAIK both the placement and the rotation steps are
required.

I was just wondering whether this might make a good 3D game, perhaps
on a 4x4x4 board made up of eight 2x2x2 sections with an object of
getting a line of four. Would anyone like to try a game by email?

The 3D rotations would probably be tricky to visualize from a flat
board diagram, but there are a lot of standard 4x4x4 "tic tac toe"
games around on which the game state could be recorded, for
considering your moves.
