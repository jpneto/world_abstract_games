What Rich is trying to do is create a framework to organize some part
of chess variants into a coherent package. His aim is marketing more
than anything. The CV site is a repository, a library of games. Its
aim is to provide a home for any chess-related game.
If I was more awake, I'd probably be able to explain this better. Rich
is looking to bring a subset of variants that is similar to FIDE and
can be easily understood by any recreational chessplayer to that very
large group of recreational chessplayers. The chessvariants.org site
is basically just there; it lets you find it. It accepts pretty much
anything, as long as it's reasonably understandable.
Maybe it's the difference between a society and an organization; one
exists, the other exists with a goal.
