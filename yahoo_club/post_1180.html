> The question is "Can an abstract game have random events after the
> coin toss but before the first move?". To me the answer is obviously

After sleeping on it, it's obvious to me you're right: one of the
basic classic theorems about abstract games is that with perfect play
the outcome would always be the same (either first-player win, second
player win, or draw), and this obviously wouldn't hold if Chess960
mechanisms were allowed. So I'm joining the Steere camp.

Thompson Nonrandom Chess (which I described a few messages ago) would
be abstract, though...
