Well, there might not be much of a difference
then. With double-moves the one-color crossing player
is going to play a good move with a color and play
as bad a move as possible with the other color. The
other player is going to do the opposite to balance it,
focusing on one direction with the other color and
avoiding to help drawing a cross in either color. I guess
that whatever they do, opportunities to switch colors
are going to occur anyway, with single moves as well.
I mean, if you fight against your opponent who
draws a cross and you play as well as her, you are
playing exactly like her indeed. If she focuses on one
direction with one color and you fight it, you do the same
-- in the orthogonal direction with the other color.
etc.

Anyway, I like double-moves :^)
