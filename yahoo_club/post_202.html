The September issue of GAMES (their 25th anniversary issue) describes
a new game called Dominions, by Christian Freeling (author of the
games at the mindsports.net\Arena site. Apparently it's going to be
added to the site soon, and it looks like the site will be revamped.
In the illustration, the background is white instead of that (to me)
unpleasant blue.

Dominion is played on an order-9 hexagonal grid (hence of 217 cells,
if I figured that right), and has elements of Go, Othello, and
Kaliko. The pieces are hexagonal tiles, somehow made so that they can
only be placed on the board in one orientation (on a computer screen
that's easy, of course), white on one side and black on the other,
and each player has a set of 63 tiles in hand. Each tile is marked
uniquely with from one to six lines connecting the center to the
midpoints of the various sides. (Since these tiles cannot be rotated,
and they all have at least one line, 2^6-1=63 are possible.)

Then the game resembles Go, where a group of allied pieces are
connected by these paths, and has to retain at least one "liberty"
(path terminating on an open space); but if it loses its last
liberty, it gets flipped to the other color (as in Othello) rather
than removed.

There are some other rules regarding placement (see GAMES, you should
be subscribing anyway! or mindsports.net will have definitive rules
soon). Schmittberger gives it his highest recommendation. I look
forward to playing it.
