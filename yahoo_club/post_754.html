I'm also saddened about the magazine's demise. Still, I think the
honorable thing to do would be for Kerry to step up and at least do the
following for his paid subscribers:

1) Provide some explanation of what is going on and what he plans to do
about it.
2) Make some good faith attempt at reimbursing those who remained loyal to
the publication and paid in advance (or minimally some form of apology).

It's only fair to those who put their trust in his enterprise - and at
some level, the cash amount at stake really has nothing to do with it.

Having said that, I think I have the right to decide where and how my
money is spent, "paltry" or not. And when a contract is violated, no
matter how small, I think those affected at least deserve both an
explanation and a good faith effort to address the situation in some way
shape or form.

At any rate, I hope that Kerry steps up and doesn't "go dark" on us, that
would be a disappointment. I also wish him well.

-- Greg
