Hi Doug,

Thanks for the info. I actually have heard of all those games but I had
forgotten that Proton is a 4x4, so I've added it to my list. Since I first
posted my query I've found some more games and various people have clued me
in to still others so my count is now up to 44 (including 7 of my own
design). I'm attaching my current list in case you're interested.

As to why:

There is a generic non-patented game system out there called the piecepack.
It is to board games what a standard deck of cards is to card games (go to
www.piecepack.org to learn more). Last year I
entered a game called Alien City in the Second Piecepack Design Competition
and ended up winning. The way these contests work, the winner has the option
to define the next contest theme and rules, and to judge the contest. He
also (based on the short tradition) provides the prize for the next winner.
I accepted, and subsequently launched the Changing Landscapes design
competition. I decided that the prize would be a hand-made hardwood 4x4
gridded playing board, bits, and case, along with rules for games that could
be played on it. One reason for this choice was because I knew of a couple
of 4x4 games I liked and also had designed two myself that fellow gamers
seemed to enjoy. Also, I wanted to give something game-related, unique, and
hand made, and this fit those requirements. Once I made this choice and
announced it however, it seemed like a good idea to look for more games to
fill out the rule booklet a bit. I worked on some more designs of my own and
I looked through the pictures from the list on the abstractstrategy website
and found quite a few more 4x4 games, but all I had for many of them was a
picture, sometimes along with author or publisher. After searching for more
information and finding rules for only a few of the games it occurred to me
that I could post on the spielfrieks and BoardGameDesign yahoogroups, asking
for information. This has actually worked out quite well because I've found
some pretty good games I didn't know of before. Many of them are also
playable with a piecepack or with some checkers or Ice House pyramids on a
board drawn on a piece of paper. Thus games like this are excellent travel
games because they are extremely portable without having to resort to tiny
inconvenient pieces.



-Mike
