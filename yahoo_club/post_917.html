Just to steer the topic back to absract games (certain members are
wasting a lot of bandwidth off topic). The abstract games Bashne
and Lasca are two interesting historical footnotes which also happen
to be cornerstone games of the ultra exclusive group, "true-stacking-
games". If I may quote revered game inventor Christian Freeling:

"[Emergo's] origin is a really bad game called Bashne, invented some
two centuries ago in Russia."

"There must have been some vivid discussions in some Sjaski club in
Russia, early in the nineteenth century."

"It may have been the vodka, but they felt they couldn't do without
it."

"Saying, as the Russian Chess historian Sargin did, that it
is 'difficult to make correct calculations in Bashne' is putting it
mildly indeed: there's an actual hazard of brain damage. Usually one
fights an opponent and uses a game. Here it's the reverse."

And finally, "Lasca died a modern classic. It's time to actually
bury it."

I have finally solved the puzzle of why Ralf is so aggravated by
Christian Freeling. Christian is right! And Ralf knows it!!
