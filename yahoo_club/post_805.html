Ralf, clarity is not in direct correlation with the number of changed
stones. At some NIM games, a lot of stones are removed but usually
these games have good clarity. On the other hand, games with little
changed stones per turn can have very bad clarity. Just this week,
Larry Wheeler told me about his game Cellf (check at Zillions site)
which is a nice original idea but the game is very hard to follow.
However, the game just changes one stone at a time.

I don't think it's just a problem of my part (and of Cam's): LOA is
hard to follow. If there is just one or two changed stones, many other
stones change their move range, since move is a parameter of the board
position. Folding that in two or three moves in the future, and even
tactical plans become blurry.

Oc, clarity is a qualitative measure. People that never saw chess
would say it has the clarity of Ultima (another game with bad clarity)
even if for, say, a chess master, the classic game is a wide open
landscape.

Anyway, what games do you think are better examples for the concept?
