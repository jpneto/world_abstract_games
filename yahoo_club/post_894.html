Impasse is a game I created a couple of years ago, but which
remained virtually unknown until Aaron Dalton dredged it up from the
depths of obscurity and programmed it for SDG
(superdupergames.org).

Now I've condensed the rules of Impasse onto a single page and so
now I'm kind of reintroducing it.

http://www.impasse.net/Impasse_rules.pdf

Of course feel free to comment. However, Impasse is, and has been
for some time, in its final release.

Impasse, like Checkers, is a true stacking game since true stacks of
checkers of height two truly occur during play. Yes, the stacks
could be represented by a flipped over single checker. Likewise in
Byte, for example a stack of four white checkers on top of four
black checkers could be represented by writing in pencil the
hexadecimal number "F0" on a disk of paper. In both cases, it's
condiderably more convenient to simply employ the stacks, as
intended.

Dictionary.com definition of "stack": "An orderly pile, especially
one arranged in layers."
