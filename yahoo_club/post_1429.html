> I'm not thrilled with the mechanic. Every time you move a "versus"
> you attract all the pieces around it closer to it, which reduces the
> mobility of the versus to the point that it can barely move. I
> haven't played it either but that's my initial impression without
> having played.


Well, no! You only attract opponent pieces one hex, you flip them
over and now they are under your control (as indicated by the central
dot color).

After a couple of games i noticed that it is really important to play
agressively: convert many opponent pieces to your own to limit your
opponent's movement options and also try to minimize the opponent's
Versus piece movement options using the pieces as blockers.

The game runs smooth but the real problem appears at the end when
only one scoring hex remain: there are situations where the Versus
pieces and a normal piece are locked in a position where no one is
willing to move his Versus to prevent the opponent from scoring.

The designer added a rule to avoid this situation: "a Versus piece
may never land on a scoring hex".
