could you correct the the following mistakes:

1.
>> POLISH CHECKERS
>> Like International Checkers but soldiers may also capture backwards

It's one of the outstanding features of international draughts
that "soldiers" (men) may capture backwards. Polish Checkers is
identical to International Checkers, except that the game ("Warcaby
polski") is sometimes also played on 64 squares. BTW, there is a
Polish variant of Hexagonal Draughts ("Warcaby heksagonalne") which
perhaps predates Christian Freeling's variant. It was invented in
1985 by Jacka Sodowskiego in Szczecina.

http://www.warcaby.beep.pl/
http://www.warcaby.beep.pl/wheksa.html


2.
>> SPANISH CHECKERS or POOL CHECKERS

>> Like International Checkers but played on the English Draughts
>> setup. Also, when a soldier is promoted the jump sequence stops.


Spanish Checkers differs from Pool Checkers:

The double corner is in Spanish Checkers on the left side, while it
is in Pool Checkers on the right.

White starts in Spanish Checkers, while Black starts in Pool Checkers.

These two differences show that Pool Checkers was influenced by Anglo-
American Checker / English Draughts.

There is the following rule in Spanish Checkers (according to the
official rules of the Spanish Checkers Association:)
If you can choose among several captures each numbering the same
number of enemy pieces, you must play that one which captures more
enemy kings.

Also, your statemtent "like international" checkers is always wrong,
as this is based on wrong international rules. Men don't capture
backwards in Spanish Checkers, German Checkers, Pool Checkers and so
on ... but they do in International Checkers.
