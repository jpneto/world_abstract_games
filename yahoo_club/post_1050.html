No, no diagrams yet. But the idea is that you have a 10x10x10 cube
made from perfectly clear glass, with its three axes oriented Up-Down,
North-South, and East-West. There are three 10x10 boards on the Down,
North, and West faces of the cube, and light shines through the cube
from Up, South, and East. The players, let's say Red and Blue, get to
choose single cubes within the 10x10x10 cube, and whenever they choose
one of these cubes it casts a Red or Blue shadow on each of the three
10x10 boards. (The players aren't allowed to play a position that
would cast the same shadow as any previous play: that's what I mean by
"not in any orthogonal line with any previously-chosen cell.")

Then the three boards where the shadows are being cast could be
interpreted as games of Hex. There would be some issues with such a
game, but perhaps additional tweaks to the rules would lead to an
interesting game.

I suppose the same concept also might be made to work with other
games. However, I admittedly present this idea in half-baked form, for
whatever it's worth.
