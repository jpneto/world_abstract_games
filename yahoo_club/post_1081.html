BoardSpace.net is my favorite site to play at mainly because the games
there (including Dipole and Tumblingdown) are played in real time.
Also the graphics are 3D perspective, textured, and very realistic
looking. It's about as good as, and in some ways better than, playing
in real life. I've wanted to get one of my games on BoardSpace for
years and I'm grateful to Dave for finally accepting and implementing
one of them (Dipole).

Tumblingdown is a very interesting, very unusual, high quality game
which I have played and which I do endorse and recommend. However I
would like to take issue with the "perfect", "near perfect" (on
another forum), and "extremely simple." There should be a liberal
sprinkling of IMHO's in there. I'm not aware of any reason why
endless cycles cannot occur in Tumblingdown which, if true, knocks it
clear out of the perfection ballpark (IMHO). As far as the simplicity
of Tumblingdown, you have specially recognized stacks of uniform
checkers (kings), the possibility of more than one king per player,
kings being created and destroyed on a single turn, and two different
kinds of moves. In my own portfolio I have two games, Copolymer and
Crossway, which are arguably much simpler than Tumblingdown. Hex
isn't even slightly concerned about Tumblingdown.

Again, I'm not pooh poohing Tumblingdown. Tumbling down is on a very
short list of games that I do recommend. I'm just noting the
conspicuous absence of IMHO's (IMHO).
