Hello all,

I was out of the club for a couple of weeks, and just saw the
discussion today.

In fact, this group was initially started to welcome discussions
about perfect-information games, i.e., no luck, no hidden
information. The "theme" is a minor concern herein; it does not
matter if the pieces are called Kings, stones, moons or whatever.

There are thousands of games, and accordingly, there are hundreds of
groups and mailing lists that deal with each game subtype. Of course,
this is not written on stone. People may think that a good argument
can be brought based on a Backgammon position or concept, for e.g.,
and I have no problem with that. But this is more an exception that
the rule. Focusing on a specific game group prevents Internet
flooding of contents. That's why there is rec.games.abstract on the
1st place...

I was never thinking of CGT definitions when I propose this club. CGT
games are included on perfect-information games, not the opposite. So
CGT games are welcome, but they do not exhaust the discussion context.

There is also no strict restriction of 3+ player games, even if I
didn't have them in mind by the time I decided to create the club.
But as always, only if they fit into the perfect-information category.
