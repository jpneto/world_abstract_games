Oh! So it's "unlikely" is it? You seem to be admitting that there's
an element of luck here. There can be no doubt that a Chess expert
would find some starting arrangements preferable to others.

The initial setup is part of the game, and a random initial setup
introduces luck into the game.

Abstract games *by definition* don't include random elements. Period.
Why is this such a stumbling block?
