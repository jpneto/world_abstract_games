I would say that Chess960 is an abstract game, because both players
have perfect information when they make their moves. They decide at
random which of the 960 setups to use, but this is prior to the actual
gameplay and no more of a problem than deciding at random which of
them is to play White. The same setup is used for both players, and
while some setups might accent or diminish White's advantage (or,
conceivably, reverse it), it's not essential that an abstract game be
well-balanced -- merely desirable.
