The best games which I discovered so far and which are not described
elsewhere on the web are:

Doppelte Dame
http://www.sweb.cz/lada.chytrackova/hry/dama.htm#Dvojita
(rules as in Anglo-Saxon Checkers , but two chess boars side by side,
White starts - I had to learn Czech to find the rules of a German
game!!!)

Jeu de Dames Africaines
http://www.sweb.cz/lada.chytrackova/hry/a-dama.htm
(always capture the largest number, because the game is obviously
influenced by Jeu de Dames International)

Kavkaz Dama
http://www.sweb.cz/lada.chytrackova/hry/o-dama.htm
(the Checkers variant of the Chechen freedom fighters was obviously
influenced by T=FCrk Damasi; play it with 33 pieces each player on a
board having 7x11 intersections)

Stredovetka bitva ("Medieval Battlefield")
http://www.sweb.cz/lada.chytrackova/hry/s-bitva.htm
(a fascinating checkers variant, reminiscent of Parton's variants --
necessary additional rule: the "rytiri" may not move back to a square
where they came from in the last move)

Ball's Chess
http://www.sweb.cz/lada.chytrackova/hry/b-sachy.htm
(just wonderful this game, not really a chess variant as there are no
royal pieces; goal is to reach one of the two squares where initially
the generals of the opponent were located)

BTW Boardline is actually Boarderline and not correctly described
(see the British Boarderline homepage).
