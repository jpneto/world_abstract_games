I think "historical significance" is what matters in the list. The
list measures what games have had important historical impact. This
is reflected in a mix of how much they are played, how popular they
are and their historical impact. Quality is another measure. It is
very tricky to go with "the best games ever" as in what a particular
person likes the most, because that is so subjective. Historical
significance is important to give things perspective. You don't want
the games to be the latest hot game that someone enjoys now, to be
overwhelm the decision-making. Eventually, you can have "Game of the
Year" awards for this new hot games. As of now, I believe a focus
needs to be to get current games recognized that are historically
significant. Even if the game is "solved" that isn't critical.

> 2) Would any categories be established initially?

That is appearing to be so. Issues arise when you deal with family of
games that are a bunch of regional variants without a single version
standing out. Three that fit this are: checkers, Tafl/Tablut, Mancala.

Anyhow, since a Hall of Fame process will have a political element, I
suggest, once things are set up, for people who want a particular game
or family of games ot get inducted, to get involved with the process.

As of now, I think 10 is the likely initial number. Then, every year,
we induct more. I am also looking at not just games, but people,
books, and innovations likely getting inducted also.
