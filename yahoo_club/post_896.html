I've often thought it would be good to have a booklet (or a book?) of
interesting variants that can be played with checkers equipment.

There are lots of "straight" checkers variants, by which I mean games
whose rules could all be expressed by writing putting various options
into the checkers rules and then saying something like, "German
Checkers uses options A1, B3, C2, and D4; Spanish Checkers uses
options A2, B4, C1, and D3; etc."

(Someone could even make a website: "Name your own checkers variant!
Choose your combination of options, type in the name you want, and
send us $20. You will receive a rule book and certificate, and your
variant will get its own page on this website!! Act now, supplies
going fast!)

But besides all those, there are original games like Byte and Impasse,
Archimedes and Loa, playable with standard checkers equipment, that
more people could be enjoying.
