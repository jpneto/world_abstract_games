Hi Richard,

Catching up on my gaming email...

A collectible card game based on mutators sounds a lot like my
metagame system GameFrame, which was indeed originally envisioned back
in 2004 or so as an open source collectible card game, but is now
incarnated as a pen and paper game, to meet the constraints of the
Games Unboxed book project.

http://rwhe.livejournal.com/tag/games+unboxed

I'm surprised you didn't allude to GameFrame when you started this
thread, since I invited you to collaborate on it when we were
corresponding about it back in early October and November.

GameFrame owes a lot to Joao's mutator concept, and openly
acknowledges the debt in the rules. Thanks, Joao! It's also been
influenced by Cosmic Encounter, Knightmare Chess, the GIPF Project,
and a lot more. "Frames" are a lot like mutators, except broader and
looser -- not as rigorous, and more like a game expansion than like a
single rule.

I'm not ready to publish GameFrame, but I can share some information
with anyone who's interested, as long as you treat it as confidential,
etc. I'm currently discussing opening the Games Unboxed project up a
little more to the public with Clark Rodeffer, my co-author, but that
discussion has been tabled until at least the New Year.
