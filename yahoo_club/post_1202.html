> What do you do to prevent an illegal configuration from happening? It
> is possible that someone put down a bishop, multiple pieces, and then
> have the only space left is the same color square as the other bishop.

Right, and rather than delve into all the details I would simply say
(as I did) "subject ... to the condition that at least one legal FRC
opening setup can be completed from the position." If the first White
Bishop is placed on a light square, then any subsequent placement that
uses up the last available dark square needed for the other White
Bishop would be illegal. Etc.

If the players find themselves unable to get to a legal opening setup,
it follows that some move has got them into this fix, and that move
was therefore illegal. I believe the usual Chess tournament rule upon
discovering that a previous move was illegal would be to back up to
that move and replay it.
