Why wouldn't it be an abstract strategy game? The anchor point for an
abstract strategy game, by IAGO standards, is 2 player combinatorial
game theory games. There is allowance for some deviation, but that is
the center point. I don't see where Chesimals has any imperfect
information or random elements of any sort. So, not sure why it wouldn't.

What had happened with "theming" or "chrome" was discussed, is that it
didn't have a quantifiable criterion. It had been put in the
criterion for evaluation via excluding games if they were any other
genre, and also in rules length.

All and all, I don't see why it wouldn't be. You start throwing in
stuff that causes rules bloat for theming reasons, then you deviate
from abstract strategy games.

That is my 2 cents on it.
