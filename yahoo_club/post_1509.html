I know that I am about 1 year late.
However I would like to point out why I don't agree with you, Mark.

Do you consider standard chess to be an Abstract Strategy Game ?
Yes ?

Then consider that in Chess, by the luck of the draw (of colors), will
probably receive a more advantageous starting arrangement than the
other player (i.e. will be white).

Even in one of the staples of abstracts (Hex) one of the player will
receive a more advantageous starting arrangement. Indeed he will
receive a WINNING starting arrangement (i.e. it will be the swapper).

In ALL games which are not theoretically drawn,the advantage will be
decided by elements outside the games (i.e. the pairing and color choosing mechanism) which are often random.
If you want to neutralize this effect you will have to play 2 games,
one with black and one with white.

So, would you consider abstract a game defined as the sum of two
Chess960 games with swapped colors ?
