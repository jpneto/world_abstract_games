Have you already read various discussions about this in the
(relatively new) Abstract Games subdomain at BGG?

The concept comes up in discussion relatively often.
The usual ideas include:

Depth of play (usually a notion of large number of skill levels
between a beginner and a master) - always more to learn, always
possible to keep progressing in skill

Simple elegant rules

All parts of the game should be interesting (e.g. the endgame is not a
boring foregone conclusion where both people know who's probably one
but they have to go through the motions to finish it and be sure; the
opening should not be forced down one or a few known "correct
sequences").

and more nebulous stuff like sense of drama, decisiveness, etc.

And of course 'passing the test of time'... players find that in
practice they like it and want to keep playing it.
