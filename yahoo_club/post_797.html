I had to dig around in the top of my closet to locate this game. It
was printed up to look like the album cover for an LP record.

I remember playing lots of games with a few friends and we were not
that very excited about it. There seemed to be something missing.
The end-games just seemed to be inevitable.

We tried a variant in which the following was allowed:

1. If the player was able to form a triangle, they had to option to
move another stone of their own. Not an enemy stone.

2. If the player formed a square, they were able to move two enemy
stones.

And cascade of formations were allowed with rule 1. So that if a
player formed a triangle then moved a stone which formed another
triangle then could move another stone. This includes the formation
of a square resulting from such a move. The only restriction being
forbidding repetition of position.

I know that this is only a slight modification to the original first
rule of triangle formation but it did liven the game up. It also
meant that forming squares became a little difficult if the opponent
was sharp.
