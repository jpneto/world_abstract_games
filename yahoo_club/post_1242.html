I will look to calm down. I am in no mood to get into heated arguments
with anyone. As far as I am concerned, IAGO needs to make as many
friends as it can here. There is a lot at stake. I ask everyone (and
demand of myself also) civility and patience. Please realize that
IAGO isn't going to be perfectly what everyone wants. I would like to
see a lot of groaning acceptance though, out of necessity of what
needs to be done.

What I will say is I look to have 2 player combinatorial game theory
games at the core and the "gravity point" things are supposed to head
towards. These games are more likely to get accepted into the
International Mind Sports games for one thing.

As I see it, practical reality does call for acceptance of Chess960,
Dark Chess and even Backgammon. Even the case of where IAGO may end
up getting involved in a chess-poker hybrid tournament (or even a
poker-backgammon tournament). IAGO isn't looking to be a poker
association, but if getting IAGO on TV explaining what it stands for
and represents and so on, needs to be done, it needs to be done.

I am asking people to please be civil (including myself) and also
realize that more harm will come to IAGO if things are done wrong,
that ever can happen to abstract strategy games (or abstracts).
Definition creep is a big deal to me.

And Mark, I am not looking to kick your games out of IAGO. I do want
them in, but realize that you may object to the way IAGO is going and
not want them there. IAGO needs to respect the wishes of designers
out there. I personally believe it is counterproductive to you, but
the decision is yours.

Also, please add your two cents (in a civil manner of course) and see
if you can get involved. The IAGO World Tour can definitely use 2
player only combinatorial game theory games dominating it, so see if
you can get any. This will set examples in place to prevent deviance.
