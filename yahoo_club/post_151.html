With GRYB-Go, stones may be entered onto the field on any open node.
The player would be tempted to place on positions which offer the
most liberties.

With "Time Vectors" Chess, the field would be divided into four
separate 4x4x4 areas. Each 4x4x4 area would occuppy one corner of
the overall field. The players would initially occupy 4x4x4 areas
which are diagonally opposite. These could be easily identified
merely by the orientation toward the individual player, but some form
of color indicator could be applied, such as a bit of tape.
The "capture-only" and "move-only" 4x4x4 areas would be in relation
to each of the players.

Please do not take umbrage to the use of the term "Time Vectors" when
referring to this form of Chess. Like I've stated before, I have not
settled on an actual title for this game. I would be interested in
any suggestions.
