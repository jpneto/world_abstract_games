Author: Rich Hutnik

Let's say you have multiple games, and you are trying them for the
first time. Neither player knows which side has an advantage. What is
the fair way to do this? When said you flip a coin, there is an
argument that the flipping of a coin to decide games makes the game in
question no longer an abstract strategy game (and, as described below,
it ends up no longer being part of tournament play for an association
of abstract strategy games). So, what exactly is the fair way to pick
sides?

Anyone have an answer here? I ask this, because I am looking to have
certain tournaments that would surprise players with games they have
never seen before (as a measure of creative problem solving instead of
memorizing lines of play). It is important to answer how to do this
fairly for tournament play.

If anyone can chime in here with an answer, it would be appreciated.
Even cases of where mutators are added (a set of standard rules
changers), or the board is modified in any way (where players never
saw it before), the ability to evaluate which side has an advantage
likely is unknown.

I also had the idea of having players play both sides, shot down for
some reason also here, so what exactly do you do here?
