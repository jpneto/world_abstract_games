Kind of. You won't be obviously locked into a move cycle. Eventually
I suppose either a position will repeat itself or someone will win,
but the problem is that it could conceivably take days before either
condition happened.

You can avoid losing by splitting one of your stacks into two of your
own stacks. Your opponent can't capture two of your stacks in one
turn. The only way you can't split one of your stacks into two of
your own stacks is when your last remaining stack has only one of your
checkers in it. In test playing, it seemed fairly easy to avoid that
situation.

The mystery here is how the "30 minutes" playing time was arrived at.
I test played it for about that long and the end was nowhere in
sight. It just seemed like endless churning.
