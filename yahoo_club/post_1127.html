But it isn't just women. Quite a few men have also expressed a view
that the competition aspect was over-stressed in Rich's original
announcements. It is a view I agree with.

Starting a new organisation will require as many people to participate
as possible. If putting more emphasis on a magazine featuring articles
about a game design, and, strategy and tactics helps garner more members
than it otherwise would have then surely that is a good thing? No-one
is saying that the competition aspect should be lost - for some it is
obviously very important - just that there are hobby players (probably
a much higher number of them) for whom competition isn't so important.
If you don't attract them then potentially you might not have a big
enough mass of people to make the organisation a success. This becomes
even more important when there are membership dues involved.
