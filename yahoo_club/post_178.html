Hi Mark,

I've always thought that backgammon, yes, even though it uses dice, is a
borderline case on being considered a true abstract strategy game. Please
note that many backgammon positions do indeed satisfy your above
requirement. As an example, take a look at the 1st and 4th problems on this
page:

http://www.inficad.com/~ecollins/backprobs.htm

In both cases, there is only one best move and the player has ALL the
information needed to figure it out. In both positions #1 and #4, there is
no "probably best move," but a VERY BEST move. (In both positions, Red must
make the one play which best saves the gammon.)

Of course, most backgammon positions aren't so cut and dried. It's often
difficult to determine which move is best, especially when one has the
random dice element to consider. But when using two dice, since the odds of
rolling any combination of numbers are known, then one can simply take this
into account when determining what the VERY BEST MOVE will be. Sure, the
dice gods may not be favorable, but it was still the best move at the time,
and should be again played if the exact same position where to occur again.
