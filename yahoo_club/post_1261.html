Some simple movement mutators that could be used without too much
difficulty:
Let's start with chess variants. Three simple related mutators are
"fission", "fusion", and "fluid".
Fission allows a "complex" piece to break down into its component
parts. A queen could "split off" a bishop which moves away from the
original position, leaving a rook in that original position.
Fusion is the opposite of fission, allowing a knight to move onto/into
a rook or bishop to form a chancellor or an archbishop.
Fluid is more flexible than the two previous mutators, allowing pieces
to move into, out of, or through other pieces.

Board Mutators is another concept that is relatively easy to implement
in CVs, in limited areas.
"Grow" is what I have in mind here. "Omnigrow" would add a row of
squares completely around the outside of a board, changing an 8x8 to a
10x10. "Unigrow" would add a row along just one edge, making an 8x8
into an 8x9.
You could also give pieces the power to add or subtract squares,
growers and destroyers.
Finally, the board could be re-arranged, with shifting ranks and files.

I have seen and/or discussed all of these options on the chessvariants
site.
