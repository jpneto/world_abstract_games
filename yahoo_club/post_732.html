Book of the Month:

100 Strategic Games - For Pen and Paper
=======================================


This book was written in April 2002 by Walter Joris, a Belgium expert
on paper-and-pencil games. It covers 100 games invented by Joris (as
claimed by him), except a few traditional games which were adapted to
be played on paper. You won't find Dots & Boxes, nor its French
forerunner La Pipopipette (invented by famous mathematician Edouard
Lucas in 1889). Also, Alternator is not included, one of my
favourites invented by Gregory K. van Patten in the 1990s. Moreover,
many traditional games are missing, which can easily played on paper,
such as Tic-tac-toe or Renju. This isn't really a drawback because
you'll find many games, which might be totally new for you.

Mancala lovers will be surprised to find several Mancala variants:

1.
Joris' Awele (#86)

This is the same game as traditional Oware (Awele, Warri, etc.), just
a simplified version played on a 2 x 3 "board". In the paper-and-
pencil version counters are crossed out before they are taken out of
their basin and distributed. Mancala enthusiasts might be annoyed by
his final statement: "A disadvantage of the Mancala games is that
they can become rather drawn out."

2.
Tornado (#87)

Obviously a plagiarism as this game is almost identical to Cirk
(Corn� van Moorsel, 1998). It only differs in a slightly different
starting position.

3.
Canicas (#88)

A very interesting game which can easily be made far more challenging
by increasing the number of internal rows (within the "fields" =
holes) from 3 to 6 and using 11 holes, instead of just 6. I also
would permit two marbles to be dropped on the larger board in each
player's first move, not just one. The game has a territorial
aspect, rather unusual for Mancala games.

4.
Rumba (#98)

Identical to La Tchouka (English: Tchuka Ruma), which was probably
invented by famous mathematician Henri Delannoy (discoverer of the
Delannoy numbers) in 1895 by transforming a Malay game to fit into a
mathematical problem. The sub-variant given by Joris is known as Le
Tchoukaillon, invented by V�ronique Gautheron in 1977.

Joris' book is very cheap (US$9.95 and Euro even less) which could be
an additional reason to buy it.
