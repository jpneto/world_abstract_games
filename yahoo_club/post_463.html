Crosse, published by TSR. My paraphrase of the (longish) rules:

object: A checkers variant won by having no piece of your own left,
or exactly four pieces that occupy the corners of a central 4x4 area.

board: 10x10 checkerboard whose central 2x2 is out-of-play, and the
other 12 squares in the central 4x4 are all in-play but follow a
counter-clockwise direction. The other, alternating squares in the
checkerboard pattern are in-play.

capture: Mandatory, maximum sequence of checkers-style captures by
jumping; in the central 4x4 this can be orthogonal. If no capture is
available, an attempted move is made as follows.

chance: There's a dice that specifies the direction (forward or
backward) and distance (range not given in the booklet), of a move.

move: The dice is rolled and a piece is moved to an empty square whose
direction (forward, backward, or sideways in the central 4x4) and
distance are dictated by the dice roll.

crosse: When a player occupies all four corners of the central 4x4,
his back row is cleared of his pieces, and he keeps rolling the dice
and moving so long as pieces are moved into his back row, and removed
from play.

-- William Chang (Saratoga CA)

p.s. the other three TSR abstracts (Chase, Kage, Steppe) seem more
interesting... Kage is like Quoridor; Steppe stacks tangram-shaped
pieces; Chase uses dice as playing pieces on a wrap-around hexagonal
board.
