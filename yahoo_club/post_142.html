I'm playing with Bill Taylor some progressive Y games, and in one
of our discussions, he said:

"Many games do quite well with the progressive transformer, 1-2-3-4-
5... But most have to be restricted in some way to keep the flavour
of the original game as much as possible, and prevent it becoming a
mere bludgeoning race. So, chess retains check-stop, our new Go has
atari-stop, 1-2-2-2 Hex (and Y) is often played non-adjacent, and so
on."

I was wondering on the 'and so on'. In fact, chess has several
restriction flavors (scottish, italian, english and portuguese
variants, check them at
http://www.chessvariants.com/multimove.dir/progressive.html )

Do any of you know any other progressive restrictions applied to some
other abstract game?

Joao

ps: btw, atari-stop Go, is just Go with progressive mutator 1-2-3-
4..., but the sequence stops with the first atari
