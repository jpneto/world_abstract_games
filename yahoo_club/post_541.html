Last week I invented a Hex variant, called Nex with the following
rules:

=================================
[NEX] On a Hex board, each player may either:
* Drop a stone of his color plus a neutral stone
* Turn two neutral stones into stones of his color and then turn
another stone of his color into a neutral stone.

Goals like hex: make a path with your stones or with neutral stones
between your two edges
=================================

Here are some sample moves:'?' means a neutral stone drop/flip, '!'
means changing a neutral into a friendly stone)

2. e7,g5? h3,f4?
3. i3,g3? g6,e6?
4. f6!,g5!,a11? d10,h4?

The game seems really interesting and I advise people who like
connection games to give it a try.

Anyway, this idea (drop a 1 friend + 1 neutral OR flip 1 friend + 2
neutrals) can be extended to many different games. I think that games
where each move is a drop are excellent targets like Go, Gomoku or
Hex. Do you people have any suggestions?
