This is what dictionary.com says on it:
http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/abstract

The more you try to model something in reality, to be more accurate,
relating to a specific topic, the more you push a game no longer being
abstract. When you work the other way, as what is typically thought
of as abstract strategy games, to work towards general principles that
are timeless, that is what matters for being abstract. Because of the
universality and timelessness, abstract strategy games allow for a
game to be used for training throughout the years.

I believe the heart of "abstract strategy" is, what is the most
general and timeless principles of strategy in games, and the closer
the game patterns itself after that, the more it is an abstract.
Thought this way, you would cluster the wording as abstract "strategy
games" (instead of "abstract strategy" games). In other words,
strategy games that are abstract, rather than games of "abstract
strategy". I don't even know if you can have any form of strategy
that is abstract, as we use strategy to apply to real life situations.
However, such situations repeat over and over again, which gives
abstract strategy games the ability to be timeless.
