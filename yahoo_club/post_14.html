Here is what I like about Scalus. The idea of
changing the size of a rectangle of stones is very
original. Also, it seems like a "natural" concept. That is,
it seems like a sensible basis for a game. I have
not tried the game yet.
Questions to ask: is there
a symmetry strategy? Can one player simply copy his
opponent's moves?
(I would think not, because you have
captures in the game. But something to think
about.)
Can the game repeat? In which case you might consider
something like the "Super Ko" rule, as in the classic game
of Go.
Finally, Scalus shares some
characteristics with another game called "Guess", or "GoChess".
Read about this interesting game
at
http://www.dfki.de/~endres/games/gochess/index-eng.html
or
http://www.cam.ac.uk/societies/archim/eureka/gess/

Good luck with Scalus !
