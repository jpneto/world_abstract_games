McNamara's game, properly called 'Connections', was briefly mentioned
on page 161. While patented much later (1993), it was already
invented in 1977. It reached the American market in 1992, where more
than 500,000 games were sold within the first year. It is used in
Japanese schools to teach children logic. In 2001 the Institute of
Informatics at Leipzig University organized a programming contest
that led to a renewed interest in the game of Connections.
