TRELLIS (standard version)

1. The board is a 15x15 checkered grid (counting the points rather
than the squares). The upper left square is a dark square. The
board begins empty, and play takes place on the points.
2. The pie rule governs the beginning of the game.
3. The object of the game is Hex-like. Black tries to connect the
top and bottom sides, and White tries to connect the left and right
sides. A corner point is considered to belong to both sides that
meet there.
4. On your turn you play two stones, which must be exactly three
points apart, in either an L-shape (i.e., the knight's relation) or
in a straight line.
5. It is permissible to play just one stone, and it is permissible
to pass.
6. Two same-color stones are considered to be connected if they're
orthogonally adjacent, or if they're diagonally adjacent over a "home
square." Black's home squares are the dark squares, and White's home
squares are the light squares.

ORBIT (standard version)

1. The board is a 16x16 grid (counting the points rather than the
squares). The board begins empty, and play takes place on the points.
2. The pie rule governs the beginning of the game.
3. The object of the game is Go-like. (It is often the case,
however, that territory need not be counted --- one player will
usually succeed in forming a giant, essentially game-winning "half-
orbit.")
4. On your turn you play a stone. It is permissible to pass your
turn. When both players pass in succession, the game concludes.
5. Two same-color stones are considered to be connected (i.e., as
belonging to the same group) if they're orthogonally adjacent or if
they're diagonally adjacent.
6. An "orbit" is a connected group which completely encircles one or
more points of the board. When an orbit is formed, any opposing
stones within the orbit are removed from the board, and it is
thereafter prohibited for the opponent to play within that orbit.
7. A "half-orbit" is a connected group which, together with one side
of the board, completely encircles one or more points of the board.
When a half-orbit is formed, it is thereafter prohibited for the
opponent to play within that half-orbit.

(Note: rules 6 and 7 can be summed up as "orbits capture and
prohibit, half-orbits prohibit.")

8. At the end of the game, any stones that cannot avoid capture are
automatically removed from the board.
9. It's possible for there to be shared territory (points where both
players are prohibited from playing).
10. Your score is simply your own territory. Captured pieces do not
count extra.
