I don't think the depth of a game could be
computed by a mathematical algorithm. The crucial point
about the definition is the reference to a community of
human players.

Depth is a measure of how a human
being learns a game, which is not (anyway not yet)
algorithmically definable. I think finding a way to compute depth
would be tantamount to building a machine that is
intelligent in the same way human beings are intelligent --
one that could do EVERYTHING people can do, including
make mistakes, get distracted or tired, daydream when
it's supposed to be working, etc.

Actually I
discuss depth, and the human factor in games, at some
length in an article on my website.
