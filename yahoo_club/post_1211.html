Rule)

----------------------------
Well, let me introduce it a variation on Korneliussen's Rule (call it
whatever, like The Hutnik Corollary to the Korneliussen's Rule). It
involves a case where there is a timing mechanism used. The variation
states that the starting player removes time from either side, and
then the second player picks what side they will play. It is a pie
rule for the game clock in cases when it is unknown what side has an
advantage. The idea of the pie rule moving pieces, while appearing
interesting, ends up looking like a solution

Another possible use for Korneliussen's (or Harald's)Rule, for Chess
960 is one player picks which configuration to use, and then the
second player decides what color/side they want to be.
