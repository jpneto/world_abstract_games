I mentioned Quarti, my finger exercise in Connect-N games. Here are the
rules:

===

Quarti is an abstract game played on a standard 19x19 Go board. There is a
sufficient amount of white and black pieces for each of the 2 players.

Players in turn place 1 piece of their color on the intersections of the
lines on the board. White begins.

After the first move each player must place their piece orthogonally
connected to another piece.

Connected pieces of the same color are called a "group" (n.b.: a group isn't
necessarily a *straight* line of pieces).

It is not allowed to create a group of (exactly) 4 pieces.

To win a game of Quarti, a player must create a group of 5 (or more) pieces.

===

That's it. It works, but I don't feel totally confident about it. Maybe
there is something deep inside.
