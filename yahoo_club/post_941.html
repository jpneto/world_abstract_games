> This is to introduce a new connection game, Pentreca, which
applies the
> concept (from Caeth) of cells claimed by edge-majority, and the
concept
> (from Trellis) of resolving intersections where four cells meet by
> parity (each player "owns" half of such bridges from the start of
the
> game)to a pentagonal grid. Pentagon + Trellis + Caeth =3D Pentreca.
>
> I've put a GIF of a board in the Photos section. Two players, Red
and
> Blue, each having a pen that marks in his color, alternately color
in a
> single edge with their color. Whenever three edges in any pentagon
have
> been colored with the same color, that player also colors in the
> pentagon. Pentagons in the same color are in the same group if they
> share an edge (regardless of whether the edge they share is also
colored
> with the same color), or if they share a corner of the same color.
The
> object is to form a group that connects the edges of the board of
your
> color.
