I actually think Othello is pretty strong in both Depth and Clarity,
considering the small/simple field of play etc. Of course, in order to
attain clarity one has to understand the basic strategy of maximizing
mobility and the tactics of edge-and-corners play. To a novice who
only counts material, the game would be pretty chaotic and opaque.

Unfortunately, due to both the small board size and the clarity of the
game, computers have been unbeatable since early on. To increase
depth, perhaps we should consider a larger board with multiple initial
islands, say 13x13 with four islands or 19x19 with nine. Ouch :-)
