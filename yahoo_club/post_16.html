When people have an idea for a abstract
board game, what should she or he must worry
about?

In the last mensage about SCALUS, there were a
question about drawish strategies. A game inventor must
always worry about it, in order to avoid creating a dead
game.

What are other rules that help avoid those common
traps in game making? Some are:

* Check 1st
player advantage. If it seems to great, and there is no
elegant way to prevent it, introduce 1-move or 3-move
equalizers

* Keep drawish strategies out of the game. Usually,
capturing rules or a odd board with a center cell or
square, prevent this.

* Avoid too many draws at
endgame.

* Avoid too powerful or too weak pieces, unless
that can be balanced in other ways.

[other
ideas?]
