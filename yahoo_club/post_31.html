Thanks, Joao for the excelent suggestions! I
think I have a simple solution that will make it
possible to eliminate my restrictions and maitain certain
things the rules confer.

I wanted the early stage
of the game be mainly isolated stone framing the
game and that the opponents stay close to each other
and not abuse the move. This lead to the restrictions
that I have. Now that I know what to do they seem like
fiddly rules now.

The solution: remove all the
restrictions and change the rule on movement to read. A piece
can move exactly as many spaces as there are empty
spaces (liberties) othogonally around that piece. (A
possible variant would make it even more Go like by
stating number of spaces a piece can go is exactly the
same as number of liberties the group that the piece
belongs in have.) So by changing from mobilization to
immobilization all the problems vanish.

As for the pie
rule, I am not quite sure, but three move equalization
sound sufficient.
