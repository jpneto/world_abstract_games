Pankhurst recorded more 100 for Ethiopia, Eritrea und Somalia, but
there must be more variants played in the Horn of Africa. Townshend
gave the rules of almost 100 Mancala games played in the Democratic
Republic of Congo alone. Take another 100 in Eastern Africa und 50
each in the South and in Western Africa. Makes 400 in Africa. Mancala
is played in many parts of Asia (Mongolia, China, Kasakhstan,
Khyrgyzia, India, Burma, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Ceylon,
Maldives, Marianes and even in Iraq). 150 games is probably the lower
limit. Let's take another 50 variants in South America and the
Caribbean and we get 600 worldwide.

How many modern variants
> (modern meaning less than 100 years) do you guess there are?

I have the rules of well over 50 games and would guess there are
about a hundred games.
Several modern games have been invented in Somalia (e.g. Fergen
Gobale) and the Congo (e.g. Mangola) during the last century.
