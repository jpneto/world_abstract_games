I understand and respect your position. However, if I try to
abstract Chess, I would say that colors are irrelevant, names are
irrelevant, colored squares are irrelevant and even if mirror the
board, the game is the same. I would like to imagine that somebody
did that to Go in the distant past :-)

History is important. To better understand the game's origins and
connections to other games, what was on the inventor's mind, and so
on... But it is mathematically irrelevant.

One of my wishes is a concise specification language for abstract
games (Zillions' ZRF was a huge step but I believe it's possible to
do better) so that a game could be presented without any ambiguity,
and 'naked', ie, without historical background and visual cosmetics.
