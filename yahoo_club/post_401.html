> some of the Mensa select games have been surprisingly mediocre

Actually, it's not surprising when you consider how "Mensa Select"
games are chosen. Publishers submit games that are being offered for
the first time to Mensa, and one of the chapters around the country
hosts the "Mensa Mind Games". This is a weekend event, which any
Mensans can attend who wishes. I'm attending this year for the first
time, but as I understand it each person gets a scorecard with about
30 games on it, and is expected to evaluate every game on the
scorecard between Friday noon and Sunday morning. So you might be
able to play it once, if it's not a long game or complicated.

So, IMHO, it's not really a method calculated to produce the best
result, nor to bring honor and esteem to the Mensa brand name.
