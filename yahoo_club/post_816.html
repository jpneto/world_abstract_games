When I juxtaposed "depth" and "clarity", "drama" and "decisiveness" in
the original article that Joao mentioned, I was thinking of all four
qualities as relating to the way human beings relate to abstract
games, rather than to innate properties of the games themselves -- all
of them as psychological rather than mathematical. (Though of course
mathematics can surely be made to apply, as it can to nearly
everything.) I mean by this that if there are other intelligent
creatures in the universe whose minds work very differently than our
own, they might find some of our clearest games opaque and vice versa.
