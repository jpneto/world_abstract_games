I've been thinking about a new form of 3D Checkers, played on a
tetrahedral board like the one I used for Tetrahedral Chess (shown at

http://www.chessvariants.com/large.dir/contest84/tetrahedralchess.html

) I would use a board with only six levels, hence 56 squares: 1x6 on
levels I and VI, 2x5 on levels II and V, and 3x4 on levels III and
IV. (See the image to get a better idea of why this forms a
tetrahedron.) I would also eliminate the coloring of the squares --
probably make the board of sheets of clear plexiglas, marked with the
outlines of squares in black.

The centers of the squares of each level are exactly above and below
the corners of the squares on the adjacent levels. Therefore, each
square in the middle of the board has 4 nearest neighbors on the
levels above and below. These 8 squares, and the 4 orthogonal
neighbors on the same level, are the 12 squares adjacent to the
square. Those 12 directions are the directions in which a King can
move, for example.

I would fill the bottom and top two levels with men at the start of
the game, which would be 16 checkers each side. A man can move to any
adjacent square in the forward direction (up for Red, down for
Black), and can jump in any direction at all. Upon ending a turn on
the furthest level, a man is promoted to a king, which can move
without capturing any unobstructed distance in any of the 12
directions, or can long-jump in any of the 12 directions. Other rules
would also resemble those for International Checkers.

I'm anxious to try it out, and interested enough in the idea to build
a board, though I'm busy with other things for a few weeks. Seems to
me this would be playable. The board is comparable in size to the
International Checkers board, and though the cells have much more
potential connectivity, most of them don't use all of it -- there are
actually only 4 cells in the middle of the board that would have the
full 12 adjacent neighbors, if I'm envisioning it correctly. I think
with a physical board to inspect it wouldn't be too mind-boggling to
play. Checkers may work better on this 3D field than chess.

(I'm also considering Tetrahedral Renju, but that would take a much
bigger board...)
