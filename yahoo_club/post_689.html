Ever been to chess tournaments? Such as ones with some really high
class players? The strategy in many cases against higher rated
players is infact to force the game into a draw. A draw in fact in
tournament chess gives you a half point. So they are not
necessarily always playing for the win. It is definitely not a rule
of conduct.

I find games such as that can be boring as obviously the player
against 'Data' was concerned. Higher rated chess players that do
play to win, try to find opening lines that can yield difficult
death defying board situations that do not yield a draw. However
this more agressive play can be hazerdous. I have watched some of
these Grand Master matches, and they will declare a draw, when the
game just started. Because they realize that the game is about to
decend into boring useless play, that will finally yield a draw in
the end anyway, in ways that my simpler mind cannot fathom. Or
maybe they are satisfied with the half point.
