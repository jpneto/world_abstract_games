Do we have the rules codified somewhere, or was it just discussed in
the thread on Fischer's Random Chess?

A reason why I ask about Fischer's Random Chess (Chess960) is that it
is played in forms on tournament level, and it would be useful for
promotion purposes to have IAGO oversee it. Idea would be to end up
modifying it so it fits the IAGO standard definition better, and then
called "IAGO Standard Chess960".

Personally, I was looking at an alternative to Fischer's Random Chess
(for IAGO) anyhow, which I am calling "IAGO Standard Billion Chess"
(tell me there is a name for it already). Players alternative placing
their pieces in the back row. Castling can still happen if there is
at least one rook to the outside of the king (with similar conditions
as in regular castling of the rook and king not moving, and king not
in check or not castling through check). Castling consists of the
King moving next to the rook on the inside, and then the rook is
placed to the inside of the King. Chess plays out normally, but
layout of pieces are in complete control of players. There is no luck
in here, but there is a chance of preferable lines of play developing
(this is what Chess960 sought to counter).

So, is Thompson's Non-Random Chess codified anywhere up on the web?
