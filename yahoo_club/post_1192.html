Hmmm. Well, certainly perfect play in a game so close to chess would
pretty likely end in a draw. The notion of ranking the games according
to favorableness to Player 1 is a little iffy, since (assuming perfect
play) all possible variants would have values of either +1, 0, or -1
to player 1, and hence there would probably be something like a
700-way tie for 480th place.

But in normal, imperfect play, players would consider certain variants
as more or less favorable depending on their own expertise with them,
their perception of their opponent's expertise with them, their
perception of their opponent's perception of their own expertise with
them, etc. Hence, 480th place would in practice be even more
subjective ...
