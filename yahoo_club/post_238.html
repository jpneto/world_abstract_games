It is strange that you didn't discover that Cleave is a childish
draw. Why did you not discover it? I think, because you look into the
wrong direction. You look at the endgame, but it is far more
important to try to kill a game in the opening. It is strange that so
many people such as Chris Huntoon and Christian Freeling invent
games, which are an early draw. It basically reveals that they are
not good game inventors. On the other hand, Kris Burm would never
invent such games, which testifies his supreme brilliancy. Chris
Huntoon has good ideas, but his games are often like unpolished
diamonds. Christian Freeling lacks originality. Most of his games are
ideas taken from other games: Dameo from Croda, Bushka from
Epaminondas, Emergo from Lasca, Medusa from Go. My impression of his
web site is: it is annoying. He runs down other games such as
Draughts, Bashne and Lasca with callow thoughts, not because they are
really bad, no, because he want to promote his own games, which are
merely clones. Somehow he feels he must justify his own "inventions",
because they are so close to other well-established games. His true
motives are rather obvious.

> Point well taken. It is an embarrassing flaw.

Let's take another example:

English Draughts / Straight Checkers (is there anything like Gay
Checkers???) ---> 2 kings can win against one, except in one exotic
position which in actual games never occurs

International Draughts (10x10) ---> 3 Kings against one is usually a
draw

Guess which game is more drawish!

English Draughts suffers much more more draws in tournament play than
International Draughts, in spite of having an endgame which is far
less drawish.

Endgame analysis has to be taken with much caution.
