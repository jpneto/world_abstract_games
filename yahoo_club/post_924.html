> I am thinking of which Abstract Games which seems drawish.
> Abalone is very drawish IMHO but what about Yinsh ?

The most drawish games are the ones that perfect play leads to draws
and the best strategy is accessible (like Tic-Tac-Toe).

There are even worst cases: games where a trivial defence tactic is
able to avoid any defeat against any player - imho, these games are
broken (and they are not so rare).

Besides that, any game that, after some experience a player is able to
easily prevent a defeat against a better player, really needs some fixing.

> Many abstract board games can't end without a winner eg. hex, unlur.

The "no draws" feature is usually seen as a good characteristic.
However that does not mean that a game with no draws is automatically
good and it also does not mean that a game allowing draws is bad. For
eg, in Chess the fact that a smaller army can still go for a tie,
provides very nice matches full of drama.
