Some more thoughts about the possible 1D Curvature positions:

There are several possible live positions in this form. A line of
one color could form a solid loop or one end of a line of one color
could curve in on itself in such a manner as to not offer a proper
liberty(sorta fractal).

Therefore, it would not necessary be that there be an attempt to form
a single line but many could exist. The creation and destruction of
potential live positions would then make up the foundation of play.
Could both players place in such a way as to form nothing but live
positions? Is it possible to prevent an opponent from forming a
potential live position?

Of course, it goes without saying(but I will say it) that a player
cannot place a stone in such a way as to create an immediate capture
position for the opponent. But with the above conditions, the player
could place and leave no liberty with the formation of a loop of
their stones.

The Ko rule would be recognized.

Then there comes the possible win condition. If both player began
with a set number of stones, the player who places their last without
a possible subsequent capture by the opponent could be consider the
winner. Captured stones are returned to their owner for use during
the game. If the surface area of the playing field became filled in
such a manner as to not offer any more placements, the win could be
determined by the player with most stones in play.

Setting the number of stones would be a great area for discussion. (I
say, experiment with the standard number in a Go set.) And it is not
necessary that both player start with the same. This would allow for
a form of handicapping.

But the surface area of the playing field would be the greatest
cosideration. Needless to say, it should be at least enough to
contain all the stones of one player. Possibly both. I would use an
area of at least the square root of the total number of stones(both
players') in diameter. This would mean that not all of the stones
would be able to fit, but it should offer a nice area.
