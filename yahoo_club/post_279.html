> Matrix. Author: unknown. Publ: Knots Games, 1979.
>
GAME ONE
Object:
To be the first to form a line of four differently colored chips. A
line consists of four spaces in either horizontal, vertical, or
diagonal directions. [The board is 4 x 4.]

To start:
Each player chooses 6 chips, three each of two colors. [The game
comes with 4 each of red, green, yellow, and blue disks.]

The play:
The players alternate in turn, placing one chip at a time on the
board.

Rules:
1. A chip cannot be placed adjacent to a chip of the same color.

2. Players cannot conceal their chips "in hand".

3. If a player cannot place a chip, he passes.

4. Players are in a tie if no one player has managed to form a line
of four different colors.

Winner:
The first player to complete a line of four differently colored chips.

Strategy:
Check your opoonent's remaining pieces to figure your moves. [Duh!]

GAME TWO
This game is the same as Game One with two exceptions:

1. Each player now chooses 8 chips each of two colors.

2. Chips of the same color CAN be placed in adjacent spaces.
