I think beauty is strongly linked with surprise. Think of meeting a
beautiful woman or tasting an exotic fruit you did not know before.
Beauty you could not expect.

So, first of all, regarding games, beautiful moves are only possible in
games which are complex enough to give room for surprise. The 'hello
world' game Tic-tac-toe is far too simple for this. Secondly, a
beautiful moves don't necessarily lead to immediate victory. They will
improve a player's position on the board and they do this in a very
unusual way. But elegance and beauty are not quantifiable nor
comparable. They are unique events, I think you can't expect an exact
definition.

The next question could be: Are beautiful moves possible in inelegant
games? A beautiful move in ... (no, I deny myself an example :)
