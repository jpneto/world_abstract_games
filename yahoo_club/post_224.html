My comment on one of the mistakes in "New Rules for Classic Games" (R.
W. Schmittberger,1992):

>Page 24-25:
>"Indeed, it can be demonstrated mathematically that in games with
>unequal forces and unequal objectives, it is impossible to create a
>perfect balance between the players, unless a draw is the correct
>outcome."

One consequence/formulation of the Curie Symmetry Principle is that:
The effect is at least as symmetric as the cause.

Ie. unequal forces can lead to a perfect balance.

For example, if you heat a circular coin with a flame from one side
(the cause - with one line of symmetry), the coin expands equally in
all directions (the effect - with full circular symmetry).

>My comment:

>It would be good to correct Schmittberger's exaggerated reputation he
>has as a game expert. He makes lots of mistakes.

Some people hate the word "expert". Experts have generally spent most
of their lives arguing themselves into a highly rigid position; who
would admit themselves to be wrong at the cost of their life's work?

And finally, please read Games People Play, by Eric Berne, and Finite
and Infinte Games, by James P Carse, for some different perspectives on
playing games.
