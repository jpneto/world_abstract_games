I made no attack on any particular individual's motivations, I merely
stated an observation. Only those who are guilty of such motivation
would find direct fault with such.

The statement that began this discussion was that cards were not
allowed in abstract games. Hopefully this false premise has been
laid to rest.

TruthForum's original premise was that abstract games could never
contain random generators. I gave several examples of accepted
abstract games which use random generators. (i.e. Mozaic)

Then TruthForum began to advocate that abstract games conformed to
the Rules of Games of No Chance. This would be a very narrow
interpretation of the abstract game. Thus the appeal to consider the
long-term effect of such restrictions.

Games of the Abstract are played on fields of various sizes and
shapes, with objects of various shapes and colors, between any number
of opponents, governed by rules from simplistic to complex. An
abstract game can consist of only pencil and paper. Random
generators are acceptable.

To say that nothing psychological may enter into an abstract game
shows limited experience of direct play between individuals. All
games contain psychological aspects, just note its use in Chess. Try
playing a game of GLE'X and not be overwhelmed.

In this Group, we primarily are concerned with abstract board games.
This category alone denotes that there are other abstract games which
are not played upon an established field.

Can there be abstract card games? Yes! I know of a really cool one,
it is called Zarcana.
