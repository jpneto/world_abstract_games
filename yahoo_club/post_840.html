Just half an hour ago, the mailman gave me "Stein um Stein: Exotik
der Brettspiele" ("Stone per Stone: Exotic World of Board Games"), a
book published in 1984 in the German Democratic Republic and written
by Heinz Machatscheck. It is totally different from his other better
known book called "Zug um Zug: Die Zauberwelt der Brettspiele" ("Move
per Move: The fascinating World of Board Games"). As I grew up in the
American satellite state of Western Germany, I wasn't much exposed to
socialist culture and the achievements of the glorious heroes of the
Soviet Union ;-).

Well, I'm serious now, this book is truly fantastic! First reason, I
got it for less than 4 Euro, including postage, from a second hand
book store close to the German-Polish demarcation line.

Second reason, the book covers lesser known board games, especially
games which are lesser known in non-rogue states.

The first chapters are about Morris and Draughts variants which are
also known in western Europe.

The "Turmspiel von Hanoi" ("Tower Dame of Hanoi"; not to be confused
with the game "Tower of Hanoi" invented by =C9douard Lucas) is a
stacking game that is played on an international Draughts board. I
haven't seen the game before. The book also gives a sample game.

This is followed by a very good chapter on Mancala games. Besides
many other variants it describes Togus kumalak, probably the best
description outside of Central Asia. The game was revived in 1948
when the Soviet of Kasakhstan organized a tournament. Machatscheck
gives a game between the masters A. Akshurajew and N. Nurlybajew with
many helpful comments.

The next chapter is about Bashni, the old Russian column checkers. It
includes a game played by W. Wiskowitow in Moscow in 1875, again with
very good comments.

The last chapters are not so interesting, but include several games
played by Native Americans before they were exterminated by American
and Spanish "imperialists".
