The combination of different well-known game mechanisms are characteristic
for Kris Burm's Project Gipf games.

>And this is all tied together
>with a score - three max - which can very easily result in a 0-0, 1-1, or

The goal is to win three out of five rings. The opponent will then have two
rings. Although the rules state a special case in which a draw could occur
("If all the markers are placed on the board before a player has won, then
the player who removed the most rings wins. If both players have removed the
same number of rings, the game ends in a draw.") -- in fact, this hardly
ever happens in real play.
