I don't understand what that means. Contrived means "to plan with
ingenuity; devise; invent: The author contrived a clever plot." That
seems like a good thing. What's an illogical objective? I'm not
saying I disagree with you necessarily. I'm just not real clear on
what you're saying.

My criticism of Versus is it just seems too complicated, like a movie
with too many plots. Pick one and run with it. It has nice "art"
with interesting playing pieces, but the rules seem arbitrary and
uninteresting to me. That's an illness, to use your expression, in
games. Making something that's interesting to look at, something
that's artistic in a static sense, with an interesting board or tiles
or playing pieces, etc., pieces that fit together in a clever way, but
the rules of the game seems uninspired.

> Why, you must ask yourself, should being the first into these
squares bring victory when something else would be just as good.

What else, for example, would be just as good?

> A nice mechanic though!

I'm not thrilled with the mechanic. Every time you move a "versus"
you attract all the pieces around it closer to it, which reduces the
mobility of the versus to the point that it can barely move. I
haven't played it either but that's my initial impression without
having played.

It also seems like it would be drawish by pulling pawns back and forth
between versuses (versi?). The author attempts to resolve that with:

"...in case of repeated attractions of the 2 ``VERSUS'' on the same
pawn, the player who does not hold the dominating colour must give way
after two repeated attractions, but you may come back later after
having played elsewhere. Should the move consecutive to the 2
attractions entail three new attractions, among which the pawn
involved in the two preceding attractions, this move will be possible
but will not trigger a third attraction of the pawn involved in the
two preceding ones."

but that just seems to add more complication to an already overly
complicated game.
