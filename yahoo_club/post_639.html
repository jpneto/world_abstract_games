I submitted the following connection game, which has a winning
condition similar to that of Hex:

"Dipole" by Cameron Browne

Dipole is a 3D connection game for two players, Black and White.

The game is played on an 8x8 square grid of holes. The top and
bottom sides of the board are coloured black, and the left and right
sides are coloured white.

Players share a common pool of 32 dipoles, where each dipole
consists of a black and white sphere joined together.

Aim: A player wins by completing a connected chain of their colour
between their two sides of the board. Two spheres are connected if
they are orthogonally (squarely) adjacent or one rest directly upon
the other.

Play: The board is initially empty. Players then take turns either
1) placing a dipole on the board, or 2) swiveling a dipole as
follows.

1) A dipole can be placed anywhere that it sits stably, and may be
flat (with both spheres on adjacent empty points on the same level)
or tilted (with one sphere on an empty point on one level and the
other sphere resting at an angle on two or three surrounding pieces).

2) A dipole already on the board may be swiveled around the end with
the opponent's colour (that is, the end with the opponent's colour
stays fixed) as long as it sits stably after the move.

Over/under rule: If a connection of one colour passes over a
connection of the other colour, then the lower connection is cut.

Making your own set: The board may be easily made with an 8x8 grid
of washers, where each washer is the same diameter as the dipole
spheres. Dipoles can be made by gluing together two smalls balls or
joining pairs of beads with small pegs.
