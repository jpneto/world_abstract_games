I postulate that any game should have a vertical depth of at least 40
moves to be a seroius challenge for homo sapiens ("the wise man").

Take Zertz. The game lasts usually not longer than 30 moves. Michael
Reitz, a friend living just a few miles away, analyzed the opening
until he reached the endgame and basically solved the game for the
original board. The enlarged board is more difficult to analyze but
will be probably solved too -- as the new game takes not much longer
than the original one. Zertz is a poor game at least for those who
are seeking a never ending challenge. It's fun to play but not very
deep at least from a professional view.

I postulate that most games which are shorter than 40 moves can be
analyzed so much that the middle game (the last stronghold that
cannot be solved by the human mind) can be bridged by the opening and
the endgame.
