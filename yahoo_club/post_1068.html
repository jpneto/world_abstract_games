I understand what you're saying. In Levee the goal is to rid the
board of all sequences of unoccupied points connecting the center to
either of his sides. But to do so you have to connect stones.

Here are the differences though. Hex is a two color game while Levee
is a one color game. Hex can't be played on a rectangular grid
pattern because the black lines and white lines could cross each
other. In Hex you must connect your two opposite sides with at least
one line, while in Levee you either need at least two lines connecting
your opposite sides, with the center sandwiched in between, or, as a
completely different goal, you could form a U around the center,
connecting both ends to one of your sides. In my opinion all of this
makes Levee very different from Hex. Sure, you could call it a twist
on Hex, but that's a big fuckin twist.
