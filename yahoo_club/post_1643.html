Hi abstract game folks,

I am a would-be game designer with a lofty goal: I want to make a game that
1) is so deep and worthy of study that it will be played for centuries to
come (like Chess & Go) and 2) is aesthetically pleasing.

I seem to have more talent with the second part of my goal than the first.
Sadly, that's less important. I have made several prototypes and gotten
feedback (bggcon
proto-alleywas
super-helpful),
but I've not yet come up with anything worthwhile.

Lately, I've been trying to come up with a good answer for this question:
"Why is Chess worthy of study?" Feel free to replace Chess with your
favorite abstract strategy game. What I really want to find are some
eloquent, simple answers. Perhaps they will help give me something to aim
for with my own game designs. Are any of you willing to take a stab at
answering that question?

Or if you know of eloquent answers online or in books, please share those.
Perhaps an easier way to answer the question is to try and list some
characteristics of games that are worthy of study. That answers more the
what question, but perhaps it will lead to answers about the why question...

Thanks!
Simon Dorfman
