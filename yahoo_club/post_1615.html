I guess wrapped up in all this is: our ability to observe and comprehend.

I can't read sheet music. But I have a friend who can read music and can
"hear" it as he reads it.

Similarly, I can imagine someone who makes church bells is familiar enough
with them that he can "hear" the sound one might make before striking it.

I can read the rules to a game and understand them pretty well just by
reading them because of my experience reading many different rules and
playing many different games. I can imagine the game and "play" it (in my
head) to get a feel for it. Even so, I like to really play the game against
someone else to really learn something about it.

Simon Dorfman
