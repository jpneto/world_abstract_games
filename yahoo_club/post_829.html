Most stacking games appear to be quite good, but some are very weak:

Ombagi

This is a Halma variant played with stacks. It was first published in
1981. The game was designed by Reinhold Wittig, a famous German game
inventor. He did a poor job. The same must be said of Andreas Keirat
the best known game tester in Germany who writes that Ombagi is quite a
good. This nonsense is typical for Keirat, and can be found whenever he
describes abstract strategy games. Ombagi has the same flaw as Halma:
just keep a few stones in your homebase, and your opponent will never
reach his goal. I'm a little bit tired of safing flawed games.
http://sunsite.informatik.rwth-aachen.de/keirat/txt/O/Ombagi.html
http://sunsite.informatik.rwth-aachen.de/keirat/txt/F/Fanorona.html
