The Way Of Go (TWOG)

Equipment:
Board:
N by N (perhaps 13 by 13) (toidal?)
Pieces:
(N^2)/2 for each player (more than
enough)
white/black.

Object: To capture enough opponent pieces to fulfill your
contract.
Contract: Contract is determined by your skill level When
you consistently win, your contract is increased by
one stone. And vice versa. A beginner's contract is
one stone.

Play: The players' turn alternate.

Pie rule: The first player places the first three
stones, which would be two white stones and a black
stone. After that the second player chooses whether to
play black or white. After that, it is the black
stone's player's turn and turn alternate
hereafter.

Turn: A turn consists of an optional move and
a
required placement.

move: A piece can move exactly
as far as
the number of liberties the group

that the piece belongs to have.

capture: If
your piece lands on an
opponent's piece after
a
move, the opponent's piece
is
captured.

placement: Pieces can be placed on any space
that is
empty.

Commentary:

As you all probably
noticed, it is nearly impossible to change the range of a
piece to anything below two, except for very well
constructed traps which forces a piece to move into a trap
that instantly takes away all its
liberties.

Making the board toidal would remove any edge
effect.

An 8x8 game would be very tactical and short, though
still very interesting. The game will unlikely to be
longer than 20 moves for 1 stone contracts. In any case,
I would definitely like to submit it for the
contest at abstract game magazine, but unfortunately I
have not subscribed it yet, so if you be so kind to
send me some instructions on how to submit the game
for the contest. It will be very
appreciated.

I'll try to implement these rules in ZRF ASAP and test
somethings out. Though zillions might not play it too
well.
