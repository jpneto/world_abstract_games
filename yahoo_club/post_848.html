>This is a simultaneous-play game.

A common misconception! Take a very good clock and you'll see that the moves
are not simultaneous. What happens is that a player can do several moves in
a row if the other player is a little bit slow.

I would call Ice Towers "involuntarily" turn-based ("passing" is due to slow
thinking, lesser manual dexterity, and intention (= voluntary passing)) .
You could also play Ice Towers voluntarily turn-based (i.e. combined with
the possibility of passing moves only voluntarily).

>I suppose it could be played as a
>turn-based game, but I'm not sure how well it would work.

According to those who tried it, the game becomes a brain-burner.

These are not true stacking games! Physically they appear like stacks, but
topologically they aren't. These are games where you just have multiple
pieces on one position.
