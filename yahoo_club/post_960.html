Copolymer - Ultra Simple Game

Author: Mark Steere

Date: 2006-04-09 22:07:18

Post ID: 960

Copolymer is an ultra simple game I came up with on April 5, 2006.
I've been play testing it for the past few days and it seems to be a
valid and most interesting game at this point.

COPOLYMER RULES: Start with a honeycomb patterned board with an odd
number of cells, such as any size regular hexagonal board. Each of
two players has a unique color felt pen or a unique color set of
tokens. Players take turns adding their tokens to the board, at
least one token per turn. Players are not allowed to pass on their
turn. Define "connection" here as the placement of one of your
tokens adjacent to two *opposing* tokens (that is, adjacent to each
of two or more of *your opponent's* tokens). Having done so, you
must add at least one more token to the board while it is still your
turn. Your turn concludes when you add a non-connecting token. You
are never required to form a connection, unless the only available
cells are connecting cells for you. Once the board fills up, the
player who has claimed the majority of cells wins.

NOTES: There is a tendency in beginner play to maximize one's token
count on every turn, but it's usually best not to do that. For
example, suppose on one of your turns you can claim a maximum of
five cells, and that doing so would enable your opponent to claim
seven cells. Alternatively, if you only claim three cells (less
than your maximum possible), enabling your opponent to claim only
two, such a play might be more advantageous.

The influence of Dots and Boxes on Copolymer is apparent, but
Copolymer is different enough that personally I wouldn't call it a
variant. The central, defining characteristics of Dots and Boxes
are the drawing of connections and enclosures, the claiming of said
enclosures, and multiple move turns. In Copolymer, the connections
and enclosures already exist, and the claiming of a cell doesn't
always trigger the addition of more tokens in the same turn.
