> What about Trax ?

I'm a poor Trax player although I win most games. I don't understand
why I win so many games so perhaps Trax isn't very clear to me.
However, I must have a good intuition about what constitutes a good
move. Perhaps clarity is as much psychological as mathematical. Only
one board position changes per move, the winning condition is very
simple, but the notation very complicated. Perhaps the latter points to
some "anti-clarity" (hey, native speakers: what's the opposite of
clarity?).

Really, intuition is another nice concept in playing a game well. It's
very important in Go, Blokus (I am FiveStars at blokus.com)and probably
in any game which is not very "tactical"!
