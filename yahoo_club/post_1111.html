Yang-Te; The Tai Chi Game=99

=A9 Colin Leamon 21/09/07. Shinermons Games.

For two players, Yin & Yang/ Black & White, with sixty pieces each
(called????) the object of the game is to occupy/ win/ hold the most
cells in a majority of the four boards. Taking turns, with Black
going first, a players go is to lay one of his pieces on a board, if
he is able, per go. If, after The Set Up, that player makes
a `Harmony' in a cell he lays one of his pieces in that cell of that
board.
=95 The moment a player runs out of pieces (even though pieces
maybe returned to him at the end of his go, or after his opponents
go) he must `knock-knock' on the table; his opponent must then take
one more go, if he is able to & the game is then over. The boards
are then scored separately & then, if you wish, the number of board
won & the scores are totalled to give a `Match Score'.
=95 Players can lay anywhere (see `Removals') & do not have to
make Harmonys with their lays; just as long as the Points/ Meridian
balance is kept throughout.
=95 Players can only "Pass" if they can not lay. If they can lay
they must lay.

The game board itself is made up of Seasons & `Winds'. Each board is
made up of a grid of cells. The cells themselves are made up
of `Points', `Meridians', & a `Virtue'.

The very middle of the board is called `The Tai Chi'
