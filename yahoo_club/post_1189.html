--- In abstractgames@yahoogroups.com, "Rich Hutnik" wrote:
>> 2a. Definition of chance has to do with any element not controlled by
> players in the game. Chance elements here besides the normal and
> expected include randomized layout of player pieces or even randomly
> generated asymmetric layout of board spaces. The only exception to
> chance would be determining play order, provided both sides are
> balanced and have near equal chance of winning. In cases where this is
> not the case, the randomly determing play order is not accepted as a
> random element in the game.
How do you determine if "both sides are balanced, and have near equal
chance of winning?" The "near equal chance of winning" part could be
determined by expected outcome (play a large number of random games
(how many?), and only accept an expected outcome in a certain interval
(how liberal?), but this wouldn't always necessarily give a
"reasonable" result (if there's only a very narrow set of moves
leading to victory, but they're obvious to human players, for instance).
