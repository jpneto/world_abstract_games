I think we are talking past one another. I was referring to the
possibility that certain rule changes might remedy the drawish nature
of custodian-capture games, a category in which I include Phalanx. I
have in mind the various attempts to reconstruct Latrunculi. If you
take the "pure" approach without a King/Eagle and allow only
traditional custodian capture -- no use of board edges, no line
captures, etc. -- the game is not winnable. In this case it's an
endgame problem. Cleave interests me because it mixes intervention
and edge custody with regular custodian capture in a way that
addresses the problem.

>Why did you not discover it? I think, because you look into the
> wrong direction. You look at the endgame, but it is far more
> important to try to kill a game in the opening.


That's true. I find the problem you are focused on to be superficial
in many cases. If Phalanx or Cleave can be salvaged by a different
initial array and otherwise play well, I see no reason to dismiss
them. If, on the other hand, they degenerate into stalemate
regardless of starting positions because capture is too difficult,
then fixing them becomes more difficult.
