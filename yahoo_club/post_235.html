I haven't played the game but I think the problem you are identifying
is related to the custodian-capture nature of the game. Most
reconstructions I've seen of Latrunculi/Petteia appear to be easy
draws. As the board thins out you can protect your remaining pieces
by clumping them together. Captures require two moves and can be
spotted -- and defended -- fairly easily. I wonder what types of
modifications might salvage these games. Perhaps two moves per turn,
capture by interposition, or allowing the board edge to count as a
custodian would help. Some of these ideas are incorporated into
Cleave (by Chris Huntoon), which is available at ZoG and appears
reasonably non-drawish.

Has anybody ever heard Epaminondas referred to as Phalanx? If I
recall correctly Parlett says this.

I had the problem of "indestructible forts" in the first version I
did of Tunneler (posted to ZoG a few weeks ago). If you invert the
Foremen and the Tunnelers on the first rank, which is how I initially
conceived the game, you can dig out the bedrock in such a way that an
impenetrable wall is built around the Foremen. This is pretty much
the same problem you identified in Phalanx. I think I corrected it
in the release version, but then again ...
