During some play-testing, an interesting form of capture was
discovered. Allowing that stones may be played at any angle with
only two possible liberties for each, if a player places two stones
adjacent(by their lonesomes) then an opponent can remove their
liberties by playing so that their stone contacts both.

This also creates the potential of another live position involving
only three stones.

Another rule: Stones must either be place directly adjacent another
or at a distance greater than the width of a stone.

This was to reduce arguments about whether a series of stones is
connected or not. It makes such quite obvious. And with the ability
to place stones at any angle, the various possible distances create
an interesting dynamic in the play.

Back to play-testing. We're trying to see if gaining control of the
perimeter can give an advantage.
