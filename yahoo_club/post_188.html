The rules of 'Games of No Chance' cannot be arbitrarily applied to
all abstract games. It can best be stated that this
represents one branch of the tree.

Although abstract games can be themeless, this does not mean that
games with themes are excluded. If that were the case, the games
would not be given decorative titles. Trax might be
called 'Infinite Selective Line/Loop Development'. Of course, this
is not easy on the ears. A theme allows the players to easily
communicate the concept of the game.

Whether the pieces are called tokens, men or stones is also the
prerogative of the developer. If there are more than one type of
piece in the game, it often becomes necessary to give them
distinctive names. This also applies to areas of the playing field.

There are many ancient games which utilize themes.

Why must abstract games have only two players? I see no
justification for this assertion. I know of several which use more
than two. Chinese checkers comes to mind, or is that game not
included in the list?

So, I say to TruthForum, "Lift up your eyes and see the infinite
depth that is Abstract Games. Enjoy the various flavors of the
banquet."
