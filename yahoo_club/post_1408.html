One aspect to this "solved" bit is that the lines of play get fully
exposed and analyzed, leading to innovation happening later and later
in the game, and further into the midgame. This eventually pushes
into the end game, and all of the game is exposed. This makes for a
game that is less exciting for people trying to follow it, or even
play it. Innovators feel stiffled.

What then happens here is that those playing a particular game wonder
what they should do next. My take is that perhaps thought can be
given in the direction this should take, before it happens.
