Pylos is the castrated version of Strat-o-Sphere. I know the inventor (David
G. Royffe) from the Gamer's Fair in Essen He is a nice guy. Pylos is meant
for people with no or very few intellectual capacities (this is basically
what Gigamic told me in an interview) who are glad when a game is over as if
it were something disgusting like a school lesson or torture. The original
game is played on a much larger board and has an interesting point-scoring.

>Mark Steere writes:
> > Does anyone know if draws can occur in Pylos? Only one ball can claim
> > the top spot, but what if nobody reaches the top?

According to Dave's rules (not the spoiled ones by Gigamic), the player who
could move last is declared the winner.

>it appears that you can place a marble that forms a square and then
>immediately remove the same marble, i.e. pass. Even if you disallow
>this, you might form a square and remove a marble from a second
>square, and then re-form the second square and remove a marble from the
>first square, etc. So it does seem that a trivial cycle is possible.

This wouldn't happen, as your opponent would block the square by putting a
stone on top of it. As you need four stones to make a square, but your
opponent only one stone to stop it, this strategy wouldn't work. BTW, in the
original rules, the last player to move wins (whether this is a circle or
not).
