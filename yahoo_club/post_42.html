Jianying, "The Way" alone was quite too arrogant
a name anyway,
if you don't mind my frankness.
"The Way of Go" sounds poetic,
modest and very open
to interpretation. I can hardly imagine
any
better name for your game. Maybe something more exactly
connected
to nice things occuring during the game could also
do, for
example about the concept of leverage, but
I don't find any nice
word for it.

But
look, there is something funny. What happens if we turn
the
game into a connecting game instead of a
capture-the-most game?
There would be a starting position with
many stones already on
the board, unconnected, on
opposite edges for example, and we
would not be allowed
to put new stones onto the board during the
game.
The moving and capturing rules would remain. The game
would
roughly be about moving your stones toward each others
and
gradually get them all connected. Now, the trouble is that
as
soon as you've connected n of your stones on an n*n
board, they
cannot move, since they would jump out of
the board. But the goal
is to get all your stones
in such a connection. Therefore the
game would
easily get into drawing or very cramped
positions
where neither player can go on. Now, I don't think it
was
invented through the same process, but there is a game
that can
be seen as a nice solution - define the
distance of move to be
smaller, namely you jump as far
as there are stones on the *line*
you are
jumping. And do you know how that game is called? A
sort
of synonym of the name of your game - "Lines of
Action".

It's another argument to keep "The Way of
Go".

Claude Chaunier
