"Why do we design?"

Don't know how good this answer is, but I design because I have to.
I enjoy games; started playing chess when I was 10 and wargames at 12.
Within a few years, I was "fixing" some of the things I didn't like in
my games. After I got out of school, I played wargames almost
exclusively because I lacked opponents and most AH/SPI-style wargames
were able to be played solitaire. Fiddled with the rules occasionally;
sometimes combined components from various games to make a new one,
and designed a few games from scratch. A couple of them were decent,
and I thought about trying to sell them, but didn't see how I could
compete with established companies. One game I worked on, off and on
for decades, was a 4D chess variant, without any knowledge that CVs
existed. After I retired, my son got me online, and I started looking
around for games. I found the ChessVariants site, and got hooked on
designing variants - it's cheap entertainment! ;-)
I design games I enjoy playing. Most are now chessvariants, at least
technically, but some aren't, and a few I think have commercial
potential. So I'm working on selling them, but not all that hard. If I
see a way to have what I think is a reasonable chance at success, I
will invest a bit and make a serious effort to sell some of my
designs. But the basic truth is that I design because I enjoy it, and
the ideas, when I have them, force themselves out of my brain, down my
arm, and onto a piece of paper.
