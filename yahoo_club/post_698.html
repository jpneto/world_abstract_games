Just to add to the discussion...

IMHO, I think another problem, very close in nature, is forcing an end
to a game.

Drawing is just when neither player can win. That's okay if, as
mentioned previously, it takes some skill (or luck, in my case) to get
to that point. What's worse is if a player can indefinetly postpone
the ending of the game (i.e. a winning player can never force a win or
a draw).

It bothers my sense of asthetics when I play a game and I realize that
I could postpone the conclusion indefinetly. Making rules to limit
the number of times a move can be iteratied can help, but that strikes
me as a somewhat inelegant solution. Connection games like Hex and
Unlur has really elegant solutions to the problem that are more to my
liking.

Anyway, just my opinion...
